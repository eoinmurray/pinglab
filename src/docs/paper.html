1. Introduction

Surrogate-gradient-trained SNNs [1] are typically simulated as discrete-time recurrent networks whose state evolves in fixed-size

steps. The choice of

is conventionally tied to the dataset encoding time or to hardware constraints, not to the dynamics being simulated. As a result, a network trained at one

is not automatically transferable to another — accuracy can drop by tens of percentage points when the inference

differs from the training

by an order of magnitude.

Published intuition frames this as a model-class issue: current-based models (e.g. snnTorch’s snn.Leaky) are described as

-sensitive, while conductance-based or otherwise biophysical models are said to be more robust. But CUBA and COBA differ along several axes at once: synaptic dynamics, reset policy, refractory periods, voltage update form. This paper asks which of those axes actually carries the

-stability claim.

Approach. We build a five-model ladder (Table 1) where each step adds one biologically-motivated feature over the last. Two further intermediate models fill in the cuba → coba gap cleanly. All models share a task, training protocol, readout, and number of trainable parameters, so differences in

-stability can be attributed to the features that distinguish them.

Finding. The load-bearing feature is the exponential synapse. It is necessary — removing it from coba (which gives us cuba) recovers the catastrophic

-sensitivity. It is sufficient — adding it to cuba (which gives us cuba-exp) recovers full

-stability, matching coba up to small baseline differences.

Shared controls. 5.1. Mechanism

The forward-dynamics argument is a statement about per-step variance in the membrane drive, not about per-step mean. All five models in the ladder have

The exp synapse decouples the integration step size seen by the membrane from the per-step spike-count variance. This is the structural reason it delivers

-stability.

5.2. Comparison to prior framings

Published “dt-sensitivity” results for CUBA-style networks typically use snnTorch-library semantics:

as a dimensionless hyperparameter, no explicit

in the model, unscaled spike drive and bias. Our snntorch-canonical matches this, and it does fail the

-sweep — consistent with prior work. Our cuba is the proper Euler discretisation, which partially fixes the sensitivity (handles the mean correctly but not the variance). The full fix is cuba-exp, which matches the biophysical models’ stability without adopting their membrane equation.

The implication is that claims of the form “biophysical models are more

-robust“ are correct empirically but mechanistically imprecise. The robustness comes from a single feature — the exponential synapse — that is part of most biophysical models but is not exclusive to them and does not require any other biophysical machinery to deliver

-invariance.

5.3. Limitations

All results are from single-seed runs. The calibration accuracies across the ladder cluster within a 7-pp band (85 “–” 92.5 %) and the qualitative

-sweep shape is consistent with theoretical prediction across every model, so we have high confidence that the feature-attribution conclusion is stable. But the small differences between adjacent ablation rows (e.g. the

pp from hard reset) would need a multi-seed replication to quantify precisely.

The task is MNIST with Poisson rate coding. A harder task — e.g. sequential MNIST, which requires temporal integration — would likely stress the ladder differently. We expect the central claim to hold: any model with a

-independent input filter should remain

-robust.

The network width is fixed at 1024 hidden units. The forward-dynamics argument does not depend on width, but the classification accuracies may.

5.4. Practical implication

For practitioners training SNN classifiers with surrogate-gradient BPTT:

if you need robustness to deployment-time

differences, adding an exponential synapse to your CUBA LIF layer is sufficient.

The code change is two extra state variables per hidden neuron (input-synapse conductance, its decay factor) and one extra exponential. No change to the membrane equation, no change to the reset policy, no refractory machinery. The full biophysical stack gives 3 “–” 5 pp of baseline accuracy on MNIST, but zero extra

-stability.

We group proposed follow-ups by the weakness they address. Every item in §7.1 is a direct robustness check for a specific claim made in §5; §7.2 extends the empirical test surface; §7.3 pushes toward mechanism. §7.4 ranks the items by priority.

This is a real limitation and has two plausible interpretations:

6.2. Extending the empirical test surface

Harder tasks. MNIST with Poisson rate coding is an easy substrate; it mostly serves as a calibration signal. The

-stability claim should be tested on tasks where temporal structure in the input matters: sequential MNIST (digit pixels streamed row-by-row), N-MNIST and DVS-Gesture (native event-camera recordings with real spike timing), and the Spiking Heidelberg Digits dataset (speech with known dt-sensitive structure). Expected result: cuba-exp holds up; cuba breaks harder; the gap widens. A negative result — cuba-exp loses stability on harder tasks — would reveal that the variance-smoothing argument is incomplete in regimes where fine spike timing carries classification signal.

Deeper and recurrent architectures. All ladder models here are single-hidden-layer feedforward. Real SNNs are often multi-layer with recurrent connections. Two questions: (a) does the exp synapse requirement extend to every synaptic path in a deep network, or is a first-layer filter sufficient? (b) if recurrence is present, does the recurrent drive

also need to be filtered, or can it pass as per-step kicks without damaging stability? Our SNNTorchNet.forward supports multi-layer and recurrent configurations already; the experiments are a few training runs away.

Width scaling. Network width

is fixed throughout. Per-step variance arguments are width-dependent: wider networks should have smaller relative fluctuations in hidden firing rates. Testing the ladder at

would test whether the stability ordering is width-invariant (the architectural claim) or whether wider networks mask the instability (a regularisation-like claim).

6.3. Deepening the mechanism

Rigorous proof. The argument in §6.1 is informal: it derives per-step variance in closed form for a uniform Poisson input and argues that stability follows. A more rigorous proof would (a) make the Bernoulli-to-Gaussian approximation explicit with an error term, (b) handle non-stationary inputs (MNIST pixels have different intensities, so per-neuron

varies), (c) quantify how the exp synapse bounds the probability that membrane overshoot exceeds threshold per step. A clean theorem along the lines of

“for any LIF with exponential synapse and

would be the formal companion to our empirical result.

The 92.5% anomaly. cuba-exp-hard-refrac trained at

ms reaches 92.5% — the highest test accuracy we observed in any configuration, higher than coba’s 90.5%. This is either single-seed noise or a real regularisation effect from the hard reset and refractory period combination. If the 5-seed replication confirms the effect, the mechanism is worth isolating: does refractory-induced sparsity act as dropout? Does hard reset prevent over-integration during burst responses? Both are testable by looking at hidden-layer representations across training epochs.

6.4. Priority ordering

If we had to pick a minimal follow-up that would strengthen the paper most, it is the combination of §7.1.1 (seed replication) and §7.2.1 (harder tasks). Seed replication fixes the precision of the feature-attribution numbers; harder tasks test whether the claim holds under meaningful temporal structure. Together they would lift the paper from “empirical observation on MNIST” to “architectural design guideline.”

7. Conclusion

Architectural

-stability in surrogate-gradient SNNs is not a property of CUBA vs COBA — it is a property of whether the input stream reaches the membrane through a low-pass filter with a

-independent time constant. The exponential synapse, with

ms, is that filter. It is necessary (removing it, as in cuba, breaks stability) and sufficient (adding it, as in cuba-exp, restores stability to the level of the fully biophysical coba).
