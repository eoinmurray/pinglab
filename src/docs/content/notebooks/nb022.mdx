## Abstract

The single place this collection trains its canonical networks. Every cell is trained once here, to a shared artifact root, and the analysis notebooks load those weights instead of retraining their own — a train-once / reuse-many policy that replaces the older "standalone runner" rule (see [ar016](/articles/ar016/)). Status: **draft** — the registry and standard are fixed, but the cells await the canonical run: the full-MNIST reference is new, and the folded sweep cells on disk predate the gamma standard ($\tau_\text{GABA} = 6$ ms), so they will be retrained at it.

## Standard

Each family fixes its synaptic time constants, a timestep, a training length, and how much of MNIST it sees (of the 70,000-image pool — 60k train + 10k test, sampled then re-split 80/20). The standard $\tau_\text{GABA} = 6$ ms puts the loop in gamma (≈ 44 Hz on the task); the τ_GABA ladder is the family that varies it:

| Family | $\tau_\text{AMPA}$ (ms) | $\tau_\text{GABA}$ (ms) | $\Delta t$ (ms) | Epochs | MNIST used |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| canonical (θ_u off) | 2 | 6 | 0.1 | 100 | 70000 (all) |
| θ_u sweep | 2 | 6 | 0.1 | 100 | 7000 (10%) |
| τ_GABA ladder | 2 | 4.5 – 27 | 0.1 | 100 | 7000 (10%) |
| Δt sweep | 2 | 6 | 0.05 – 1.0 | 100 | 7000 (10%) |
| init variants | 2 | 6 | 0.1 | 100 | 7000 (10%) |

The **canonical** family is the full-MNIST reference (coba and ping at θ_u = off) the 10%-trained sweeps are read against. $\tau_\text{AMPA}$ is fixed at 2 ms throughout. [nb044](/notebooks/nb044/)'s Δt audit is the exception that varies the timestep; the τ_GABA ladder the one that varies the inhibitory decay.
