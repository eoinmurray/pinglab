## Abstract

Trains PING and COBA from scratch for 100 epochs (three seeds each) and asks whether the firing rate converges once the accuracy has. It does not — not for COBA. Test accuracy plateaus by ≈15 epochs in both architectures, but PING's E rate locks to a tight ≈ 9 Hz attractor (cross-seed to 0.1 Hz) while COBA's keeps climbing through epoch 100. Accuracy is a point; for COBA, the rate is a manifold the optimiser drifts along at constant accuracy.

## Method

Reads the shared θ_u = off baselines (coba and ping, three seeds each) from the training hub — [nb022 (Training)](/notebooks/nb022/), which fixes the recipe (100 epochs, mem-mean readout, no rate regulariser) — and plots their per-epoch training history. The question, following [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) / [nb044](/notebooks/nb044/): once test accuracy has plateaued, does the firing rate also settle? **Converged** means a last-10-epoch slope below 0.1 pp/ep (accuracy) or 0.05 Hz/ep (rate).

<Figure id="Figure 2" title="PING — loss, accuracy, firing rate vs epoch" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ping_curves.png" alt="Three panels for PING (three seeds overlaid): train and test loss vs epoch; test accuracy vs epoch; and E (solid) and I (dashed) firing rate vs epoch.">
PING, three seeds. Loss and accuracy plateau by ≈ 15 epochs as in COBA, but here the **E rate locks flat at ≈ 4–9 Hz** — converged. (The I rate, dashed, keeps climbing to ≈ 40 Hz, driven by the still-growing input weights, the same force that drives COBA's E rate up.) The loop pins the excitatory rate; without it, COBA's drifts.
</Figure>
