## Abstract

A super-compound that puts the gamma onset's *empirics* directly above its *theory*. The top two rows are the [nb054](/notebooks/nb054/) turn-on — the E-rate, I-rate, and lobe–trough-contrast maps over the $W_{ei} \times W_{ie}$ plane, with three example rasters (loop-off / weak / strong). The bottom row is the [nb033](/notebooks/nb033/) mean-field bifurcation — the 4D Hopf crossing, the supercritical-reversible hysteresis sweep, and gamma frequency vs $\tau_\text{GABA}$. The smooth empirical turn-on and its mean-field explanation read as one story: *what* the network does, and *why*.

## Method

No new computation of its own — the entry imports the [nb054](/notebooks/nb054/) and [nb033](/notebooks/nb033/) runners and recomputes their data (the untrained-PING grid sweep and the calibrated 4D mean-field numerics), then lays the two anchor figures out as a single 3×3 panel. Recomputing rather than copying keeps the figure reproducible from the two sources. Full derivations and controls live in the source entries; this is the combined view.
