## New papers

Recently published, caught from the recency feeds (arXiv, bioRxiv, journals, PubMed).

### Synchronization modes in bipartite oscillator networks

[arxiv.org/abs/2606.20345](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20345) · arXiv q-bio.NC · 18 Jun 2026

Collective oscillations arising from interactions *between* an excitatory and an inhibitory population rather than from recurrent coupling within one, analysed in a Kuramoto–Sakaguchi framework with coexisting strongly- and partially-synchronized regimes.

**Relevance:** this is PING's phase-model abstraction. The strong-vs-partial synchrony coexistence echoes our lobe–trough contrast turning on across the coupling plane ([Claim 2](/articles/ar009/#claim-2-rhythmicity-turns-on-smoothly-across-the-weiwiew_ei-times-w_ieweiwie-plane--a-predictable-supercritical-hopf-bifurcation)); a cheap caricature of the bifurcation we report numerically.

### Disrupted hippocampal theta–gamma coupling and spike-field coherence following traumatic brain injury

[doi.org/10.7554/eLife.100642](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.100642) · eLife · 12 Jun 2026

After experimental TBI, hippocampal theta–gamma cross-frequency coupling and spike-field coherence break down, tracking the learning and memory deficits — evidence that precise rhythm-locked timing, not just rate, supports the computation.

**Relevance:** an in-vivo case where losing gamma timing costs function. Supports the framing that the rhythm does computational work (our [Claim 6](/articles/ar009/#claim-6-the-gating-is-dynamical-and-robust), timing- not mean-dependent), here shown by its pathological absence.

### Theta oscillations selectively support attentional exploration of space

[jneurosci.org/content/46/25/e2017252026](http://www.jneurosci.org/cgi/content/short/46/25/e2017252026?rss=1) · J Neurosci · 24 Jun 2026

Sustained vs reoriented spatial attention are dissociated by theta-band dynamics: the rhythm selectively supports *exploration* (reorienting) rather than sustained selection.

**Relevance:** background for the Introduction's "functions of rhythms" framing. A clean example of a rhythm carrying a specific control function — the kind of functional role we argue gamma plays for sparsity.

### Identifying structural design principles shaping the computational abilities of recurrent networks

[arxiv.org/abs/2606.23874](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23874) · arXiv q-bio.NC · 22 Jun 2026

Maps how recurrent-network *architecture* (connectivity structure) sets the computations a network can perform, beyond generic expressivity bounds.

**Relevance:** methodologically adjacent to [Claim 4](/articles/ar009/#claim-4-the-sparsity-is-supplied-by-the-architecture-not-learned) (the sparsity is architectural, not learned). If structure dictates computation, freezing the E↔I loop as a structural prior is the same move — worth citing when we argue architecture over training.

## Old papers

Resurfaced by Semantic Scholar recommendations — related work matched to the project bibliography, not necessarily recent.

### Empirical scaling laws in balanced networks with conductance-based synapses

[arxiv.org/abs/2605.12404](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12404) · Semantic Scholar (bibliography seed) · 2026

Derives scaling laws for strongly coupled, recurrent balanced networks built from conductance-based synapses, closing part of the gap between the analytically convenient current-based model and the biophysically realistic conductance one.

**Relevance:** directly load-bearing for [Claim 2](/articles/ar009/#claim-2-rhythmicity-turns-on-smoothly-across-the-weiwiew_ei-times-w_ieweiwie-plane--a-predictable-supercritical-hopf-bifurcation). Our mean-field is conductance-based and calibrated without a fitted scale; their scaling laws are a yardstick to check ours against, and a possible analytic handle on how the gamma onset moves with coupling.

### Approximate macroscopic dynamics of spiking networks from the transport equation

[arxiv.org/abs/2605.14319](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14319) · Semantic Scholar (bibliography seed) · 2026

A population-level reduction of integrate-and-fire networks: firing-rate fluctuations across neurons, trials, and populations derived from solutions to the transport (continuity) equation for the membrane-potential density.

**Relevance:** another route to the same place as our 4D conductance mean-field. Worth reading against [Claim 2](/articles/ar009/#claim-2-rhythmicity-turns-on-smoothly-across-the-weiwiew_ei-times-w_ieweiwie-plane--a-predictable-supercritical-hopf-bifurcation) to see whether a density approach predicts the Hopf onset more sharply than the low-dimensional reduction does.

### Multi-objective optimisation with oscillatory dynamics in spiking neural networks

[arxiv.org/abs/2605.25224](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25224) · Semantic Scholar (bibliography seed) · 2026

Fitting recurrent SNNs that must also produce realistic oscillations, framed as a multi-objective optimisation balancing task/data fit against oscillatory structure.

**Relevance:** the closest neighbour to what we do — training spiking nets that must stay rhythmic. Their explicit fit-vs-rhythm objective is an alternative to our spike-budget penalty and a good Discussion contrast.

### From cortical synchronous rhythm to brain-inspired learning: an oscillatory SNN with time-delayed coordination

[arxiv.org/abs/2605.01656](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01656) · Semantic Scholar (bibliography seed) · 2026

A learning primitive in which rhythm-determined spike timing — not just rate — carries information, coordinated through time delays across a distributed spiking network.

**Relevance:** sits in our gap statement (trained-but-rhythmic SNNs). Like [Yan et al. 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-63771-x), it imposes/leverages rhythm rather than letting an E↔I loop generate it — a contrast case for [Claim 4](/articles/ar009/#claim-4-the-sparsity-is-supplied-by-the-architecture-not-learned) (architecture-generated, not injected).

### Human neuronal firing varies with the frequency of local field potential oscillations

[doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.3003818](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.3003818) · Semantic Scholar (bibliography seed) · PLoS Biology · 2026

Human single-unit recordings showing neurons fire preferentially at a specific *frequency* of the surrounding LFP oscillation — frequency tuning of firing, beyond the usual phase tuning.

**Relevance:** empirical company for [Claim 5](/articles/ar009/#claim-5-the-e-rate-is-set-by-the-gamma-clock-one-spike-per-cell-per-cycle). If real neurons key their firing to oscillation frequency, that is the in-vivo shadow of our $r_E = a + p\,f_\gamma$ — the rate set by the rhythm. A citation candidate for the rate-vs-timing framing.
