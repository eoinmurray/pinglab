## Introduction

First rung-to-rung ablation on the [model ladder](/models/): [*snntorch-clone*](/models/#snntorch-clone) (snnTorch-style, $\Delta t$-free by construction) vs [*cuba*](/models/#cuba) (exact Euler, per-step drive scaled so each kick integrates to a fixed per-ms quantity). [*snntorch-library*](/models/#snntorch-library) sits alongside as a parity probe — any gap between it and *snntorch-clone* at matched config localises to the update rule, not to pinglab's implementation.

The snnTorch spec was not built for Δt-sweeps (see [why](/models/#snntorch-clone)), but real users follow [Tutorial 3 § 1.4](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_3.html#1-4-lapicque-s-lif-neuron-model)'s $\beta = e^{-\Delta t/\tau}$ pattern and readily stray into that regime. Halving *dt* doubles the number of per-step kicks in the snnTorch path while leaving *cuba*'s per-ms drive unchanged; Δt-stability is the sharpest test of that invariance.

## Method

Train each model at *dt* ∈ \{{dt1}, {dt2}\} ms, then sweep inference over *dt* ∈ \{{(cfg.dt_sweep ?? []).join(", ")}\} ms with weights frozen. Two regimes let us separate absolute eval-*dt* effects from ratio (eval/train) effects.

**Matched init.** *snntorch-clone* and *cuba* share the *CUBANet* class and allocate every parameter in the same order at matched seed; *cuba* then receives a one-shot init-scale (*W* × $dt/(1-\beta)$, *b* × $1/(1-\beta)$, recomputed per regime) that cancels its per-step drive factors at training *dt*. *snntorch-library* uses its own *kaiming_uniform_* — independent init, parity probe only. Preflight asserts pre-scale bit-equality for the *CUBANet* family. 

**Frozen inputs**: encoded once at the finest *dt* and OR-pooled to coarser ones, so only the LIF update changes across the sweep.

### Why the snnTorch rate drops as *dt* grows

The sag is the **bias term**. Under the snnTorch update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + W{\cdot}s + b$, $\beta = e^{-dt/\tau}$, the steady-state membrane under Poisson drive of rate $r$ is

$$\text{mem}_{ss} = \frac{W \cdot \mathbb{E}[s_{step}]}{1-\beta} + \frac{b}{1-\beta}.$$

With frozen inputs $\mathbb{E}[s_{step}] \approx r\,dt$ and $1-\beta \approx dt/\tau$ for small $dt/\tau$, the spike contribution is $W\,r\,\tau$ — *dt-invariant* — while the bias contribution is $b\,\tau/dt$, scaling as **1/dt**. Eval-*dt* spans 0.05–2.0 ms (40×) in both regimes, so the prediction is 40× in both.

Canonical paths exceed the pure 1/*dt* prediction: once membrane passes threshold, spike rate rises super-linearly with drive, amplifying the bias swing. *cuba*'s update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + \tfrac{1-\beta}{dt} W{\cdot}s + (1-\beta)\, b$ cancels both 1/(1-β) terms in steady state — bias becomes $b$, spike becomes $W\,r\,\tau$, both *dt*-invariant.

## Implications

The experiment confirms the [ladder's](/models/) core claim: the [snnTorch update](/models/#snntorch-clone) has no $\Delta t$-invariance, and [*cuba*'s](/models/#cuba) $(1-\beta)/dt$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors are the minimum correction that restores it. The snnTorch paths fan across 1–2 orders of magnitude of hidden rate in both regimes, matching the predicted $b\,\tau/dt$ bias scaling; *cuba* holds flat at dt={dt1}. The dt={dt2} regime tests only the snnTorch paths at this tier — *cuba*'s silent-network result there is a training-budget issue, not a ladder defect.

Every later rung inherits the continuous discretisation, so Δt-stability is a **ladder invariant** from CUBA onwards. Any further rung that breaks Δt-stability is a rung defect, not biophysics.
