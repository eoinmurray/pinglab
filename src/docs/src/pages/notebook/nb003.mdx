## Introduction

First rung-to-rung ablation on the [CUBA ladder](/models/#the-ladder). *cuba* scales the per-step drive by $(1-\beta)/dt$ so each kick integrates to a fixed per-ms quantity; *snntorch-clone* does not, carrying the canonical tutorial discretisation. *snntorch-library* (the real *snn.Leaky* cell, canonical update) sits alongside as a parity probe — so any gap at matched config localises to the update rule, not to pinglab's implementation. Halving *dt* doubles the number of per-step kicks in the canonical path while leaving *cuba*'s per-ms drive unchanged; Δt-stability is the sharpest test of that invariance.

## Method

Train each model at *dt* ∈ \{{dt1}, {dt2}\} ms, then sweep inference over *dt* ∈ \{{(cfg.dt_sweep ?? []).join(", ")}\} ms with weights frozen. Two regimes let us separate absolute eval-*dt* effects from ratio (eval/train) effects.

**Matched init.** *snntorch-clone* and *cuba* share the *SNNTorchNet* class and allocate every parameter in the same order at matched seed; *cuba* then receives a one-shot init-scale (*W* × $dt/(1-\beta)$, *b* × $1/(1-\beta)$, recomputed per regime) that cancels its per-step drive factors at training *dt*. *snntorch-library* uses its own *kaiming_uniform_* — independent init, parity probe only. Preflight asserts pre-scale bit-equality for the *SNNTorchNet* family. 

**Frozen inputs**: encoded once at the finest *dt* and OR-pooled to coarser ones, so only the LIF update changes across the sweep.

### Why the canonical rate drops as *dt* grows

The sag is the **bias term**. Under the canonical update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + W{\cdot}s + b$, $\beta = e^{-dt/\tau}$, the steady-state membrane under Poisson drive of rate $r$ is

$$\text{mem}_{ss} = \frac{W \cdot \mathbb{E}[s_{step}]}{1-\beta} + \frac{b}{1-\beta}.$$

With frozen inputs $\mathbb{E}[s_{step}] \approx r\,dt$ and $1-\beta \approx dt/\tau$ for small $dt/\tau$, the spike contribution is $W\,r\,\tau$ — *dt-invariant* — while the bias contribution is $b\,\tau/dt$, scaling as **1/dt**. Eval-*dt* spans 0.05–2.0 ms (40×) in both regimes, so the prediction is 40× in both.

Canonical paths exceed the pure 1/*dt* prediction: once membrane passes threshold, spike rate rises super-linearly with drive, amplifying the bias swing. *cuba*'s update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + \tfrac{1-\beta}{dt} W{\cdot}s + (1-\beta)\, b$ cancels both 1/(1-β) terms in steady state — bias becomes $b$, spike becomes $W\,r\,\tau$, both *dt*-invariant.

## Implications

The canonical discretisation is fundamentally tied to its train-*dt*: the per-ms bias drive scales as *dt_train*/*dt_eval*, independent of absolute *dt*, and the canonical paths fan across 1–2 orders of magnitude of rate in both regimes. *cuba*'s $(1-\beta)/dt$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors are the minimum correction — both required, one per drive term — making it the unique per-step scaling with a *dt*-invariant steady-state membrane. The dt={dt1} regime confirms the claim; dt={dt2} tests only the canonical paths at this tier.

Every later rung of the CUBA ladder inherits the continuous discretisation, so Δt-stability is a **ladder invariant** — if a later rung breaks it, that is a rung defect, not biophysics.
