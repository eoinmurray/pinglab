## Introduction

If an SNN is trained at one simulation timestep *dt* and evaluated at another, does it still classify correctly? The answer depends on how the LIF ODE is discretised.

[snnTorch](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io)'s [Tutorial 3 § 1.2](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_3.html#the-decay-rate-beta) sets $\Delta t = 1$ and absorbs the $(1-\beta)$ prefactor into the weight, yielding $U_{t+1} = \beta\,U_t + W\,s_t$. *dt* no longer appears in the update. This entry sweeps eval-*dt* against that update and against a version that keeps *dt* in it.

**Models** (see [the ladder](/models/)):

- [*standard-snn*](/models/#standard-snn) — pinglab reimplementation of snnTorch's *snn.Leaky*. Tutorial-3 rule, *dt*-free.
- [*snntorch-library*](/models/#snntorch-library) — snnTorch's *snn.Leaky* directly. Parity probe against *standard-snn*.
- [*cuba*](/models/#cuba) — same LIF, ZOH-integrated without the $\Delta t = 1$ substitution. *dt* appears in the update.

[Parthasarathy et al.](/references/#temporal-discretisation) report the same failure for the Tutorial-3 rule and propose two remedies: an average-loss regulariser (§2.5) and a ZOH weight-rescaling transport (App. 1C, Eq 27). nb003 bakes the ZOH prefactors into the update rule itself — *cuba*'s $(1-\beta)/dt$ on drive and $(1-\beta)$ on bias, matching Paper Eq 25.

## Method

1. Train each model at two timesteps: *dt* = {dt1} ms (fine) and *dt* = {dt2} ms (coarse).
2. Freeze weights and evaluate at a grid of *dt* values: \{{(cfg.dt_sweep ?? []).join(", ")}\} ms.
3. Plot accuracy and hidden-layer firing rate vs eval-*dt*. A *dt*-stable model stays flat across the grid.

- **Matched init.** *standard-snn* and *cuba* start from bit-identical random weights. *cuba* receives a one-shot rescaling that cancels its per-step drive factors at train-*dt*, so both models produce the same initial firing rate. Without it *cuba* is silent at init.
- **Frozen input, paper-matched transports.** The MNIST Poisson encoding is generated once at train-*dt* and transported to each sweep *dt* via one of the three transports the paper defines. All three are run per regime:
  - **upsample** — zero-pad to finer eval-*dt* (§2.1 Fig 1B)
  - **downsample** — sum-pool to coarser eval-*dt* (§2.3)
  - **resample** — fresh Poisson at the target *dt* (§2.1 alternative)
- **Matched surrogate gradient.** All three models use slope 1 (overriding snnTorch's slope-25 default), so *standard-snn* vs *snntorch-library* is a pure update-rule + init parity probe.

## Discussion

**Pinglab parity.** *standard-snn* and *snntorch-library* overlap in accuracy and share shape in rate (Results §2, §3), so the sag is the Tutorial-3 update rule rather than a pinglab implementation bug.

**Input transport is not the driver.** The resample row of Figures 8 and 9 tracks the count-preserving row on both sides of train-*dt*, so the *dt*-dependence lives in the LIF update rather than in input rebinning.

**Training budget is not the driver.** All three models reach comparable best-acc per regime (Results §1), so the eval-*dt* gap is a frozen-weight *dt*-stability effect rather than a capacity or budget artefact.

**Partial mechanism: the bias term.** Steady-state analysis accounts for the magnitude of the snnTorch fan but not the direction. Under the snnTorch update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + W{\cdot}s + b$ with $\beta = e^{-dt/\tau}$ and Poisson drive of rate $r$,

$$\text{mem}_{ss} = \frac{W \cdot \mathbb{E}[s_{step}]}{1-\beta} + \frac{b}{1-\beta}.$$

With frozen inputs $\mathbb{E}[s_{step}] \approx r\,dt$ and $1-\beta \approx dt/\tau$ for small $dt/\tau$, the spike contribution is $W\,r\,\tau$ (*dt*-invariant) and the bias contribution is $b\,\tau/dt$ (scales as 1/*dt*). The rate-ratio column in Results §2 is order-of-magnitude consistent with this: snnTorch ratios are in the $\sim$10–40× range. Figure 2 shows the fan's slope changes sign between regimes, which the bias-only prediction cannot reproduce. Each regime solves a different optimisation problem (drive/step, $\beta$, bias steady-state, and BPTT depth all change with train-*dt*) and lands on its own operating point; the bias term is the most tractable piece of that operating point.

***cuba* cancellation.** *cuba*'s update $\text{mem} = \beta \cdot \text{mem} + \tfrac{1-\beta}{dt} W{\cdot}s + (1-\beta)\, b$ cancels both $1/(1-\beta)$ terms in steady state: bias → $b$, spike → $W\,r\,\tau$, both *dt*-invariant.

**Practical upshot.** For fixed-*dt* work, Tutorial-3 is sufficient. For cross-*dt* transfer (sampling-rate changes, hardware with a different clock, comparison to continuous-time biology), reintroduce the prefactors. Every later rung of the [ladder](/models/) inherits CUBA's ODE-derived discretisation, so *dt*-stability is a ladder invariant from CUBA onwards.

## Success criteria

- ✅ ***cuba* rate-flat across eval-*dt***. Ratio $\sim${ratioFmt(rateRatio(cuba1Cp), 2)}× / $\sim${ratioFmt(rateRatio(cuba2Cp), 2)}× (criterion ≤ 2×).
- ✅ **snnTorch paths sag**. Rate ratios $\sim${ratioFmt(rateRatio(snn1Cp))}–{ratioFmt(rateRatio(lib2Cp))}× (criterion ≥ 10×).
- ✅ **Pinglab parity with snnTorch**. *standard-snn* tracks *snntorch-library*.
- ✅ **Encoder-mode neutrality**. Same sag/flat pattern under all three transports.
- ⚠ **Training-budget headroom**. Comparable best-acc per regime but budget-limited at **{cfg.tier ?? "?"}** tier; large-tier rerun in Next steps.
