## Introduction

Stub entry for the [Spiking Heidelberg Digits](https://zenkelab.org/resources/spiking-heidelberg-datasets-shd/) dataset — 20 classes of spoken digits (English + German) encoded as cochleogram spike trains over 700 input channels and $\sim$1 s per trial. Picked up after entry [004](/notebook/nb004/) parked sMNIST: SHD is natively event-based, so the dt / discretisation / encoding questions the earlier notebooks studied apply directly rather than being mediated by an artificial row-by-row encoding.

## Method

[*cuba*](/models/#cuba) model with two hidden layers (128, 128), trained on SHD at *medium* tier (2000 samples × 10 epochs) with the **rate** readout (cumulative last-hidden spike counts projected once at the final timestep). Trials run for 1000 ms at *dt* = 1 ms (1000 steps). Two axes are swept as a 3×3 grid: learning rate $\eta \in \{3\mathrm{e}{-3},\, 1\mathrm{e}{-2},\, 3\mathrm{e}{-2}\}$ and *init_scale_weight* $\in \{1.0,\, 3.0,\, 5.0\}$. *init_scale_bias* is fixed at $1/(1-\beta) \approx 10.5$ — the value derived in nb004 for depth-2 *cuba*. An earlier pre-sweep baseline at $(\eta = 0.01,\, \text{isw} = 3.0)$ compared *rate* (36.5%) vs *li* (21.75%) readouts at matched init, and picked *rate* as the honest pre-tuning reference for this sweep; the *li* readout needs its own init + $\tau$ pass before a fair readout comparison.

Two patterns stand out. (i) *Higher* $\eta$ *helps across the board* — the $\eta = 0.03$ column is on average the strongest, suggesting the medium-tier budget (10 epochs × 2000 samples) is learning-rate-limited rather than capacity-limited. (ii) *init_scale_weight* = 1.0 *is competitive* — the field-default no-extra-scaling baseline ties or beats the isw = 3.0 recipe inherited from nb004's sMNIST tuning. The best cell combines both: aggressive LR with no weight-init up-scaling, beating the pre-sweep baseline by ~2.5 pp.

## Implications

The nb004 cuba recipe (*init_scale_weight* = 3.0, tuned against row-by-row sMNIST) does not transfer cleanly to SHD — at depth 2, with SHD's dense spike input over 700 channels, isw = 1.0 is already saturating the drive, and pushing it higher no longer helps. Learning rate is the dominant knob at this budget.

## Success criteria

- ✅ **SHD pipeline trains end-to-end**. Depth-2 *cuba* on 700-channel SHD at the **{numbers.tier ?? "?"}** tier runs to completion with no NaN / collapse.
- ✅ **Monotonic training**. Train loss falls and test accuracy rises across {cfg.epochs ?? "?"} epochs — the network is integrating temporal evidence, not memorising or diverging.
- ✅ **Above chance**. Best test accuracy {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% vs the 5% chance floor (20 classes) — real classification signal.
- ✅ **Sweep beats pre-sweep baseline**. Winner cell improves on the untuned 36.5% reference by ~2.5 pp, confirming the grid is doing useful work rather than sitting on a flat surface.
- ❌ **Readout ordering matches field expectation**. Earlier matched-init comparison showed field-standard *li* readout (21.75% best) *underperforms* the *rate* baseline (36.5%) — the opposite of what Zenke-style pipelines report. Cause candidates (init-scale bleed, readout $\tau$ too short) flagged earlier; resolving this is a Next-steps item once the *cuba* baseline itself is tuned.
- ⚠ **Absolute accuracy below field reference**. Published SHD baselines with similar-sized recurrent SNNs reach 60–80%; the {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% post-sweep number here still has a gap to close via depth / width / budget.
