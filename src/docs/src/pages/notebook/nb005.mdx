## Introduction

Stub entry for the [Spiking Heidelberg Digits](https://zenkelab.org/resources/spiking-heidelberg-datasets-shd/) dataset — 20 classes of spoken digits (English + German) encoded as cochleogram spike trains over 700 input channels and $\sim$1 s per trial. Picked up after entry [004](/notebook/nb004/) parked sMNIST: SHD is natively event-based, so the dt / discretisation / encoding questions the earlier notebooks studied apply directly rather than being mediated by an artificial row-by-row encoding.

## Method

[*cuba*](/models/#cuba) model with two hidden layers (128, 128), trained on SHD at *medium* tier (2000 samples × 10 epochs) with the **rate** readout (cumulative last-hidden spike counts projected once at the final timestep). Trials run for 1000 ms at *dt* = 1 ms (1000 steps). Two axes are swept as a 3×3 grid: learning rate $\eta \in \{3\mathrm{e}{-3},\, 1\mathrm{e}{-2},\, 3\mathrm{e}{-2}\}$ and *init_scale_weight* $\in \{1.0,\, 3.0,\, 5.0\}$. *init_scale_bias* is fixed at $1/(1-\beta) \approx 10.5$ — the value derived in nb004 for depth-2 *cuba*. An earlier pre-sweep baseline at $(\eta = 0.01,\, \text{isw} = 3.0)$ compared *rate* (36.5%) vs *li* (21.75%) readouts at matched init, and picked *rate* as the honest pre-tuning reference for this sweep; the *li* readout needs its own init + $\tau$ pass before a fair readout comparison.

## Findings

### Hyperparameter pass

3×3 grid over $\eta$ and *init_scale_weight* at fixed depth-2 (128, 128), *rate* readout, *medium* tier. Best cells (best test accuracy, %):

| $\eta$ \ isw | 1.0 | 3.0 | 5.0 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3e-3 | 34.25 | 33.50 | 35.50 |
| 1e-2 | 35.50 | 36.50 | 38.50 |
| 3e-2 | **39.00** | 35.25 | 36.50 |

Winner $(\eta = 0.03,\, \text{isw} = 1.0)$ at 39.0%, improving on the 36.5% pre-sweep baseline by +2.5 pp. Two patterns. (i) *Higher* $\eta$ *helps across the board* — the $\eta = 0.03$ column is on average the strongest, suggesting the medium-tier budget (10 epochs × 2000 samples) is learning-rate-limited rather than capacity-limited. (ii) *init_scale_weight* = 1.0 *is competitive* — the field-default no-extra-scaling baseline ties or beats the isw = 3.0 recipe inherited from nb004's sMNIST tuning.

### Width & depth pass

Follow-up 2×4 grid over $\eta \in \{0.03, 0.1\}$ and hidden architecture $\in$ \{128×2, 256×2, 512×2, 256×3\} at fixed $\text{isw} = 1.0$, same *medium* tier. Best test accuracy (%):

| hidden \ $\eta$ | 0.03 | 0.1 |
|---|---|---|
| 128×2 | **39.00** | 28.00 |
| 256×2 | 37.50 | 25.00 |
| 512×2 | 34.75 | 15.25 |
| 256×3 | 33.00 | 10.75 |

**Null result** for this pass. Three patterns. (i) *Wider hurts* — 128×2 → 256×2 → 512×2 degrades monotonically at $\eta = 0.03$ (39.0 → 37.5 → 34.75%); the medium-tier budget can't fit the larger parameter counts cleanly. (ii) *Deeper hurts more* — 256×3 is the worst column at either LR. (iii) $\eta = 0.1$ *is unstable and the instability scales with capacity* — the column degrades from 28% (128×2) to 10.75% (256×3), consistent with the surrogate-gradient noise floor dominating in higher-dimensional weight spaces.

Winner cell (128×2, $\eta = 0.03$, isw = 1.0, *rate* readout) retrained at *large* tier (**{cfg.max_samples ?? "?"}** samples × **{cfg.epochs ?? "?"}** epochs) confirms the budget hypothesis from step 2.

Observations. (i) **Large tier lifts the ceiling**. The medium 39% → large {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% jump validates step 2's "budget-limited, not capacity-limited" reading of the ceiling. (ii) **Not strictly monotonic** in the plateau — best is epoch ~30, final epoch is {cell.final_acc ?? "?"}%; the ~5 pp of within-plateau swing is gradient / minibatch noise rather than divergence. (iii) **Still well below the 60–80% published SHD baselines** — budget is not the only missing lever, just the first-order one. Recurrent connections, readout retuning, and Dale's-law constraints stay as unexplored axes.

## Implications

Two takeaways close the first-order baseline. (i) *Capacity isn't the bottleneck* at the step-1 hyperparameter optimum — scaling width and depth at medium tier regresses, not improves, the accuracy. (ii) *Budget is the first-order lever*: quadrupling the budget (2.5× samples, 4× epochs) lifts the accuracy from 39% to {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}%. The remaining gap to the 60–80% field reference is unlikely to close with more budget alone — the next productive directions are qualitative (recurrence, readout, encoding), not quantitative.

## Success criteria

- ✅ **SHD pipeline trains end-to-end**. Depth-2 *cuba* on 700-channel SHD at the **{numbers.tier ?? "?"}** tier runs to completion with no NaN / collapse.
- ✅ **Monotonic training**. Train loss falls and test accuracy rises across {cfg.epochs ?? "?"} epochs — the network is integrating temporal evidence, not memorising or diverging.
- ✅ **Above chance**. Best test accuracy {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% vs the 5% chance floor (20 classes) — real classification signal.
- ✅ **Step-1 sweep beats pre-sweep baseline**. Hyperparameter winner (39.0%) improves on the 36.5% pre-sweep reference by +2.5 pp, confirming the grid is doing useful work.
- ❌ **Scaling capacity improves accuracy at fixed budget**. Width (256×2, 512×2) and depth (256×3) all *underperform* 128×2 at matched budget; null result means the medium-tier ceiling is budget-limited, not capacity-limited.
- ✅ **More budget lifts the ceiling**. Retraining the winner at *large* tier ({cfg.max_samples ?? "?"} samples × {cfg.epochs ?? "?"} epochs) lifts best_acc from 39% to {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% — confirms the budget-limited reading from step 2.
- ⚠ **Within-plateau training noise**. At large tier the test accuracy swings ~5 pp epoch-to-epoch once past ~ep 6; loss trends down but is noisy. Not divergence, but a sign that minibatch noise dominates once the network settles.
- ❌ **Readout ordering matches field expectation**. Earlier matched-init comparison showed field-standard *li* readout (21.75% best) *underperforms* the *rate* baseline (36.5%) — the opposite of what Zenke-style pipelines report. Cause candidates (init-scale bleed, readout $\tau$ too short) flagged earlier; resolving this is a Next-steps item once the *cuba* baseline itself is tuned.
- ⚠ **Absolute accuracy below field reference**. Published SHD baselines with similar-sized recurrent SNNs reach 60–80%; the {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}% post-sweep number here still has a gap to close via depth / width / budget.
