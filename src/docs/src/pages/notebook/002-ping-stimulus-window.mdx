# PING stimulus window

*002 · Saturday, April 18 2026 · {nbRunId} (seed {cfg.seed}, {cfg.tier})*

**PING** — Pyramidal-Interneuron Network Gamma — is a rhythm that emerges when a population of excitatory (**E**) neurons and inhibitory (**I**) neurons drive each other: E fires, recruits I, I silences E, I decays, E fires again. The cycle repeats at ~30–80 Hz ("gamma"). But it only runs when E gets enough external drive; below threshold the network sits in uncorrelated background firing.

This entry maps that threshold. Each frame of the video below is a fresh {cfg.sim_ms} ms simulation. The "stim window" is {cfg.step_on_ms}–{cfg.step_off_ms} ms — before and after the window, E receives a baseline drive; during the window, that drive is multiplied by the **overdrive** factor shown on the frame. The sweep walks overdrive from {cfg.scan.min}× (no boost) to {cfg.scan.max}× (strong boost) over {cfg.scan.frames} frames.

<video controls loop muted playsinline width="100%" src="/figures/notebook/002-ping-stimulus-window/scan.mp4" />

What to watch: the top raster is E spikes, the smaller raster below is I spikes. At low overdrive the stim window looks the same as the rest of the trial. As overdrive grows past threshold, vertical bands appear inside the window — that's gamma: E firing in near-synchrony, I firing in the gaps between E bursts.

At the canonical overdrive of {cfg.canonical_overdrive}×, E fires at {r.pre.e.toFixed(1)} Hz before the window with I silent. Inside the window I wakes to {r.stim.i.toFixed(1)} Hz and E's time-averaged rate drops to {r.stim.e.toFixed(1)} Hz — the bursts are higher, but I-dominated gaps pull the mean down. After the window: {r.post.e.toFixed(1)} / {r.post.i.toFixed(1)} Hz. The onset is sharp: below threshold nothing happens, above it the rhythm snaps on.

## Time-step sensitivity

Gamma has a period of ~15–30 ms; if the integration step *dt* is too coarse, the simulator can't resolve the E→I→E round-trip and the rhythm distorts or diverges. Here overdrive is held at {dtScan.fixed_overdrive}× and *dt* is swept from {dtScan.min} ms to {dtScan.max} ms over {dtScan.frames} frames.

<video controls loop muted playsinline width="100%" src="/figures/notebook/002-ping-stimulus-window/scan_dt.mp4" />

At fine *dt* the rhythm is clean. As *dt* grows past a few tenths of a millisecond the gamma bands smear, then the sim becomes unstable. This is the upper bound on how coarsely you can step the solver while still getting PING — a practical rule for picking *dt* when scaling up the network.

Zoom in on the gamma-onset threshold (overdrive 2×–5×) and pin down where the rhythm first appears.

Widen the window to 800 ms and count gamma cycles inside it — confirm the cycle period is set by the I-cell decay constant, not the window length.
