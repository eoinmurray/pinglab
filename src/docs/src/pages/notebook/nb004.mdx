## Introduction

Stub entry for sequential MNIST (sMNIST). MNIST is presented one pixel (or one row) at a time over the trial window rather than as a full image, so the network has to integrate information across time to classify — a memory/dynamics test rather than an input-encoding test.

## Method

First-cell baseline to validate the pipeline: one [*cuba*](/models/#cuba) model with two hidden layers (64, 64), trained on sMNIST at *extra_small* tier (200 samples × 3 epochs). Each MNIST digit is presented one row at a time over 280 ms (28 rows × 10 ms/row) at *dt* = 1 ms. Depth ≥ 2 is required: single-hidden-layer networks bottleneck at the 28-input interface and fail to learn on sMNIST. This cell exists to prove end-to-end correctness of the dataset loader + row-by-row encoder + depth-2 ladder config, not to measure anything specific — the cell matrix comes after.

## Findings

Baseline reaches 26% accuracy at medium tier (2000 samples × 10 epochs, *init_scale_weight* = 3.0, *init_scale_bias* ≈ 10.5) — well above 10% chance, monotonic climb, no collapse. Pipeline and depth-2 ladder are confirmed working. Excitatory rate decays from 6 Hz to 2 Hz over training while accuracy still rises, so the readout is learning despite quieter dynamics.

## Implications

The row-by-row sMNIST setup works, but this entry is being parked at the baseline rather than built out into a full cell matrix. The 28-pixel row bottleneck is an artificial temporal signal, and the input-encoding question studied in entry [003](/notebook/nb003/) barely applies when rows are already pixel values. A natively event-based dataset is a stronger fit for the dt/discretisation/encoding comparisons this notebook is built around.
