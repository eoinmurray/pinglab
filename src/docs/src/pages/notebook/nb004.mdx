## Introduction

[Models](/models/) previously discretised COBA/PING's membrane with *forward Euler* while CUBA used exact (exponential) Euler on the homogeneous part. That asymmetry made the CUBA → COBA rung of the ladder a mix of *what's in the ODE* and *which integrator*, not a clean isolation of the biophysical additions.

This entry accompanies the PR that unifies the discretisation: COBA/PING now use **exponential Euler under a zero-order hold** on $g_e, g_i$ over each step. The closed-form update is
$$
v_{t+1} = v_\infty + (v_t - v_\infty)\,\exp(-\Delta t / \tau_{\text{eff}})
$$
with $\tau_{\text{eff}} = C_m / (g_L + g_e + g_i)$ and $v_\infty = (g_L E_L + g_e E_e + g_i E_i) / (g_L + g_e + g_i)$. Changing the integrator changes the gradient magnitudes BPTT sees through the membrane; this entry checks that (a) matched accuracy is reachable under the new integrator, and (b) the current *--cm-back-scale 1000* default is still appropriate.

## Method

PING is trained on MNIST at matched config under the cartesian product of two integrators (*expeuler*, *fwd*) and four dampening factors (*cm_back_scale ∈ {200, 500, 1000, 2000}*). All other flags are held fixed: *dt = 0.25 ms*, *ei-strength = 0.5*, *w-in = 1.2*, *w-in-sparsity = 0.95*, *lr = 1e-4*, *adaptive-lr*. Seed is fixed so weight init and Poisson encoding are identical across cells. Tier {cfg.tier ?? "?"} — see [conventions § 8](/llm-conventions/#8-run-sizing-tiers).

## Implications

At the *extra_small* tier (3 epochs, 200 samples) the two integrators reach parity from *cm_back_scale = 500* upward, with exp-Euler holding 55% across 500–2000 while fwd-Euler slips to 50% above 500. Both collapse to ~20% at *cm_back_scale = 200*, confirming the dampening's lower working edge is somewhere between 200 and 500 for this config. The existing *cm_back_scale = 1000* default remains a reasonable choice under the new integrator — exp-Euler is flat across the upper three cells, so the ranking gives no reason to change the shipped default. These numbers rank cells; they don't resolve small accuracy deltas. A medium/large tier run would be needed to decide whether exp-Euler offers a meaningful accuracy gain beyond the robustness-across-*cm_back_scale* observation.

## Next steps

- Re-run at a larger tier (*medium* or *large*) once the *cm_back_scale* optimum is identified — *extra_small* only establishes the ranking, not a production-grade accuracy.
- Extend the parity check to feedforward-only COBA (*--ei-strength 0*) to rule out interaction between the integrator change and the PING loop specifically.
- Check whether the *dt*-stability picture (nb003) shifts under exp-Euler — PING at coarse *dt* is where the forward-Euler membrane was weakest.
