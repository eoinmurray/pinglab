## Introduction

[nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) characterised the PING rate floor empirically: a recruitment cliff where the I-loop engages, gamma at ≈25 Hz, $W^{EI}$ moves the cliff and $W^{IE}$ does not. Both phenomena are bifurcation behaviours; the natural framework is Wilson-Cowan mean-field. The goal here is to derive the recruitment threshold $I_\text{ext}^\star$ and oscillation frequency $\omega^\star$ from the equations alone and check whether they match the spiking simulations.

## Method

### Derivation

Five steps reduce the spiking network to a 4D mean-field system.

**1. Single-cell COBA equations.** E and I cells obey (see [art003](/articles/art003/)):

$$
C_m^E\,\dot V_j^E = -g_L^E(V_j^E - E_L) - g_{i,j}^E(V_j^E - E_I) + I_{\text{ext},j}, \qquad
C_m^I\,\dot V_k^I = -g_L^I(V_k^I - E_L) - g_{e,k}^I(V_k^I - E_E)
$$

(no AMPA on E and no I→I term because $W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$). Synaptic conductances are first-order filters of presynaptic spike trains:

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_{e,k}^I = -g_{e,k}^I + \sum_j W^{EI}_{kj}\,s_j^E, \qquad
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_{i,j}^E = -g_{i,j}^E + \sum_k W^{IE}_{jk}\,s_k^I
$$

**2. Population averaging.** Define rates $E(t) = N_E^{-1} \sum_j s_j^E(t)$, $I(t) = N_I^{-1} \sum_k s_k^I(t)$. Assume homogeneous coupling $W^{EI}_{kj} = w^{EI}$, $W^{IE}_{jk} = w^{IE}$; conductances become single population variables driven by the rates. Defining $\tilde W^{EI} = w^{EI} N_E$ and $\tilde W^{IE} = w^{IE} N_I$:

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I = -g_e^I + \tilde W^{EI}\,E, \qquad \tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E = -g_i^E + \tilde W^{IE}\,I
$$

**3. Driving-force linearisation.** Approximate $V \approx V_\text{rest}$ in the synaptic terms $-g(V - E_\text{rev})$. This makes the synaptic current linear in conductance: $-g_i^E \Delta V_\text{inh}$ with $\Delta V_\text{inh} = V_\text{rest} - E_I = 15$ mV (similarly $\Delta V_\text{exc} = 65$ mV for E→I). With $V$ approximately constant, the population firing rate is a function of the effective current via a single-neuron f-I curve $\Phi$.

**4. Population time constants.** Add relaxational dynamics with membrane time constants $\tau_E, \tau_I$:

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh}), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I(g_e^I\,\Delta V_\text{exc})
$$

**5. Absorb constants.** Rescale $g \mapsto g \cdot \Delta V$, $W^{IE} \equiv \tilde W^{IE}\,\Delta V_\text{inh}$, $W^{EI} \equiv \tilde W^{EI}\,\Delta V_\text{exc}$. The result is the 4D system used below.

| Step | Approximation | What's lost |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | homogeneous coupling, smooth-rate density | heterogeneity, finite-size variability |
| 3 | $V \approx V_\text{rest}$ in driving force | shunting, threshold-crossing nonlinearity |
| 4 | single relaxational mode at $\tau_E, \tau_I$ | sub-membrane dynamics, refractoriness |

### The 4D system

After (1)–(5), the mean-field equations are

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I(g_e^I) \tag{1}
$$

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I = -g_e^I + W^{EI}\,E, \qquad
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E = -g_i^E + W^{IE}\,I \tag{2}
$$

in state $(E, I, g_e^I, g_i^E)$.

### Why we can't collapse to 2D

If we send $\tau_\text{AMPA}, \tau_\text{GABA} \to 0$ in (2), $g_e^I \to W^{EI} E$ and $g_i^E \to W^{IE} I$ instantaneously, recovering the textbook 2D Wilson-Cowan system. But the Jacobian of the 2D system has

$$
\mathrm{tr}(J_\text{2D}) = -\frac{1}{\tau_E} - \frac{1}{\tau_I}
$$

— constant in $I_\text{ext}$ and strictly negative. Hopf requires $\mathrm{tr}(J) = 0$, so **the 2D reduction with $W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$ cannot oscillate**. Canonical Wilson-Cowan works around this with non-zero $c_1$ (E→E self-excitation), which puts a positive term on the diagonal and lets the trace cross zero. Our architecture sets $W^{EE} = 0$ by construction. An alternative 2D route — adding a cubic $-\beta E^3$ self-feedback term à la FitzHugh-Nagumo — is derived and tested in the *"2D-cubic dead end"* section below; it also fails. The 4D system is the right reduction.

### 4D Jacobian and Hopf criterion

At a fixed point $(E^\star, I^\star, g_e^{I\star}, g_i^{E\star})$:

$$
J = \begin{pmatrix}
-1/\tau_E & 0 & 0 & -\Phi'_E/\tau_E \\
0 & -1/\tau_I & \Phi'_I/\tau_I & 0 \\
W^{EI}/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 \\
0 & W^{IE}/\tau_\text{GABA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{GABA}
\end{pmatrix} \tag{3}
$$

with $\Phi'_E, \Phi'_I$ at the fixed-point arguments. Eigenvalues come in two complex-conjugate pairs once the loop is engaged. The Hopf is the smallest $I_\text{ext}^\star$ at which the leading pair crosses the imaginary axis: $\mathrm{Re}(\lambda) = 0$, $\mathrm{Im}(\lambda) = \omega^\star$, with predicted gamma frequency $f^\star = \omega^\star / (2\pi)$.

Order-of-magnitude estimate: balancing $\tau_\text{AMPA}$ and $\tau_\text{GABA}$ gives $\omega^\star \sim 1/\sqrt{\tau_\text{AMPA} \tau_\text{GABA}}$, so $f^\star \approx 37$ Hz — gamma range.

### Super- vs subcritical Hopf

Two qualitative types, distinguished by the sign of the first Lyapunov coefficient $\ell_1$ (Kuznetsov 2004):

- **Supercritical** ($\ell_1 < 0$): stable limit cycle emerges smoothly past the bifurcation, $A \propto \sqrt{I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star}$, no hysteresis.
- **Subcritical** ($\ell_1 > 0$): amplitude jumps discontinuously, hysteresis on reverse sweep.

The numerics use direct integration to determine criticality: simulate the 4D ODE at a sequence of $I_\text{ext}$ values around $I_\text{ext}^\star$, measure asymptotic amplitude, check whether $A^2$ is linear in $(I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star)$. nb038's Figure 3 zoom (smooth I-rate onset) and nb025 Figure 6 (no hysteresis) both predict supercritical.

### Caveats

- Mean-field assumes asynchronous firing — PING is precisely the regime where synchrony matters. Hopf calculation is most valid at infinitesimal amplitude (right at the bifurcation), loses faithfulness as the cycle grows.
- Driving-force linearisation drops $V$-dependence; conductance-based shunting effects are absorbed into $\Phi$.
- Refractoriness saturates $\Phi$ at $1/\tau_\text{ref}$; not load-bearing for silent-FP analysis.

## The 2D-cubic dead end

A natural follow-up question (raised by Tim) is whether the missing positive linear feedback can be put back by an explicit cubic self-suppression in $E$, à la FitzHugh-Nagumo. The derivation below shows the cubic cannot save us — *it stabilises amplitude but does not destabilise the fixed point*. The merged figure below confirms it numerically with the same parameters as the 4D analysis above.

### Derivation

Start from the 2D Wilson-Cowan equations without self-coupling:

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E\!\left(I_\text{ext} - W^{IE} I\right) \tag{6a}
$$

$$
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I\!\left(W^{EI} E\right) \tag{6b}
$$

with sigmoid gains $\Phi_{E,I}(x) = r_\text{max}/(1 + e^{-(x-\theta)/k})$. Two reductions get us to a polynomial system.

**Step 1 — Taylor-expand $\Phi_E$ at its inflection.** Let $u = I_\text{ext} - W^{IE} I - \theta_E$ be the drive offset. Sigmoids are odd around the inflection, so even-order terms vanish:

$$
\Phi_E(\theta_E + u) = \Phi_E(\theta_E) + a\,u + \tfrac{1}{6} b\,u^3 + O(u^5) \tag{7}
$$

with $a = \Phi'_E(\theta_E) = r_\text{max}/(4 k_E) > 0$ and $b = \Phi'''_E(\theta_E) < 0$.

**Step 2 — Linearise $\Phi_I$** around the silent state: $\Phi_I(W^{EI} E) \approx \Phi'_I(0)\,W^{EI}\,E$.

**Step 3 — Express the cubic in $E$ rather than $u$.** At leading order the linearised $E$ dynamics give $E^\star \approx a\,u$ at any quasi-steady drive, so $u \approx E/a$. Substituting back into the cubic term, $\tfrac{1}{6} b\,u^3 \to (b/6 a^3)\,E^3 \equiv -\beta\,E^3$ with $\beta \equiv -b/(6 a^3) > 0$.

Folding constants into $I_\text{ext}$ and writing $\alpha \equiv a$, $\gamma \equiv a\,W^{IE}$, $\delta \equiv \Phi'_I(0)\,W^{EI}$ leaves the FitzHugh-Nagumo system:

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \alpha\,E - \beta\,E^3 - \gamma\,I + \alpha\,I_\text{ext} \tag{8a}
$$

$$
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \delta\,E \tag{8b}
$$

### Why the cubic cannot save us

The Jacobian of (8) at a fixed point $E^\star$ is

$$
J = \begin{pmatrix} (-1 + \alpha - 3\beta (E^\star)^2)/\tau_E & -\gamma/\tau_E \\ \delta/\tau_I & -1/\tau_I \end{pmatrix}, \tag{9}
$$

and a Hopf requires $\text{tr}(J)$ to cross zero. Rearranging $\text{tr}(J) = 0$:

$$
\alpha - 3\beta (E^\star)^2 = 1 + \tau_E/\tau_I. \tag{10}
$$

The left-hand side is maximised at $E^\star = 0$ where it equals $\alpha$. The right-hand side is bounded below by 1. So a necessary condition for *any* fixed point to lose stability is

$$
\boxed{\;\alpha > 1 + \tau_E/\tau_I.\;} \tag{11}
$$

The cubic enters only through the $-3\beta (E^\star)^2$ term, which *lowers* the left-hand side. It can stabilise an already-destabilised fixed point at finite amplitude (the saturating self-suppression that fixes the limit-cycle radius in a supercritical Hopf), but it cannot raise $\alpha$ past the threshold. Linear destabilisation must come from elsewhere.

The parameters are inherited from the 4D analysis above, so the comparison is on equal footing. Plugging in:

$$
\alpha = \frac{r_\text{max}}{4 k_E} = \frac{0.2}{4 \cdot 0.2} = 0.25, \qquad 1 + \tau_E/\tau_I = 5.
$$

We have $\alpha = 0.25$ where we need $\alpha > 5$ — a factor of twenty off. The trace stays strictly negative for all $I_\text{ext}$ and no Hopf exists. Making the sigmoid steeper ($k_E$ smaller) raises $\alpha$ linearly, but reaching $\alpha = 5$ would require $k_E = 0.01$ — a sigmoid that is essentially a step function, qualitatively wrong as a gain model for an LIF population near rheobase. The deficit is structural, not tuning-limited.

### What's structurally missing

The linear gain $\alpha$ is the strength of positive feedback on $E$. The canonical Wilson-Cowan 1972 model includes an E→E self-coupling $W^{EE}$, which renormalises $\alpha \to W^{EE}\,\Phi'_E$; with $W^{EE} \approx 12$ and a steep sigmoid this comfortably clears the threshold, and the cubic reduction Hopfs by the standard mechanism. Our Börgers-style PING architecture has $W^{EE} = 0$ — there is no E-loop to renormalise $\alpha$, and so the 2D-cubic route is structurally inapplicable regardless of how the cubic is tuned.

The destabilisation that does drive the 4D Hopf above comes from a different mechanism entirely: the synaptic phase delays $\tau_\text{AMPA}, \tau_\text{GABA}$. These have no analogue in (8) — adiabatically eliminating them to get a 2D system kills the very feature that produces the oscillation. A faithful 2D reduction would have to keep the synaptic timescales as explicit delays, yielding a delay-differential equation rather than a polynomial ODE.
