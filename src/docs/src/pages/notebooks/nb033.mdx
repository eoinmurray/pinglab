## Introduction

[nb024](/notebooks/nb024/) characterised the PING rate floor empirically: a recruitment cliff where the I-loop engages, gamma at ≈25 Hz, $W^{EI}$ moves the cliff and $W^{IE}$ does not. Both phenomena are bifurcation behaviours; the natural framework is Wilson-Cowan mean-field. The goal here is to derive the recruitment threshold $I_\text{ext}^\star$ and oscillation frequency $\omega^\star$ from the equations alone and check whether they match the spiking simulations.

## Method

### Derivation

Five steps reduce the spiking network to a 4D mean-field system.

**1. Single-cell COBA equations.** E and I cells obey (see [art003](/articles/art003/)):

$$
C_m^E\,\dot V_j^E = -g_L^E(V_j^E - E_L) - g_{i,j}^E(V_j^E - E_I) + I_{\text{ext},j}, \qquad
C_m^I\,\dot V_k^I = -g_L^I(V_k^I - E_L) - g_{e,k}^I(V_k^I - E_E)
$$

(no AMPA on E and no I→I term because $W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$). Synaptic conductances are first-order filters of presynaptic spike trains:

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_{e,k}^I = -g_{e,k}^I + \sum_j W^{EI}_{kj}\,s_j^E, \qquad
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_{i,j}^E = -g_{i,j}^E + \sum_k W^{IE}_{jk}\,s_k^I
$$

**2. Population averaging.** Define rates $E(t) = N_E^{-1} \sum_j s_j^E(t)$, $I(t) = N_I^{-1} \sum_k s_k^I(t)$. Assume homogeneous coupling $W^{EI}_{kj} = w^{EI}$, $W^{IE}_{jk} = w^{IE}$; conductances become single population variables driven by the rates. Defining $\tilde W^{EI} = w^{EI} N_E$ and $\tilde W^{IE} = w^{IE} N_I$:

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I = -g_e^I + \tilde W^{EI}\,E, \qquad \tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E = -g_i^E + \tilde W^{IE}\,I
$$

**3. Driving-force linearisation.** Approximate $V \approx V_\text{rest}$ in the synaptic terms $-g(V - E_\text{rev})$. This makes the synaptic current linear in conductance: $-g_i^E \Delta V_\text{inh}$ with $\Delta V_\text{inh} = V_\text{rest} - E_I = 15$ mV (similarly $\Delta V_\text{exc} = 65$ mV for E→I). With $V$ approximately constant, the population firing rate is a function of the effective current via a single-neuron f-I curve $\Phi$.

**4. Population time constants.** Add relaxational dynamics with membrane time constants $\tau_E, \tau_I$:

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh}), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I(g_e^I\,\Delta V_\text{exc})
$$

**5. Absorb constants.** Rescale $g \mapsto g \cdot \Delta V$, $W^{IE} \equiv \tilde W^{IE}\,\Delta V_\text{inh}$, $W^{EI} \equiv \tilde W^{EI}\,\Delta V_\text{exc}$. The result is the 4D system used below.

| Step | Approximation | What's lost |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | homogeneous coupling, smooth-rate density | heterogeneity, finite-size variability |
| 3 | $V \approx V_\text{rest}$ in driving force | shunting, threshold-crossing nonlinearity |
| 4 | single relaxational mode at $\tau_E, \tau_I$ | sub-membrane dynamics, refractoriness |

### The 4D system

After (1)–(5), the mean-field equations are

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I(g_e^I) \tag{1}
$$

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I = -g_e^I + W^{EI}\,E, \qquad
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E = -g_i^E + W^{IE}\,I \tag{2}
$$

in state $(E, I, g_e^I, g_i^E)$.

### Why we can't collapse to 2D

If we send $\tau_\text{AMPA}, \tau_\text{GABA} \to 0$ in (2), $g_e^I \to W^{EI} E$ and $g_i^E \to W^{IE} I$ instantaneously, recovering the textbook 2D Wilson-Cowan system. But the Jacobian of the 2D system has

$$
\mathrm{tr}(J_\text{2D}) = -\frac{1}{\tau_E} - \frac{1}{\tau_I}
$$

— constant in $I_\text{ext}$ and strictly negative. Hopf requires $\mathrm{tr}(J) = 0$, so **the 2D reduction with $W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$ cannot oscillate**. Canonical Wilson-Cowan works around this with non-zero $c_1$ (E→E self-excitation), which puts a positive term on the diagonal and lets the trace cross zero. Our architecture sets $W^{EE} = 0$ by construction. An alternative 2D route — adding a cubic $-\beta E^3$ self-feedback term à la FitzHugh-Nagumo — is explored in [nb034](/notebooks/nb034/) and also fails for our parameters. The 4D system is the right reduction.

### 4D Jacobian and Hopf criterion

At a fixed point $(E^\star, I^\star, g_e^{I\star}, g_i^{E\star})$:

$$
J = \begin{pmatrix}
-1/\tau_E & 0 & 0 & -\Phi'_E/\tau_E \\
0 & -1/\tau_I & \Phi'_I/\tau_I & 0 \\
W^{EI}/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 \\
0 & W^{IE}/\tau_\text{GABA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{GABA}
\end{pmatrix} \tag{3}
$$

with $\Phi'_E, \Phi'_I$ at the fixed-point arguments. Eigenvalues come in two complex-conjugate pairs once the loop is engaged. The Hopf is the smallest $I_\text{ext}^\star$ at which the leading pair crosses the imaginary axis: $\mathrm{Re}(\lambda) = 0$, $\mathrm{Im}(\lambda) = \omega^\star$, with predicted gamma frequency $f^\star = \omega^\star / (2\pi)$.

Order-of-magnitude estimate: balancing $\tau_\text{AMPA}$ and $\tau_\text{GABA}$ gives $\omega^\star \sim 1/\sqrt{\tau_\text{AMPA} \tau_\text{GABA}}$, so $f^\star \approx 37$ Hz — gamma range.

### Super- vs subcritical Hopf

Two qualitative types, distinguished by the sign of the first Lyapunov coefficient $\ell_1$ (Kuznetsov 2004):

- **Supercritical** ($\ell_1 < 0$): stable limit cycle emerges smoothly past the bifurcation, $A \propto \sqrt{I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star}$, no hysteresis.
- **Subcritical** ($\ell_1 > 0$): amplitude jumps discontinuously, hysteresis on reverse sweep.

The numerics use direct integration to determine criticality: simulate the 4D ODE at a sequence of $I_\text{ext}$ values around $I_\text{ext}^\star$, measure asymptotic amplitude, check whether $A^2$ is linear in $(I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star)$. nb024's Figure 19 zoom (smooth I-rate onset) and Figure 6 (no hysteresis) both predict supercritical.

### Caveats

- Mean-field assumes asynchronous firing — PING is precisely the regime where synchrony matters. Hopf calculation is most valid at infinitesimal amplitude (right at the bifurcation), loses faithfulness as the cycle grows.
- Driving-force linearisation drops $V$-dependence; conductance-based shunting effects are absorbed into $\Phi$.
- Refractoriness saturates $\Phi$ at $1/\tau_\text{ref}$; not load-bearing for silent-FP analysis.

## Results
