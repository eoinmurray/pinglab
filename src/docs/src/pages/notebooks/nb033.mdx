## Abstract

A mean-field account of [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/)'s recruitment cliff. In the 4D conductance DMFT the cliff is a **Hopf bifurcation** of the silent fixed point: its drive threshold is read off the Jacobian eigenvalues, the imaginary part of the crossing pair gives a gamma rhythm at $f^\star \approx 24.5$ Hz set by the synaptic timescales, and direct simulation classifies the onset as **supercritical** ‚Äî a soft but sharp switch from silence into a small-amplitude cycle.

## Methods

### The 4D DMFT model

We start with $N_E + N_I$ coupled conductance-based spiking neurons and reduce them, step by step, to four coupled ODEs for the population-mean rates $E(t), I(t)$ and the population-mean conductances $g_e^I(t), g_i^E(t)$. Each step replaces one degree of freedom with a smoother, lower-dimensional approximation; the cost of each is recorded at the end. The full chain is:

1. Summary of COBA model
2. Homogeneous coupling and population means
3. Driving-force linearisation
4. Population rate from an f-I curve
5. Absorb the driving-force constants.

**Step 1. Summary of COBA model.** Index E cells by $j \in \{1, \ldots, N_E\}$ and I cells by $k \in \{1, \ldots, N_I\}$. From the [COBANet model](/articles/ar003/) membrane equation, with the architecture constraints $W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$ (no recurrent E‚ÜíE, no I‚ÜíI) and an external drive $I_{\text{ext},j}$ added on E:

$$
C_m^E\,\dot V_j^E \;=\; -g_L^E(V_j^E - E_L) \;-\; g_{i,j}^E(V_j^E - E_I) \;+\; I_{\text{ext},j}
\tag{1}
$$

$$
C_m^I\,\dot V_k^I \;=\; -g_L^I(V_k^I - E_L) \;-\; g_{e,k}^I(V_k^I - E_E)
\tag{2}
$$

The E cell has no $g_e^E$ term because the only excitation it receives in this analysis is $I_\text{ext}$ (the feedforward drive); the I cell has no $g_i^I$ because there is no I‚ÜíI coupling. The conductances $g_{i,j}^E, g_{e,k}^I$ are themselves driven by presynaptic spikes through first-order linear filters:

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_{e,k}^I \;=\; -g_{e,k}^I \;+\; \tau_\text{AMPA}\,\sum_j W^{EI}_{kj}\,s_j^E(t)
\tag{3}
$$

$$
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_{i,j}^E \;=\; -g_{i,j}^E \;+\; \tau_\text{GABA}\,\sum_k W^{IE}_{jk}\,s_k^I(t)
\tag{4}
$$

with $s_j^E(t) = \sum_n \delta(t - t_{j,n}^E)$ the spike train of E-cell $j$ as a sum of Dirac pulses at its firing times (similarly for $s_k^I$). (3)‚Äì(4) are the continuous-time forms of the synaptic conductance updates in [COBANet](/articles/ar003/) ‚Äî exponential synapses with per-spike weight $W$ and decay $\tau$.

**Step 2. Homogeneous coupling and population means.** Replace each random weight by its population mean, $W^{EI}_{kj} \to w^{EI}$ and $W^{IE}_{jk} \to w^{IE}$. The presynaptic sums in (3)‚Äì(4) become

$$
\sum_j W^{EI}_{kj}\,s_j^E \;\longrightarrow\; w^{EI} \sum_j s_j^E \;=\; w^{EI}\, N_E\, E(t)
\tag{5}
$$

$$
\sum_k W^{IE}_{jk}\,s_k^I \;\longrightarrow\; w^{IE} \sum_k s_k^I \;=\; w^{IE}\, N_I\, I(t)
\tag{6}
$$

introducing the *population-mean firing rates*

$$
E(t) \;\equiv\; \frac{1}{N_E}\sum_{j=1}^{N_E} s_j^E(t),
\qquad
I(t) \;\equiv\; \frac{1}{N_I}\sum_{k=1}^{N_I} s_k^I(t).
\tag{7}
$$

A *smooth-rate ansatz* (short-window averaging) treats $E(t), I(t)$ as continuous. This drops weight heterogeneity and **finite-size noise** ‚Äî the shot noise $\mathrm{Var}[E(t)] \propto E(t)/N_E$ that vanishes only as $N_E, N_I \to \infty$. At finite $N_E = 1024, N_I = 256$ the residual $O(N^{-1/2})$ fluctuations smear the Hopf onset and sustain a weak noisy gamma below threshold, both seen in the spiking simulations.

With no cell index left, every E cell sees the same $g_i^E$ and every I cell the same $g_e^I$, collapsing the per-cell conductances to population means. Defining lumped couplings

$$
\tilde W^{EI} \;\equiv\; w^{EI} N_E, \qquad \tilde W^{IE} \;\equiv\; w^{IE} N_I
\tag{8}
$$

the conductance dynamics become

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I \;=\; -g_e^I \;+\; \tilde W^{EI}\,E
\tag{9}
$$

$$
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E \;=\; -g_i^E \;+\; \tilde W^{IE}\,I
\tag{10}
$$

Two equations, down from $N_E + N_I$. (The fan-in scale $\tilde W$ folds into $W^{EI}, W^{IE}$ in Step 5.)

*Running system, end of Step 2 ‚Äî conductances are now two population means; the membrane is still per-cell but sees those means:*

$$
\begin{aligned}
C_m^E\,\dot V_j^E &= -g_L^E(V_j^E - E_L) \;-\; g_i^E\,(V_j^E - E_I) \;+\; I_\text{ext} \\
C_m^I\,\dot V_k^I &= -g_L^I(V_k^I - E_L) \;-\; g_e^I\,(V_k^I - E_E) \\
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I &= -g_e^I \;+\; \tilde W^{EI}\,E \\
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E &= -g_i^E \;+\; \tilde W^{IE}\,I
\end{aligned}
$$

**Step 3. Driving-force linearisation.** In COBA the synaptic current is conductance times a *driving force*, $-g\,(V - E_\text{rev})$ ‚Äî a product of two moving quantities ($g$ and $V$) that makes the model nonlinear and ties the two together. Cut the knot by freezing $V$ at its resting value $V_\text{rest} = E_L = -65$ mV *inside the driving force only* ‚Äî the leak and spike threshold keep their full $V$-dependence, handled by the f-I curve in Step 4. With the reversal potentials from [COBANet](/articles/ar003/), each driving force becomes a fixed voltage gap:

$$
\Delta V_\text{inh} \;\equiv\; V_\text{rest} - E_I \;=\; -65 - (-80) \;=\; 15~\text{mV}
\tag{11}
$$

$$
\Delta V_\text{exc} \;\equiv\; V_\text{rest} - E_E \;=\; -65 - 0 \;=\; -65~\text{mV}
$$

The synaptic currents in (1)‚Äì(2) then lose their $V$-dependence and become proportional to conductance alone:

$$
-g_i^E (V_j^E - E_I) \;\approx\; -g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh}
\tag{12}
$$

$$
-g_e^I (V_k^I - E_E) \;\approx\; -g_e^I\,\Delta V_\text{exc} \;=\; +g_e^I \cdot |E_E - V_\text{rest}|
\tag{13}
$$

‚Äî inhibition pulls $V$ down ($\Delta V_\text{inh} = +15$ mV), excitation pushes it up ($|\Delta V_\text{exc}| = 65$ mV). Removing the multiplicative coupling between $g$ and $V$ is what reduces COBA to a current-based (CUBA) form. The price is **shunting**: with $V$ fixed we ignore that conductance also lowers the membrane's effective time constant ($\tau_\text{eff} = C_m / g_\text{tot}$) ‚Äî the loss the table records.

*Running system, end of Step 3 ‚Äî the synaptic currents are now linear in conductance (no $V$ left in the driving force):*

$$
\begin{aligned}
C_m^E\,\dot V_j^E &= -g_L^E(V_j^E - E_L) \;-\; g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh} \;+\; I_\text{ext} \\
C_m^I\,\dot V_k^I &= -g_L^I(V_k^I - E_L) \;+\; g_e^I\,|\Delta V_\text{exc}| \\
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I &= -g_e^I \;+\; \tilde W^{EI}\,E \\
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E &= -g_i^E \;+\; \tilde W^{IE}\,I
\end{aligned}
$$

**Step 4. Population rate from an f-I curve.** Under (11)‚Äì(13) the membrane equations (1)‚Äì(2) have the form $C_m \dot V = -g_L (V - E_L) + I_\text{syn}$ ‚Äî exactly LIF with a synaptic current. A LIF cell receiving constant net input current $I$ fires at rate $\phi(I)$ where $\phi$ is its f-I curve. Replacing each cell's spike output by its rate response gives

$$
E(t) \;\approx\; \phi_E\!\big(I_\text{eff}^E(t)\big), \qquad
I(t) \;\approx\; \phi_I\!\big(I_\text{eff}^I(t)\big)
\tag{14}
$$

with effective input currents (from (1)‚Äì(2) with (11)‚Äì(13) substituted)

$$
I_\text{eff}^E(t) \;=\; I_\text{ext}(t) \;-\; g_i^E(t)\,\Delta V_\text{inh}
\tag{15}
$$

$$
I_\text{eff}^I(t) \;=\; g_e^I(t)\,|\Delta V_\text{exc}|
\tag{16}
$$

(I cells receive only excitation in this architecture; E cells receive external drive minus the GABA shunt.) The instantaneous-rate replacement (14) is only valid when input changes are slow compared to the membrane integration time. In reality, $E(t)$ relaxes toward the f-I-curve fixed point on its membrane time constant $\tau_E$. Encode this with an explicit relaxation:

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E \;=\; -E \;+\; \Phi_E\!\big(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh}\big)
\tag{17}
$$

$$
\tau_I\,\dot I \;=\; -I \;+\; \Phi_I\!\big(g_e^I\,|\Delta V_\text{exc}|\big)
\tag{18}
$$

where $\Phi_E, \Phi_I$ are the smooth steady-state gain functions (sigmoids in the numerics). Two more equations down ‚Äî together with (9)‚Äì(10), four equations in $(E, I, g_e^I, g_i^E)$.

*Running system, end of Step 4 ‚Äî a closed 4D rate model in $(E, I, g_e^I, g_i^E)$, constants not yet absorbed:*

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tau_E\,\dot E &= -E \;+\; \Phi_E\!\big(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E\,\Delta V_\text{inh}\big) \\
\tau_I\,\dot I &= -I \;+\; \Phi_I\!\big(g_e^I\,|\Delta V_\text{exc}|\big) \\
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I &= -g_e^I \;+\; \tilde W^{EI}\,E \\
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E &= -g_i^E \;+\; \tilde W^{IE}\,I
\end{aligned}
$$

**Step 5. Absorb the driving-force constants.** The fixed prefactors $\Delta V_\text{inh}, |\Delta V_\text{exc}|$ in (17)‚Äì(18) and the fan-in scalings in (9)‚Äì(10) are just multiplicative constants ‚Äî they don't carry dynamical information. Fold them into the couplings:

$$
W^{EI} \;\equiv\; \tilde W^{EI} \cdot |\Delta V_\text{exc}|, \qquad
W^{IE} \;\equiv\; \tilde W^{IE} \cdot \Delta V_\text{inh}
\tag{19}
$$

and likewise absorb the $|\Delta V_\text{exc}|$ inside the I-cell argument by redefining $g_e^I \mapsto g_e^I \cdot |\Delta V_\text{exc}|$ (similarly for $g_i^E$). The conductances now have units of input current rather than ¬µS; the f-I curves take their argument directly. This change of variables is pure bookkeeping ‚Äî no dynamics are lost.

#### Approximations Summary

The four equations after (9)‚Äì(10) + (17)‚Äì(18) + (19) are exactly the 4D system shown next.

| Step | Approximation | What's lost |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | homogeneous coupling, smooth-rate density | weight heterogeneity, finite-size noise |
| 3 | $V \approx V_\text{rest}$ in driving force | shunting, threshold-crossing nonlinearity |
| 4 | single relaxational mode at $\tau_E, \tau_I$ | sub-membrane dynamics, refractoriness |
| 5 | absorb constants into $W$ and $g$ | nothing (bookkeeping only) |

#### The 4D system

After Steps 1‚Äì5, the mean-field equations are

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E(I_\text{ext} - g_i^E), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I(g_e^I) \tag{20}
$$

$$
\tau_\text{AMPA}\,\dot g_e^I = -g_e^I + W^{EI}\,E, \qquad
\tau_\text{GABA}\,\dot g_i^E = -g_i^E + W^{IE}\,I \tag{21}
$$

in state $(E, I, g_e^I, g_i^E)$. Whether 4D is *minimal* ‚Äî or a 2D reduction would do ‚Äî is settled in [Rejection of 2D models](#rejection-of-2d-models) below.

#### 4D Jacobian

At a fixed point $(E^\star, I^\star, g_e^{I\star}, g_i^{E\star})$:

$$
J = \begin{pmatrix}
-1/\tau_E & 0 & 0 & -\Phi'_E/\tau_E \\
0 & -1/\tau_I & \Phi'_I/\tau_I & 0 \\
W^{EI}/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{AMPA} & 0 \\
0 & W^{IE}/\tau_\text{GABA} & 0 & -1/\tau_\text{GABA}
\end{pmatrix} \tag{22}
$$

with $\Phi'_E, \Phi'_I$ evaluated at the fixed-point arguments.

### Rejection of 2D models

The textbook tool for an E/I bifurcation is a 2D Wilson-Cowan rate model ‚Äî two rates $(E, I)$ with sigmoid gains and instantaneous coupling. With no recurrent E‚ÜíE or I‚ÜíI synapse ($W^{EE} = W^{II} = 0$) it reads

$$
\tau_E\,\dot E = -E + \Phi_E\!\left(I_\text{ext} - W^{IE} I\right), \qquad
\tau_I\,\dot I = -I + \Phi_I\!\left(W^{EI} E\right). \tag{23}
$$

This is exactly the $\tau_\text{AMPA}, \tau_\text{GABA} \to 0$ limit of the 4D system (20)‚Äì(21). As the synaptic time constants vanish the conductance equations lose their derivatives and go algebraic, $g_e^I = W^{EI} E$ and $g_i^E = W^{IE} I$ ‚Äî the synapses slaved instantaneously to the rates ‚Äî and substituting these into the rate equations (20) recovers (23) term for term. So Wilson-Cowan *is* the 4D model with its synaptic clock zeroed out: a legitimate reduction only if the synapses relax fast relative to the rhythm, which they do not ($\tau_\text{GABA} \approx 9$ ms is the same order as the gamma period).

It cannot host the rhythm. Its divergence is pinned to the negative constant $-1/\tau_E - 1/\tau_I$ ‚Äî with no diagonal recurrence the gain $\Phi'$ multiplies only the off-diagonal cross-terms ‚Äî so by the Bendixson‚ÄìDulac theorem it encloses no periodic orbit for *any* drive, coupling, or gain shape: not merely no Hopf, but no oscillation anywhere in phase space. What it discards is the round-trip synaptic delay (E excites I after $\tau_\text{AMPA}$, I shunts E after $\tau_\text{GABA}$), and that lag is exactly what destabilises the silent state into a rhythm ‚Äî inhibition that tracks $E$ with zero delay can only damp, never overshoot. Holding it is irreducibly ‚â• 4D, which is why the analysis stays in the conductance model above; any 2D rate model that *does* oscillate has imported a destabiliser PING lacks (recurrent excitation in Wilson-Cowan, a cubic self-gain in FitzHugh-Nagumo), reproducing the rhythm through a fictional mechanism. Figure 1 makes the contrast concrete.

### Locating the Hopf

The recruitment cliff is a **Hopf bifurcation** of the silent fixed point ‚Äî the smallest drive $I_\text{ext}^\star$ at which a complex-conjugate eigenvalue pair of the $4\times4$ Jacobian (22) reaches the imaginary axis ($\mathrm{Re}(\lambda) = 0$, $\mathrm{Im}(\lambda) \neq 0$), so the fixed point loses stability to a *growing oscillation* rather than a static shift. To find it, sweep $I_\text{ext}$, track the fixed point (near-silent until the loop engages), and diagonalise $J$ at each step; $I_\text{ext}^\star$ is where the leading pair crosses. Tracking all four eigenvalues certifies the onset: if exactly one complex pair reaches the axis while the others stay well damped this is a *simple, generic* Hopf ‚Äî not a double-Hopf (two pairs crossing together, which would spawn tori a linear analysis cannot capture) ‚Äî precisely the condition under which a local near-onset analysis is valid. Canonical parameters: $\tau_E = 20$, $\tau_I = 5$, $\tau_\text{AMPA} = 2$, $\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms; $W^{EI} = 80$, $W^{IE} = 60$.

### Calculating its frequency

At the crossing the eigenvalue pair is purely imaginary, $\lambda = \pm i\omega^\star$, and its imaginary part is the angular frequency of the nascent oscillation: the gamma frequency is $f^\star = \omega^\star / 2\pi$, read straight off the Jacobian at threshold. To test the mechanistic claim that the inhibitory decay sets the period, the Hopf is re-found at each $\tau_\text{GABA}$ of nb041's retrained sweep and the resulting $f^\star$ compared against the spiking network's measured $f_\gamma$.

### Classifying sub- or supercritical

Whether the limit cycle is born with zero amplitude (supercritical, soft onset) or with a finite jump and hysteresis (subcritical, hard onset) is classically the sign of the first Lyapunov coefficient of the Hopf normal form. Rather than compute it, we read it off the *full 4D field* by direct simulation: integrate (20)‚Äì(21) from a small perturbation of the fixed point across a grid of $I_\text{ext}$ straddling $I_\text{ext}^\star$ and measure the steady-state peak-to-peak amplitude $A = \max_t E(t) - \min_t E(t)$ ‚Äî exactly zero at a fixed point, positive the instant a limit cycle exists. The *shape* of $A(I_\text{ext})$ decides the class. Near the crossing the oscillation amplitude $r$ obeys a radial flow $\dot r = \lambda\,r + \ell_1\,r^3 + \dots$, with $\lambda \propto (I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star)$ the growth rate and $\ell_1$ the first Lyapunov coefficient; the limit cycle is the balance $r^2 = -\lambda/\ell_1$. A *stabilising* cubic ($\ell_1 < 0$) gives a real cycle $r \propto \sqrt{I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star}$, so $A^2 \propto (I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star)$ ‚Äî the supercritical signature; a *destabilising* cubic ($\ell_1 > 0$) gives no small stable cycle, so the amplitude appears with a jump and hysteresis.

## Results

### Frequency

Sweeping $I_\text{ext}$ and diagonalising $J$ at each step locates the Hopf at

$$
I_\text{ext}^\star = 1.30, \qquad \omega^\star = 0.154~\text{rad/ms}, \qquad f^\star = \frac{\omega^\star}{2\pi} \approx 24.5~\text{Hz},
$$

squarely in the PING gamma band. Exactly one complex pair crosses the imaginary axis while the others stay damped ‚Äî a simple Hopf (Figure 2). The absolute scale is not parameter-free (the mean-field's coupling units set it), but the *dependence* on the synaptic timescales is the predictive content: across a $\tau_\text{GABA}$ sweep $f^\star$ tracks the spiking network's gamma ‚Äî qualitatively, not quantitatively (Figure 3).

### Super or subcritical

Direct simulation of (20)‚Äì(21) gives an amplitude that grows continuously from zero above threshold, with $A^2 \propto (I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star)$ (slope $3.2 \times 10^{-4}$, $R^2 = 0.997$) and no jump or hysteresis ‚Äî a **supercritical Hopf**, the soft recruitment cliff of nb025 (Figure 4). Because $A \propto \sqrt{I_\text{ext} - I_\text{ext}^\star}$ the onset is soft but *sharp*: the sensitivity $dA/dI_\text{ext}$ diverges at threshold, so most of the amplitude appears in a thin sliver of drive just past $I_\text{ext}^\star$.
