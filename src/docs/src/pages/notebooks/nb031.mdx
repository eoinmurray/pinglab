## Introduction

[030 — COBA + NMDA cannot sustain post-stimulus activity](/notebooks/nb030/) showed COBA is structurally bistable: decay or seizure, no in-between. With ping (inhibition on) a sustained attractor exists — but where exactly is the boundary? This entry zooms in on the lower edge of ping's sustained band by fixing the input and sweeping $W_{ee}$ in fine steps from 0 to 0.1.

## Method

Untrained PING-config COBANet (*ei_strength = 1*), $N_E = ${cfg.n_e}, slow channel on at gain {cfg.slow_syn_gain}. **Input is fixed at MNIST digit {cfg.digit_class} (sample 0), pixel-weighted Poisson-encoded** on $N_{IN} = 784$ channels at peak rate $R = ${cfg.input_rate_hz} Hz during $[0, ${cfg.t_stim_ms}]$ ms, then silence to ${cfg.t_total_ms} ms.

**Sweep.** $W_{ee}$ mean over $\{${cfg.w_ee_grid?.join(", ")}\}$ μS (pre-fan-in); all other parameters held constant.

**Outcome classification** (same as [nb030](/notebooks/nb030/)):

- *seizure* — late-window rate $r_2 \geq 120$ Hz.
- *decay* — $r_2 < 1$ Hz or still actively declining ($r_2 / r_1 < 0.5$).
- *sustained* — anywhere else: stable late-window rate.

## Discussion

The decay → sustained transition is gradual rather than sharp. From $W_{ee} = 0.00$ to $0.06$ the late-window rate climbs monotonically (0.7 → 0.5 → 1.1 → 3.8 Hz), with the slope-aware classifier still calling everything decay because the trace is *still falling* at the end of the trial. At $W_{ee} = 0.08$ the trace flattens, and the classifier flips to sustained. The boundary is a continuous one — somewhere between $0.06$ and $0.08$ the recurrent excitation crosses the threshold where the slow channel can hold a steady-state non-zero level against the inhibitory + leak losses.

The settled rate at the bottom of the sustained band is low — 5–9 Hz at $W_{ee} \in \{0.08, 0.10\}$, well below the gamma-locked $\sim 90$ Hz attractor at $W_{ee} = 0.5$. So the sustained band has internal structure: a low-rate edge at small $W_{ee}$ where the attractor barely exists, and a high-rate gamma regime at larger $W_{ee}$. Following the boundary further (e.g. a $W_{ee}$ sweep from $0.10 \to 0.50$ at the same input) would map this internal gradient.

## Next steps

- **Continue the sweep upward.** $W_{ee} \in [0.10, 0.50]$ at fixed input rate — does the settled rate climb continuously, or jump?
- **Multi-seed at the boundary.** Run $W_{ee} \in \{0.06, 0.07, 0.08\}$ with 10+ seeds to see if the decay → sustained transition is seed-stochastic at the boundary.
- **Decay-time fits.** For the decay panels, fit an exponential to the post-stim trace and check whether the time constant matches $\tau_{NMDA}$ or differs by $W_{ee}$.
