## Introduction

[030 — Coba + NMDA cannot sustain post-stimulus activity](/notebooks/nb030/) showed the inhibitory loop is structurally required for stable post-stim activity. [031 — Ping $W_{ee}$ transition at fixed input](/notebooks/nb031/) located the lower edge of ping's sustained band at $W_{ee} \approx 0.07$, and the gamma-locked attractor regime at $W_{ee} \approx 0.5$. Together those notebooks said: *the operating regime DMTS needs exists in ping with NMDA*.

The natural follow-up — and the prediction I made at the close of nb031 — is that adding *--trainable-w-ee* on top should be enough to actually train DMTS, because gradient descent can move $W_{ee}$ to wherever it needs to be inside the sustained band. This entry runs that experiment.

**Result: it doesn't train.** Same chance plateau the nb028/29 chain hit. Gradient signal flows, but the optimizer lands in a local minimum where the readout outputs uniform logits and the loss bottoms out at $\ln 2$. The "trainable $W_{ee}$ solves it" prediction is empirically wrong at this recipe.

## Method

**Task: DMTS.** Each trial is $T_s + T_d + T_p$ = {cfg.t_sample_ms} + {cfg.t_delay_ms} + {cfg.t_probe_ms} ms ({cfg.t_sample_ms + cfg.t_delay_ms + cfg.t_probe_ms} ms total).

1. Sample window: digit_A from MNIST, Poisson-encoded at peak rate {cfg.stim_peak_rate_hz} Hz.
2. Delay window: input is identically zero.
3. Probe window: digit_B from MNIST, same encoding.
4. Target: 1 if digit_A == digit_B (match), 0 otherwise.

Batches are balanced 50/50 match vs non-match.

**Network.** ping COBANet ($\text{ei\_strength} = ${cfg.ei_strength}), $N_E = ${cfg.n_e}, $N_{IN} = 784$.

- *--trainable-w-ee* on; $W_{ee}$ initialised at ({cfg.w_ee_init_mean}, {cfg.w_ee_init_std}) μS.
- *--slow-syn* on at gain {cfg.slow_syn_gain} ($\tau_{NMDA} = 100$ ms).
- *--sgcc* on at $\alpha = ${cfg.sgcc_alpha} — the principled gradient stabilizer from [nb027](/notebooks/nb027/).
- *--ei-strength = 1*; $W_{ei}$, $W_{ie}$ at ping defaults.

**Readout.** Custom $\mathrm{Linear}(N_E \to 2)$ on the probe-window mean E spike count, bypassing the built-in mem-mean readout. This is deliberate: integrating over the whole trial (sample + delay + probe) dilutes the gradient by averaging in the long silent delay window. Only what happens during the probe contributes to the loss.

**Training.** Adam, lr = {cfg.lr}, batch = {cfg.batch_size}, {cfg.epochs} epochs at {cfg.n_train} training pairs. Per-step gradient clip at 1.0. Test set is {cfg.n_test} held-out pairs.

## Discussion

What this notebook actually shows is that the prediction at the end of nb031 — "trainable $W_{ee}$ + SGCC + NMDA + ping should be enough; the operating regime exists, training will find it" — is empirically wrong at the recipe tried here. The structural availability of a sustained attractor is necessary but not sufficient.

**The diagnostic we get from gradient norms.** Both $W_{ee}$ and $W_{out}$ receive non-zero gradient throughout training; this isn't a NaN or vanishing-gradient story. The optimizer is moving, just in a direction that converges to the uniform-output minimum: a readout that outputs $\sim$50/50 logits regardless of the input. From a loss-landscape view that's a stable basin — uniform output gives constant loss $\ln 2$ on any balanced batch, with no first-order incentive to move in any particular direction.

**Why this is the same failure as nb028/29.** Both runs of the predecessor chain ([nb028](https://...), [nb029](https://...) — both since nuked) hit identical chance plateaus. The conclusion documented at the time was that the issue is **cold-start binary credit assignment**: with a randomly-initialised hidden representation that doesn't yet encode sample-vs-probe discrimination, the per-sample gradient on $W_{out}$ averages to near-zero across balanced match/non-match batches. I claimed the trainable $W_{ee}$ would break this by making the hidden representation a *learned* function of the input — varying sample-to-sample even if mean-balanced. The empirical result here says: gradient on $W_{ee}$ exists and is non-zero, but it isn't large enough or directed enough to escape the uniform-readout basin within the training budget tried.

**What the network IS learning.** Looking at the gradient time-series, $|W_{ee}.\text{grad}|$ collapses by ~100× between epoch 1 and epoch 2 (from $\sim 8 \times 10^{-4}$ down to $\sim 10^{-5}$), while $|W_{out}.\text{grad}|$ stays in the $10^{-3}$ range. This is the signature of the optimizer reaching a flat region in $W_{ee}$ space while the readout continues to wiggle in a basin around uniform output. Effectively, the network freezes its dynamics early and the readout never finds a discriminative direction.

## Implication

DMTS in this codebase, at this scale, with this readout/loss combination, is still unsolved. The deeper architectural problem identified by nb028/29 — that the loss landscape has a strong uniform-output basin that gradient descent falls into before it can find the discriminative basin — wasn't dissolved by enabling $W_{ee}$ learning. Trainable $W_{ee}$ adds capacity but not direction.

## Next steps (concrete, not vague)

- **Warm-start from MNIST classifier weights.** Train coba+SGCC on regular MNIST classification to a working hidden representation (this is nb027's recipe), then transplant the hidden-side weights into the DMTS net with a fresh 2-class readout. The hidden state at probe time would then encode something *useful* about each digit out of the box; the readout's per-sample gradient should escape the uniform-output basin because samples differ at the hidden layer from the very first batch.
- **Contrastive / triplet loss instead of binary CE.** A pair-based loss produces a non-zero per-sample gradient even on balanced batches because it compares each sample to a negative pair rather than to a single label. Removes the structural cancellation that produces the $\ln 2$ basin.
- **Multi-way ($N > 2$) match task.** Instead of binary "is this a match", do "which of 4 candidates matches the sample?". Multi-class CE gives the per-sample gradient the same per-sample directional variance that made [nb027](/notebooks/nb027/) work on 10-class MNIST.

Three independent attacks on the same structural problem. Any one of them is a separate notebook.
