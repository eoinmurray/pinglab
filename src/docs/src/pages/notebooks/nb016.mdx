## Introduction

External drive is one knob ([nb003](/notebooks/nb003/)); internal E→I→E coupling strength is the other. With the stim-window overdrive pinned at {scan.fixed_overdrive}× (flat input through the trial), this notebook sweeps the E→I coupling strength — walking the network from the async baseline (E and I effectively decoupled) through the emergence of gamma as the feedback loop closes — and tries to extract a single-frame scalar metric from the resulting trace. Two views are kept in parallel: the population firing rate (the raw signal we look at) and the autocorrelation peak (a candidate scalar score). Input rate and $W_\text{in}$ are bumped relative to [nb003](/notebooks/nb003/) / [nb005](/notebooks/nb005/) so E has enough baseline drive to recruit I at all without a stim pulse. See [The Notebooks](/docs/the-notebooks/) for how this entry's runner/artifact/figure triple is wired up.

### Population rate

For each frame of the video we replay the same Poisson input through a fresh ping network at that frame's ei value, record the E hidden-layer spikes, and bin them at 2 ms to recover a smoothed population rate (Hz, averaged over all $N_E$ neurons). The first {numbers.autocorr_metric?.onset_skip_ms ?? "20"} ms onset transient is discarded for the downstream metrics; the full trace is shown in the stacked plot.

### Autocorrelation

The candidate single-frame metric is the **autocorrelation peak** at non-zero lag — async noise has none, a periodic burst train has a tall, sharp one at the gamma period. We take the post-onset rate (after the {numbers.autocorr_metric?.onset_skip_ms ?? "20"} ms transient), z-score it, autocorrelate via FFT, and normalise so lag 0 = 1. The metric is the prominence of the strongest peak in the {numbers.autocorr_metric?.lag_min_ms ?? "5"}–{numbers.autocorr_metric?.lag_max_ms ?? "60"} ms window (peak height minus its nearest valley). No shuffles required.

### Autocorrelation

<Figure id="Figure 3" title="per-frame autocorrelation" src="/figures/notebooks/nb016/autocorr_stack.png" alt="Stacked autocorrelation curves of the post-onset E rate, one row per ei value sampled across the sweep.">
  Per-frame autocorrelation of the post-onset E rate. One row per ei value (10 sampled across the sweep). At low ei the curve decays straight to zero; from the threshold up, a periodic side-peak appears at the gamma lag and stays. The lag of the first side-peak shifts from $\sim$14 ms ($\sim$70 Hz) at threshold to $\sim$20 ms ($\sim$50 Hz) at ei = 1, the canonical PING slowing-with-coupling-strength.
</Figure>

Lifts off near the threshold, but a finite-sample noise floor of $\sim$0.4 sits in the async regime — `find_peaks` always picks the deepest noise excursion as a "peak", and on a 115-sample window that excursion has positive expectation.
