## Introduction

[025 — slow synapse](/notebooks/nb025/) added an NMDA-like long-decay excitatory channel on the *input* side of E cells, then tried to train coba on DMTS. The slow synapse fixed the information pathology — the network has a usable long-timescale variable for the first time — but DMTS still couldn't escape the chance plateau because the gradient pathology was orthogonal: BPTT through a long-trial binary classification with vanilla Adam is unstable at any LR strong enough to break logit symmetry.

This entry tries the LSNN paper's mechanism instead: **adaptive LIF (ALIF)**. A per-neuron slow variable $a_t$ rises with each spike and raises that neuron's own threshold:

$$
a_{t+1} = e^{-\Delta t/\tau_a}\, a_t + s_t, \qquad V_\text{th,eff} = V_\text{th} + \beta a_t
$$

Different memory mechanism (output-side, self-suppressive) than slow synapse (input-side, additive). The LSNN paper (Bellec et al. 2018) showed reliable DMTS training with this substrate at standard hyperparameters — so the test is whether plugging ALIF into our coba pipeline lets DMTS train where slow synapse couldn't.

The new oscilloscope flags: *--alif* (boolean), *--tau-adapt* (default 700 ms), *--alif-beta* (default 1.7 mV per spike).

## Discussion

The DMTS attempt at small tier sat flat at chance for five epochs with no NaN. Extending to medium tier (LSNN's actual setup: lr=10⁻³, batch=256, no v-grad-dampen, gradient clip 0.5) then revealed the real obstruction: with the v-grad dampener turned down to let BPTT signal through, **137 of 140 training batches produced non-finite gradients**. We characterised the dampening/learning space deliberately:

| `v_grad_dampen` | Medium-tier outcome |
|---|---|
| 1 (off) | NaN gradient on essentially every batch — 98% of updates skipped |
| 3 | Stable, no learning (loss stays at $\ln 2$) |
| 10 | Stable, no learning |
| 1000 (nb024 default) | Stable, learns MNIST but not DMTS |

This makes the structural issue explicit. The conductance synaptic term

$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial g_e} \left[g_e (V - E_e)\right] = V - E_e
$$

couples voltage and conductance multiplicatively. BPTT through ~1500 timesteps of this multiplication produces gradient magnitudes that explode whenever any cell's $V$ is near $E_e$ — which is essentially always for an active network. The dampener can mute the explosion but it mutes the *signal* by the same factor (since both flow through the same Jacobian path), so there is no setting that lets DMTS-relevant gradient through without also letting the explosion through.

**Together with [nb025's slow-synapse failure](/notebooks/nb025/), this localises the actual blocker:** COBA biophysics has a gradient pathology that's orthogonal to the choice of memory mechanism. Slow synapse and ALIF *both* give the network the long-timescale variable working memory needs, but neither helps because the bottleneck is the BPTT pipeline, not the architecture's representational capacity. The LSNN paper trains DMTS reliably on **CUBA** neurons; their additive coupling has no multiplicative voltage–conductance Jacobian, which is why their vanilla-Adam recipe works there and our adapted version fails here.

## What we learned

- ALIF mechanism is implemented and works mechanically (Cell 1).
- A second memory substrate, parallel to nb025's slow synapse, produces the same DMTS failure.
- The dampening/training space has been mapped — there is no Goldilocks setting at our scale.
- The real architectural barrier is the conductance synapse's multiplicative Jacobian, not anything about the memory mechanism.

## Next steps

The natural next experiment is a **CUBA + ALIF + trainable W_rec** replica of LSNN proper — same neuron substrate Bellec et al. used. CUBA's additive synaptic coupling avoids the explosion entirely. Implementing this would mean adding ALIF support to CUBANet (currently COBA-only) and a fresh notebook (nb027) following LSNN's exact recipe (lr=10⁻³, surrogate slope ≈ 0.3, batch=256, no dampener). If that *does* train DMTS while our COBA versions don't, the conclusion will be specific and actionable: working-memory tasks belong on CUBA rungs, not COBA.
