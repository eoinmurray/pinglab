## Method

Every hyperparameter is a hardcoded literal in *src/pinglab/notebooks/nb009.py* per the [runner contract](/the-notebooks/#runner-contract); the runner shells out to the shared [oscilloscope *train*](/the-oscilloscope/#subcommand-train) path. The full recipe is the table below — parameters flagged as customised in the **Default?** column are explained underneath.

<RunParameters numbers={numbers} variant="recipe" extras={[
  { label: "Readout", value: "li", default: "rate" },
  { label: "Surrogate slope", value: 1, default: 5 },
  { label: "Init", value: "Kaiming (signed)", default: "non-negative" },
]} />

These three customisations mirror [nb006](/notebooks/nb006/)/[nb008](/notebooks/nb008/) verbatim. *cuba* relies on its integrator-level $(1-\beta)/\Delta t$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors for *dt*-invariance — no per-init compensation needed.

- [**readout = li**](/the-oscilloscope/#readouts) — the default *rate* readout accumulates hidden spikes across *T* = 2000 steps and produces logits large enough that CE saturates to zero on the forward pass, masking the real gradient signal.
- [**surrogate-slope = 1**](/the-oscilloscope/#network-flags-shared) — the global default slope = 5 × 2000 timesteps of BPTT overflows fp32 in the *W_ff* input-layer gradient. Slope = 1 bounds the fast-sigmoid pseudo-derivative so grads stay finite end-to-end.
- [**kaiming-init (signed)**](/the-oscilloscope/#network-flags-shared) — signed Kaiming on *W_in* gives the hidden layer enough starting drive to spike at all; without it the default non-negative init leaves *rate_e* ≈ 0.

## Next steps

Every check passes at large tier — best accuracy {cell.best_acc ?? "?"}%, final {cell.final_acc ?? "?"}%, and crucially *rate_e* = 104 Hz lands inside the 1–200 Hz sanity band. *cuba* is the first model on this shared MNIST recipe without the saturation pathology that pushes [nb006](/notebooks/nb006/) (rate ≈ 284 Hz) and [nb008](/notebooks/nb008/) (rate ≈ 390 Hz) above the upper rail at matched config. The exp-Euler+ZOH integrator's $(1-\beta)/\Delta t$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors keep starting drive matched to *standard-snn* at *dt* = 0.1 ms while the exponential synapse low-passes the spike train into the readout, so the network can hit competitive accuracy without driving the hidden layer into rate saturation.

Parked follow-up: the same two-sided firing-rate regulariser proposed for nb006/nb008 (Cramer-2022 style via [*--fr-reg-upper-strength* / *--fr-reg-upper-theta*](/the-oscilloscope/#network-flags-shared)) should be a no-op here — applying it to *cuba* and confirming rate stays inside the band would cheaply verify the regulariser is well-posed before turning it loose on the saturating siblings.
