## Introduction

PING (pyramidal-interneuron gamma) is the gamma-band oscillation produced by an excitatory population $E$ feeding an inhibitory population $I$ which in turn feeds back onto $E$. The standard story:

1. The E population starts firing.
2. After an AMPA-delay ($\tau_{\text{AMPA}} \approx 2$ ms), the I population spikes.
3. GABA-A inhibition silences E for the GABA-decay time ($\tau_{\text{GABA}} \approx 9$ ms).
4. Inhibition decays away; E refires.

The interspike-interval comes out around $\tau_{\text{GABA}} + \tau_{\text{AMPA}} \sim 11$ ms, giving a fundamental near $\sim 30$ Hz — the gamma band.

This entry strips PING down to its biophysical fundamentals and characterises it *in isolation from any task*. No training, no readout, no loss — just a free-running network driven by Poisson input. Goal: see the rhythm emerge, measure its frequency, and trace how each piece of the recipe (E-I coupling strength, AMPA / GABA decay constants, refractory period) shapes the gamma peak.

The full conductance-based model that produces PING is documented in [The LIF Neuron](/articles/art002/#conductance-based-synapses-coba) and on the [pinglab models page](/docs/models/#cobanet); this notebook is the empirical companion piece.
