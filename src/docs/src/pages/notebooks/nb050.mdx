## Abstract

Vreeswijk & Sompolinsky's 1996 *Science* paper showed that a sparsely-connected network of excitatory and inhibitory cells with strong recurrent weights settles, generically, into an *asynchronous irregular* (AI) state — both populations firing at moderate rates, individual ISIs close to Poisson (CV ≈ 1), low pairwise correlations across cells, and deterministic chaos (positive Lyapunov exponent). The mean recurrent inputs from E and I are individually large but cancel; what's left is fluctuations that drive each cell across threshold by noise, not by deterministic integration. This entry asks whether the same network that produces canonical PING in the gamma-gated-sparsity collection can also reach AI, and finds that it can: with fixed-fan-in sparse connectivity, per-cell independent external drive on both populations, and tuned recurrent weights, we land at $\langle r_E\rangle = 17$, $\langle r_I\rangle = 21$ Hz, per-E ISI CV = 1.02, per-I ISI CV = 1.13, a broadband PSD with no gamma peak, near-zero pairwise correlations, and a positive Lyapunov exponent. Side-by-side with canonical PING (sharp 31 Hz peak, per-E CV 0.62, per-I CV 0.03, perturbations re-locked), the AI cell is a qualitatively distinct dynamical regime of the *same architecture*, selected by operating point alone.

## Methods

### The V&S asynchronous-irregular state

The original 1996 model was a binary-spin network: $N$ units, each in state 0 or 1, updated asynchronously. Each unit received sparse random recurrent input from $K \ll N$ presynaptic units, plus an external excitatory drive. The crucial scaling ansatz: synaptic strengths $J \propto 1/\sqrt{K}$ and external drive $\propto \sqrt{K}$. Under that scaling, mean recurrent E and I inputs into each cell are each $O(\sqrt{K})$, large, *opposite-signed*, and self-organise to cancel; the residual is fluctuations of order $O(1)$ around the cancelled mean. With cells sitting near threshold and noise of comparable scale, the dynamics become:

- **Asynchronous** — pairwise spike-train cross-correlation across cells stays $O(1/\sqrt{K})$, vanishing in the large-$K$ limit.
- **Irregular** — each cell's ISI distribution approaches Poisson, CV → 1.
- **Rate-balanced** — mean rates are set by the balance condition, not by the bare external drive vs threshold.
- **Chaotic** — positive maximum Lyapunov exponent; small perturbations decorrelate within one membrane time constant.

Brunel 2000 reproduced the regime with LIF neurons instead of binary spins. Renart et al. 2010 reproduced it with conductance-based synapses. The state is robust across these substrates — it depends on the balance ansatz, not on the specific single-cell model.

### Our substrate

Conductance-based LIF, two populations $N_E = 1024, N_I = 256$, recurrent matrices $W^{EE}, W^{EI}, W^{IE}, W^{II}$ between them. The same network class used throughout the gamma-gated-sparsity collection ([ar003](/articles/ar003/)) for PING. To reach the V&S regime we needed four ingredients that the PING runs don't use:

- **An $I \to I$ matrix.** Canonical PING has none; V&S requires it to break the synchronous I-burst that would otherwise re-establish the cycle.
- **Fixed-fan-in sparse connectivity.** Every post cell draws exactly $K = \text{round}((1 - \text{sparsity})\,N_\text{pre}) \approx 10$ random presynaptic inputs in each recurrent matrix. This is V&S's exact-$K$ random connectivity — *not* per-entry Bernoulli sparsity, which gives a binomial fan-in that varies cell to cell (mean 10 but std ≈ 3.3, range 2–21) and broadens the rate distribution. Fixing $K$ holds every cell statistically equivalent: it cut the per-cell rate dispersion from CV $= 0.79$ (Bernoulli) to $0.33$ (exact-$K$).
- **Per-E-cell independent external drive.** Each E cell receives its own uncorrelated Poisson stream, applied directly to its $g_E$, bypassing the feedforward $W_\text{in}$ matrix. This is the per-cell uncorrelated noise V&S takes as input.
- **Per-I-cell independent external drive.** Same for the I population. Without it, I-cell membrane fluctuations are entirely E-correlated through $W^{EI}$ and per-I CV stalls below 1.

Both cells run on the same COBANet ($N_E = 1024, N_I = 256$, $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms, $T = 1000$ ms) and differ only in the recurrent matrices, the connectivity, and the external drive:

| Quantity | PING (canonical, control) | V&S AI |
|---|---|---|
| External drive on E | Poisson at 20 Hz, routed through $W_\text{in} = \mathcal{N}(1.5, 0.3)$ | per-cell independent Poisson at 45 Hz × $0.38$ μS, bypassing $W_\text{in}$ |
| External drive on I | none (only via $W^{EI}$ from E spikes) | per-cell independent Poisson at 8 Hz × $0.25$ μS |
| Recurrent connectivity | dense | fixed fan-in, exactly $K \approx 10$ inputs per post cell |
| $W^{EI}$ init | `ei-strength 1.5` (biophysical defaults) | $\mathcal{N}(0.6, 0.18)$ μS |
| $W^{IE}$ init | (same as $W^{EI}$) | $\mathcal{N}(3.0, 0.9)$ μS |
| $W^{II}$ init | $0$ (no $I \to I$) | $\mathcal{N}(0.4, 0.12)$ μS |
| Architectural class | conductance-based LIF, two populations | (same) |

The PING column is the canonical biophysical recipe from [nb023](/notebooks/nb023/). The AI column inherits the same network class and adds (i) the $W^{II}$ matrix, (ii) fixed-$K$ sparse recurrents, (iii) per-cell independent drive on both populations.

Each cell produces a five-panel figure: E raster, I raster, Welch PSD on the population-mean E trace (matching the [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) pipeline), per-E-neuron ISI-CV histogram with the Poisson reference at CV = 1, and the mean pairwise spike-train cross-correlogram on 100 random E-cell pairs.

### Fluctuation-vs-mean diagnostic

V&S says cells fire because input fluctuations are comparable to the gap between mean drive and threshold ($\eta = \sigma_I / |I_{th} - \mu_I| \gtrsim 1$). Computing per-cell mean and std of total membrane current $I_{tot}(t) = g_E(E_e - V) + g_I(E_i - V) + g_L(E_L - V)$ on the same trial:

### Chaos probe (Lyapunov)

The sharpest mechanistic test: clone the network, perturb every membrane voltage by ε = 2 mV at $t = 0$, rerun on *identical frozen input* (same Poisson drive, same seed), and track $D(t)$ = the number of E cells whose spike differs between the two copies. A chaotic network amplifies the perturbation ($D$ grows); a stable one re-absorbs it ($D \to 0$).

<Figure id="Figure 5" title="Spike-train divergence after an ε = 2 mV perturbation" src="/figures/notebooks/nb050/lyapunov_divergence.png" alt="Line plot of spike-train divergence D(t) (E cells differing) vs time since perturbation, 0 to 1000 ms. Grey trace (PING): an initial spike to ~7 cells, then a series of decaying pulses at the gamma period, monotonically shrinking and reaching exactly 0 by ~500 ms and staying there. Red trace (V&S AI): rises quickly from 0 to a sustained fluctuating level around 5-8 cells within ~100 ms, then stays there for the full 1000 ms, never returning to 0.">
  **PING re-locks; V&S AI does not.** PING (grey): the perturbation produces decaying gamma-locked pulses — each cycle the perturbed copy is briefly out of step, then the limit cycle pulls it back — and $D$ reaches exactly 0 by ≈ 500 ms. That's a stable attractor ($\lambda \leq 0$). V&S AI (red): $D$ rises to a sustained ≈ 6-cell divergence within ≈ 100 ms and stays there, never re-locking — the perturbation saturates rather than vanishing, the signature of a positive Lyapunov exponent.
</Figure>

A caveat on magnitude: the steady divergence is a handful of cells out of 1024, because the strong shared frozen input partially *entrains* both copies (the Mainen-Sejnowski reliability effect — strongly-driven cells fire reliably regardless of network state). So our AI cell is noise-entrained with chaos riding on top, rather than the strongly-autonomous-chaos regime of V&S's quenched-input network. But the *qualitative* distinction is unambiguous: PING's perturbation vanishes to exactly zero; AI's saturates at a sustained nonzero level.

## Discussion

Two regimes of the same architecture, separated by operating point alone. The biophysical substrate is rich enough to hold the loop together as a cycle clock (PING) and flexible enough to hold the cancelled-mean balance ansatz (AI). The matrices that gamma-lock in dense, strong-coupled territory dissolve into noise-driven crossings under sparse, balanced drive.

### How close are we to the canonical V&S state?

The two qualitative signatures that *define* AI in the V&S sense are reproduced cleanly:

- **CV → 1.** Both populations have per-neuron ISI CV indistinguishable from Poisson (0.97 and 1.08). The PING I-population CV of 0.03 makes the contrast vivid.
- **No spectral peak.** The Welch PSD is broadband — the cycle clock that drives PING is gone.

So at the level of *what the state looks like*, we have the V&S regime.

### What's different from the canonical setup

Three substrate differences, plus three *mechanistic* signatures now checked:

1. **Conductance-based LIF, not binary spins.** V&S 1996 used binary asynchronous-update units. Brunel 2000 and Renart 2010 ported the regime onto LIF and conductance-based synapses respectively; this work follows that route. The substrate change matters quantitatively (specific scaling constants, rate-vs-drive curves) but the regime itself is robust to it.
2. **Fixed-$K$ connectivity: matched to V&S.** We use exact-$K$ random connectivity (every post cell draws exactly $K$ presynaptic inputs), the V&S convention, rather than per-entry Bernoulli sparsity. The difference is not cosmetic: Bernoulli sparsity gives a binomial fan-in (std ≈ 3.3 on mean 10, range 2–21) that broadens the per-cell rate distribution to CV $= 0.79$; fixing $K$ tightens it to $0.33$, making the cells statistically equivalent as V&S assumes.
3. **Hand-tuned weights, not the $1/\sqrt{K}$ scaling ansatz.** V&S derive their weight scaling from the requirement that recurrent fluctuations stay $O(1)$ in the large-$N$ limit. We picked weights by tuning on the diagnostic. The numerical result is in the right regime, but we haven't *verified* that the recurrent mean drives are individually $O(\sqrt{K})$-large and self-cancelling — that diagnostic would be a follow-up.
4. **Pairwise correlations: confirmed AI-like.** The mean cross-correlogram on 100 random E-cell pairs gives peak |C| = 0.010 for the AI cell vs 0.041 for PING — a 4× separation, and the PING value is structured (gamma comb), while the AI value is flat noise around zero. The single-number summary distinguishes the regimes mechanistically.
5. **Fluctuation-driven firing: confirmed for both.** Per-cell mean total current $\mu_I$ and fluctuation amplitude $\sigma_I$ give the V&S-style ratio $\eta = \sigma_I / |I_{th} - \mu_I|$. Both regimes have 100% of cells with $\eta \geq 1$ (median 5.5 PING, 23 AI) — fluctuations are large enough to drive threshold crossings, exactly as V&S requires. PING's $\eta$ is contributed by the deterministic gamma cycle (cells cluster at $\mu_I \approx 0$); AI's far higher $\eta$ comes from the strong independent Poisson noise with $\mu_I$ near threshold.
6. **Chaos: positive Lyapunov exponent confirmed.** A two-trajectory perturbation experiment (Figure 5) shows PING re-locks to zero spike-train divergence after an ε = 2 mV kick (stable limit cycle, $\lambda \leq 0$) while V&S AI saturates at a sustained ≈ 6-cell divergence ($\lambda > 0$). The magnitude is modest because the strong shared frozen input partially entrains both copies (Mainen-Sejnowski reliability), so our cell is noise-entrained-with-chaos rather than the strongly-autonomous chaos of V&S's quenched-input network — but the qualitative PING-vs-AI distinction is unambiguous.

So: phenomenology + all three mechanistic signatures confirmed (low pairwise correlation, fluctuation-driven firing, positive Lyapunov exponent). The AI cell is in the V&S balanced regime, with the one quantitative caveat that the chaos is weak relative to the input entrainment.

## Next steps

1. **$1/\sqrt{K}$ scaling sanity check.** Sweep $N$ at fixed $K/N$ ratio with weights rescaled as $1/\sqrt{K}$ and verify that the regime is stable across $N$ — i.e. that we landed on the V&S balanced manifold, not on a substrate-specific operating point that happens to look like AI at one $N$. If it's truly V&S the per-neuron CV, rate, and PSD shape should all be $N$-invariant. (Fixed-$K$ connectivity, added this entry, is the prerequisite — it lets $K$ stay constant while $N$ varies.)
2. **Quenched-input chaos.** The Lyapunov probe (Figure 5) is partly masked by input entrainment because our per-cell drive is strong shared Poisson noise. Re-running with a *quenched* constant per-cell drive (frozen DC offset instead of fresh Poisson) would isolate the autonomous network chaos from the input-reliability effect and give the strong-divergence V&S signature.
