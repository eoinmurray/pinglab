## Abstract

Vreeswijk & Sompolinsky's 1996 *Science* paper showed that a sparsely-connected network of excitatory and inhibitory cells with strong recurrent weights settles, generically, into an *asynchronous irregular* (AI) state ‚Äî both populations firing at moderate rates, individual ISIs close to Poisson (CV ‚âà 1), and low pairwise correlations across cells. The mean recurrent inputs from E and I are individually large but cancel; what's left is fluctuations that drive each cell across threshold by noise, not by deterministic integration. This entry asks whether the same network that produces canonical PING in the gamma-gated-sparsity collection can also reach AI, and finds that it can: with sparse recurrent connectivity, per-cell independent external drive on both populations, and tuned recurrent weights, we land at $\langle r_E\rangle = 18$, $\langle r_I\rangle = 29$ Hz, per-E ISI CV = 0.97, per-I ISI CV = 1.08, and a broadband PSD with no gamma peak. Side-by-side with canonical PING (sharp 31 Hz peak, per-E CV 0.62, per-I CV 0.03), the AI cell is a qualitatively distinct dynamical regime of the *same architecture*, selected by operating point alone.

## Methods

### The V&S asynchronous-irregular state

The original 1996 model was a binary-spin network: $N$ units, each in state 0 or 1, updated asynchronously. Each unit received sparse random recurrent input from $K \ll N$ presynaptic units, plus an external excitatory drive. The crucial scaling ansatz: synaptic strengths $J \propto 1/\sqrt{K}$ and external drive $\propto \sqrt{K}$. Under that scaling, mean recurrent E and I inputs into each cell are each $O(\sqrt{K})$, large, *opposite-signed*, and self-organise to cancel; the residual is fluctuations of order $O(1)$ around the cancelled mean. With cells sitting near threshold and noise of comparable scale, the dynamics become:

- **Asynchronous** ‚Äî pairwise spike-train cross-correlation across cells stays $O(1/\sqrt{K})$, vanishing in the large-$K$ limit.
- **Irregular** ‚Äî each cell's ISI distribution approaches Poisson, CV ‚Üí 1.
- **Rate-balanced** ‚Äî mean rates are set by the balance condition, not by the bare external drive vs threshold.
- **Chaotic** ‚Äî positive maximum Lyapunov exponent; small perturbations decorrelate within one membrane time constant.

Brunel 2000 reproduced the regime with LIF neurons instead of binary spins. Renart et al. 2010 reproduced it with conductance-based synapses. The state is robust across these substrates ‚Äî it depends on the balance ansatz, not on the specific single-cell model.

### Our substrate

Conductance-based LIF, two populations $N_E = 1024, N_I = 256$, recurrent matrices $W^{EE}, W^{EI}, W^{IE}, W^{II}$ between them. The same network class used throughout the gamma-gated-sparsity collection ([ar003](/articles/ar003/)) for PING. To reach the V&S regime we needed four ingredients that the PING runs don't use:

- **An $I \to I$ matrix.** Canonical PING has none; V&S requires it to break the synchronous I-burst that would otherwise re-establish the cycle.
- **Sparse random recurrent connectivity.** Each entry of $W^{EE}, W^{EI}, W^{IE}, W^{II}$ is zeroed independently with probability $1 - K/N$ and surviving entries rescaled to preserve mean total drive. We use $K \approx 10$ ($N \approx 1000$), in the range V&S used for their numerical examples.
- **Per-E-cell independent external drive.** Each E cell receives its own uncorrelated Poisson stream, applied directly to its $g_E$, bypassing the feedforward $W_\text{in}$ matrix. This is the per-cell uncorrelated noise V&S takes as input.
- **Per-I-cell independent external drive.** Same for the I population. Without it, I-cell membrane fluctuations are entirely E-correlated through $W^{EI}$ and per-I CV stalls below 1.

Both cells run on the same COBANet ($N_E = 1024, N_I = 256$, $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms, $T = 1000$ ms) and differ only in the recurrent matrices, the connectivity sparsity, and the external drive:

| Quantity | PING (canonical, control) | V&S AI |
|---|---|---|
| External drive on E | Poisson at 20 Hz, routed through $W_\text{in} = \mathcal{N}(1.5, 0.3)$ | per-cell independent Poisson at 45 Hz √ó $0.38$ ŒºS, bypassing $W_\text{in}$ |
| External drive on I | none (only via $W^{EI}$ from E spikes) | per-cell independent Poisson at 8 Hz √ó $0.25$ ŒºS |
| Recurrent sparsity | 0 (dense) | 0.99 ($K \approx 10$ presynaptic inputs per post cell) |
| $W^{EI}$ init | `ei-strength 1.5` (biophysical defaults) | $\mathcal{N}(0.6, 0.18)$ ŒºS |
| $W^{IE}$ init | (same as $W^{EI}$) | $\mathcal{N}(3.0, 0.9)$ ŒºS |
| $W^{II}$ init | $0$ (no $I \to I$) | $\mathcal{N}(0.4, 0.12)$ ŒºS |
| Architectural class | conductance-based LIF, two populations | (same) |

The PING column is the canonical biophysical recipe from [nb023](/notebooks/nb023/). The AI column inherits the same network class and adds (i) the $W^{II}$ matrix, (ii) sparse recurrents, (iii) per-cell independent drive on both populations.

Each cell produces a five-panel figure: E raster, I raster, Welch PSD on the population-mean E trace (matching the [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) pipeline), per-E-neuron ISI-CV histogram with the Poisson reference at CV = 1, and the mean pairwise spike-train cross-correlogram on 100 random E-cell pairs.

## Discussion

Two regimes of the same architecture, separated by operating point alone. The biophysical substrate is rich enough to hold the loop together as a cycle clock (PING) and flexible enough to hold the cancelled-mean balance ansatz (AI). The matrices that gamma-lock in dense, strong-coupled territory dissolve into noise-driven crossings under sparse, balanced drive.

### How close are we to the canonical V&S state?

The two qualitative signatures that *define* AI in the V&S sense are reproduced cleanly:

- **CV ‚Üí 1.** Both populations have per-neuron ISI CV indistinguishable from Poisson (0.97 and 1.08). The PING I-population CV of 0.03 makes the contrast vivid.
- **No spectral peak.** The Welch PSD is broadband ‚Äî the cycle clock that drives PING is gone.

So at the level of *what the state looks like*, we have the V&S regime.

### What's different from the canonical setup

Three substrate differences, plus one *mechanistic* signature we now have, plus one we still don't:

1. **Conductance-based LIF, not binary spins.** V&S 1996 used binary asynchronous-update units. Brunel 2000 and Renart 2010 ported the regime onto LIF and conductance-based synapses respectively; this work follows that route. The substrate change matters quantitatively (specific scaling constants, rate-vs-drive curves) but the regime itself is robust to it.
2. **Per-entry Bernoulli sparsity, not exact-$K$ random connectivity.** V&S draws exactly $K$ presynaptic inputs per post cell; we zero each entry of $W$ independently with probability $1 - K/N$. At $N \gtrsim 100$ the law-of-large-numbers makes the two indistinguishable for population dynamics, but the cell-to-cell variability in actual fan-in is larger here, which broadens the rate distribution slightly above the V&S prediction.
3. **Hand-tuned weights, not the $1/\sqrt{K}$ scaling ansatz.** V&S derive their weight scaling from the requirement that recurrent fluctuations stay $O(1)$ in the large-$N$ limit. We picked weights by tuning on the diagnostic. The numerical result is in the right regime, but we haven't *verified* that the recurrent mean drives are individually $O(\sqrt{K})$-large and self-cancelling ‚Äî that diagnostic would be a follow-up.
4. **Pairwise correlations: confirmed AI-like.** The mean cross-correlogram on 100 random E-cell pairs gives peak |C| = 0.010 for the AI cell vs 0.041 for PING ‚Äî a 4√ó separation, and the PING value is structured (gamma comb), while the AI value is flat noise around zero. The single-number summary distinguishes the regimes mechanistically.
5. **Chaos not measured.** V&S characterise AI by a positive maximum Lyapunov exponent. We haven't computed it; the cleanest test is a two-trajectory perturbation experiment.

So: we have the *phenomenological* AI state and one of two mechanistic signatures (low pairwise correlation). The remaining gap is the chaos check, which would distinguish "V&S balanced manifold" from "any unstructured-broadband firing pattern that happens to also be uncorrelated."

## Next steps

1. **Mean-cancellation check.** Track the time-averaged total recurrent E and I input to each cell separately. V&S predicts they're each $O(\sqrt{K})$-large and approximately cancel, with the cancellation gap setting the rate. PING predicts coherent burst-driven oscillations in both.
2. **Exact-$K$ random connectivity.** Replace the per-entry Bernoulli sparsifier with a fixed-fan-in sampler that draws exactly $K$ presynaptic inputs per post cell. Removes the cell-to-cell fan-in variance that broadens our rate distribution above the V&S prediction and lets us hold $K$ fixed while sweeping $N$.
3. **$1/\sqrt{K}$ scaling sanity check.** Sweep $N$ at fixed $K/N$ ratio with weights rescaled as $1/\sqrt{K}$ and verify that the regime is stable across $N$ ‚Äî i.e. that we landed on the V&S balanced manifold, not on a substrate-specific operating point that happens to look like AI at one $N$. If it's truly V&S the per-neuron CV, rate, and PSD shape should all be $N$-invariant.
4. **Chaos / Lyapunov measurement.** Compute the maximum Lyapunov exponent from a two-trajectory perturbation experiment: clone the network state at $t = 0$, perturb one copy infinitesimally, integrate both, and measure the exponential separation rate of their spike trains. AI predicts $\lambda_\text{max} > 0$ (perturbations decorrelate within ‚âà one $\tau_\text{mem}$); PING predicts $\lambda_\text{max} \approx 0$ (perturbations get re-locked into the cycle).
