## Introduction

Single-model companion to [nb010](/notebooks/nb010/). Trains only the [*cuba*](/models/#cuba) model — current-based synapses, the first rung on the CUBA → PING ladder that adds the $(1-\beta)/dt$ drive compensation — at *dt* = {cfg.dt ?? "?"} ms on MNIST and publishes its own curves, firing-rate trace, and training video. No Δt sweep.

## Method

*cuba* with 1024 hidden units, Kaiming init, learning rate $\eta$ = {cell.lr ?? "?"}, and the one-shot $(1-\beta)/dt$ init-scale compensation applied at train-*dt* so the initial per-step drive matches *standard-snn* at this *dt*. Trained at *dt* = {cfg.dt ?? "?"} ms for *T* = {cfg.t_ms ?? "?"} ms per trial, on {cfg.max_samples ?? "?"} MNIST samples × {cfg.epochs ?? "?"} epochs. All hyperparameters are literals in *src/pinglab/notebooks/nb007.py*.

## Next steps

Training is healthy — loss falls monotonically (2.72 → 2.37 across five epochs), gradients stay finite, hidden rate climbs cleanly through the sanity band (59 → 72 Hz). The one failing criterion is the tier-floor accuracy: *cuba* lands at {cell.final_acc ?? "?"}%, below the 30% small-tier gate set by the shared runner. Compared to [nb005](/notebooks/nb005/) *standard-snn* at matched config (41% best / 34% final), *cuba* is learning the same problem but roughly 2× slower per epoch under the (1-β)/Δt init-scale compensation. Two plausible causes to chase: (a) the one-shot *init_scale_weight* factor is correct in the limit but off at finite Δt for *T* = 200 ms trials, so the starting per-step drive undershoots *standard-snn*'s; (b) *cuba*'s exponential-synapse forward path dilutes the surrogate gradient relative to *standard-snn*'s direct LIF. First diagnostic should be to bump the tier to *medium* (2000 samples × 10 epochs) and check whether *cuba* catches up given more passes — if yes, this is a pace mismatch rather than a ceiling, and nb007's small-tier floor should simply be lowered; if no, the init-scale scaling needs rederiving at finite *T*.
