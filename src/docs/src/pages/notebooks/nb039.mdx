## Introduction

[nb035](/notebooks/nb035/)–[nb038](/notebooks/nb038/) characterise PING in the conductance-based (COBA) regime, where every synapse has an explicit exponential filter with timescale $\tau_\text{AMPA} = 2$ ms or $\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms. The gamma cycle period emerges from those filters — pull $\tau_\text{GABA}$ longer and the cycle stretches; shorten it and the cycle compresses ([nb037 Figure 6](/notebooks/nb037/)).

This entry builds a current-based (CUBA) PING — a leaky integrate-and-fire network with the same E↔I wiring as the COBA-PING from nb035 — and runs it in two synaptic configurations:

- **Instant**: every presynaptic spike contributes its weight to the post-synaptic current at the next step, then vanishes. No exponential filter anywhere.
- **Exponential**: each pathway carries an exponentially-decaying synaptic current with $\tau_\text{AMPA} = 2$ ms on excitation, $\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms on inhibition — the same values used in COBA-PING.

No training, no MNIST: just forward dynamics under spatially uniform Poisson input at varying rates. The contrast lets us see what the synaptic filters add on top of what the LIF membrane already provides.

## Method

### Network (shared between both configurations)

- $N_E = 1024$ excitatory LIF neurons, $N_I = 256$ inhibitory LIF neurons, $N_\text{in} = 784$ input channels — same population sizes as the COBA-PING baselines.
- **Membrane**: $\tau_m = 20$ ms, $V_\text{rest} = -70$ mV, $V_\text{th} = -50$ mV, hard reset to $V_\text{reset} = -70$ mV. Effective input resistance $R_m = 200$ (dimensionless — chosen so a handful of input spikes per ms can push $V$ from rest to threshold).
- **Weights** are drawn once at seed 42 and frozen. Means match the canonical PING recipe — $W_\text{in} \sim \mathcal{N}(1.2, 0.12)$ at 95% sparsity, $W^{EI} \sim \mathcal{N}(1.0, 0.1)$, $W^{IE} \sim \mathcal{N}(2.0, 0.2)$ — clamped to be non-negative. The sign of the I→E pathway is applied at the current sum, not in the weight tensor.

### Two synaptic configurations

For each timestep $t$ ($\Delta t = 0.1$ ms, trial length $T = 200$ ms), with $s^E$ / $s^I$ the binary spike vectors of the previous step and $x_t \in \{0,1\}^{N_\text{in}}$ the Poisson input.

**Instant synapses:** currents are direct sums of presynaptic spikes weighted by $W$.

$$
I^E_t = x_t W_\text{in} - s^I_{t-1} W^{IE}, \qquad I^I_t = s^E_{t-1} W^{EI}.
$$

**Exponential synapses:** each pathway carries a synaptic state variable that integrates spike contributions and decays with the appropriate timescale.

$$
I^{E,\text{exc}}_t = d_\text{AMPA}\,I^{E,\text{exc}}_{t-1} + (1 - d_\text{AMPA})\,x_t W_\text{in},
$$

$$
I^{E,\text{inh}}_t = d_\text{GABA}\,I^{E,\text{inh}}_{t-1} + (1 - d_\text{GABA})\,s^I_{t-1} W^{IE},
$$

$$
I^{I}_t = d_\text{AMPA}\,I^{I}_{t-1} + (1 - d_\text{AMPA})\,s^E_{t-1} W^{EI},
$$

with $d_\text{AMPA} = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{AMPA}}$, $d_\text{GABA} = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{GABA}}$. The $(1 - d)$ prefactor scales the per-spike contribution so the steady-state current under constant spike rate matches the instant case — only the *temporal distribution* of current differs between the two modes, not the integrated drive per spike.

**Common membrane integration** (both modes):

$$
V^E_{t+1} = V^E_t + \frac{\Delta t}{\tau_m}\!\left(-(V^E_t - V_\text{rest}) + R_m\, I^E_t\right),
$$

with analogous update for $V^I$. Spike when $V \geq V_\text{th}$, hard reset to $V_\text{reset}$.

The instant-synapse case reproduces all three qualitative signatures of COBA-PING: a recruitment cliff at ≈20 Hz input, inhibitory clamp on E rate across two decades of drive, and a clean periodic I-burst PSTH at gamma cadence. The cycle period comes from the LIF membrane time constant $\tau_m = 20$ ms — the time E needs to recover from $V_\text{reset}$ back above threshold after an I-pulse hyperpolarises it. With no synaptic filters, the membrane is the only clock in the network.

## Exponential synapses

Replacing every instantaneous current with the corresponding exponentially-filtered synaptic state changes the cycle period substantially.

## Discussion

Both synaptic configurations reproduce the two architectural signatures of PING — recruitment cliff and inhibitory clamp on E rate. The difference shows up in the cycle period:

| | Instant synapses | Exponential synapses |
|---|---|---|
| Cycle period | ≈25 ms | ≈60–80 ms |
| PSTH peak frequency | ≈40 Hz (gamma) | ≈14 Hz (beta) |
| E rate at 100 Hz input | 0.73 Hz | 1.16 Hz |
| I rate at 100 Hz input | 22.8 Hz | 49.5 Hz |

The naive expectation is that adding synaptic filters would speed the cycle up by introducing additional delays. The data say the opposite: filtering makes the cycle *slower*. The reason is the $\tau_\text{GABA}$ hold-off — once the I-population fires, the resulting inhibitory current persists for several $\tau_\text{GABA}$, suppressing E for ≈3 × 9 = 27 ms regardless of when E's membrane recovers. In the instant case, inhibition vanishes the step after the I-burst, so the cycle period is set purely by $\tau_m$ membrane recovery from $V_\text{reset}$ to $V_\text{th}$ ≈ 20–25 ms. Filtering replaces "fast inhibition + slow membrane" with "slow inhibition + slow membrane", and the slow inhibition wins.

Neither CUBA-PING variant lands at the canonical 25 Hz gamma the COBA-PING baselines produce ([nb038 Figure 3](/notebooks/nb038/)). The COBA configuration sits between these two CUBA cases — closer to instant in cycle period than to exp, because conductance-based inhibition is **shunting** (multiplicative on $V - E_\text{GABA}$) rather than additive: a high $g_\text{GABA}$ pulls $V$ toward $E_\text{GABA}$ but doesn't hold it there as a sustained negative current would. Once $g_\text{GABA}$ decays, $V$ can drift back toward threshold quickly, unlike the CUBA-exp case where the inhibitory current itself takes $\tau_\text{GABA}$ to fade.

So the cycle clock partitions between three contributions in COBA-PING — membrane recovery, synaptic timescales, and the shunting vs additive distinction — with $\tau_\text{GABA}$ being the dominant *but not sole* parameter the [nb037 τ_GABA sweep](/notebooks/nb037/) identifies. The CUBA variants here bracket the COBA behaviour from both sides: instant CUBA puts all the clock on $\tau_m$, exp CUBA puts most of it on $\tau_\text{GABA}$, COBA splits the difference.
