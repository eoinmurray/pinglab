## Introduction

## Method

Six models trained at two *dt* regimes; per-model recipes mirror their sibling per-model trainer ([nb006](/notebooks/nb006/)–[nb011](/notebooks/nb011/)) verbatim.

**Models** (see [the ladder](/models/)):

- [*standard-snn*](/models/#standard-snn) — pinglab reimplementation of snnTorch's *snn.Leaky*. [snnTorch Tutorial 3 § 1.2](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_3.html#the-decay-rate-beta) derives the proper Euler form $U(t+\Delta t) = (1-\Delta t/\tau)\,U(t) + (\Delta t/\tau)\,I_\text{in}\,R$, then simplifies by setting $\Delta t = 1$, $R = 1$, and absorbing the $(1-\beta)$ prefactor into the learnable weight $W$. Here $\beta = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{mem}}$ is the per-step membrane decay factor — exact under exponential-Euler integration of the homogeneous LIF equation. After the Tutorial-3 simplification $\Delta t$ no longer appears in the update — each input spike adds exactly $W_i$ to the membrane regardless of how fine the timestep is.
- [*standard-snn-exp*](/models/#standard-snn) — *standard-snn* with an exponential synapse ($\tau_\text{syn} \approx 2$ ms) on the feedforward inputs to the hidden layer. Single-knob ablation isolating the synaptic kernel ([nb007](/notebooks/nb007/)).
- [*snntorch-library*](/models/#snntorch-library) — snnTorch's *snn.Leaky* directly. Parity probe against *standard-snn*.
- [*cuba*](/models/#cuba) — same LIF, exp-Euler + ZOH integration restoring the $(1-\beta)/dt$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors. *dt* is back in the update.
- [*coba*](/models/#coba) — conductance-based LIF with exponential synapses, hard reset, refractory. COBANet with *--ei-strength 0* (feedforward-only).
- [*ping*](/models/#ping) — COBA plus the frozen E→I→E recurrent loop that produces gamma.

Train-*dt* sweeps over {dt1} ms and {dt2} ms — same recipe per regime, only *--dt* changes.

The biggest cross-model confounds for treating *model* as the sole independent variable are *--lr* ($\sim$100× spread) and *--w-in* init (PING pair only). Both are inherited from the per-model recipes by design — the conductance-based dynamics of coba/ping with multiplicative $g \cdot (V - E_\text{rev})$ are far more LR-sensitive than the additive snnTorch update, so a uniform LR would either diverge the PING pair or undertrain the rest.

### Hidden layer firing rate vs eval-*dt*

{hasFig("firing_rates.png") && (
  <Figure id="Figure 2" title="Hidden firing rate vs eval-dt" src="/figures/notebooks/nb012/firing_rates.png" alt="Mean hidden firing rate vs eval-dt, 2x2 grid (rows: count-preserving / resample, columns: train-dt)">
    Mean hidden-layer firing rate under frozen weights as eval-dt is swept. Top row: count-preserving transports (solid = upsample, dashed = downsample); bottom row: resample. Red dashed vertical marks train-dt. Color groups family: blue gradient = snnTorch family, green = cuba, orange/red = PING family.
  </Figure>
)}

The snnTorch family (blue) increases as eval-*dt* shrinks below train-*dt*, while
cuba/coba/ping (green/orange/red) stay roughly flat. The mechanism is visible
directly in the steady-state membrane potential.

For the [Tutorial-3](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_3.html#the-decay-rate-beta)
update used by [*standard-snn*](/models/#standard-snn) and
[*snntorch-library*](/models/#snntorch-library), $U_{t+1} = \beta U_t + W s_t +
b$, ($\beta = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{mem}}$). Take the expectation under mean input spike rate $r$: $\mathbb{E}[s_t] =
r\Delta t$, so each step adds $Wr\Delta t + b$ on average. At the fixed point
$U_{ss} = \beta U_{ss} + Wr\Delta t + b$, giving $U_{ss} = (Wr\Delta t +
b)/(1-\beta)$. Using $1-\beta \approx \Delta t/\tau$ for $\Delta t \ll \tau$ (because for very small $x$, $e^{-x} \approx 1 - x + x^2/2 - \ldots \approx 1 - x$),
this is $U_{ss} \approx Wr\tau + b\tau/\Delta t$.

The spike contribution $W r \tau$ is
*dt*-invariant; the bias contribution $b\tau/\Delta t$ scales as $1/\Delta t$.
Halving eval-*dt* doubles the bias term — and the network fires harder to
compensate. The
cuba/coba/ping families restore the missing $(1-\beta)/\Delta t$ and $(1-\beta)$
prefactors during integration, so $U_{ss} = W r + b$ — fully *dt*-invariant by
construction.
