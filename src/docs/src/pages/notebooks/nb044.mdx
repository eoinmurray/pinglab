The Δt audit asks whether the [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) headline E rate (≈ 7 Hz) is a physical (Hz) property of the trained network or an artefact of the integration timestep. The honest answer is **partially the latter**: the gamma *cycle* is Δt-invariant but per-cycle *participation* scales with Δt, so the time-averaged rate increases with Δt even though the cycle physics survives. Scaffolded by [art009](/articles/art009/) §Leg 1 item 4.

## Method

Train one PING from scratch per $\Delta t \in \{0.05, 0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 1.0\}$ ms × seed $\in \{42, 43, 44\}$ = 15 cells. Total physical time $T = 200$ ms held constant (step count varies 4000 → 200). Batch size 64 throughout — smaller than [nb025](/nb025/)'s 256, but matched across the sweep so per-step compute and memory stay comparable, and the Δt = 0.05 cells (4000 timesteps × $N_E$ × $N_I$) fit in a single A100. All other PING recipe parameters held to [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/).

After training, run inference on the test set; report mean E rate (Hz), accuracy, and a single-trial raster from seed 42 per Δt for visual cycle-period inspection.

The raster strip is the load-bearing diagnostic. Across the 20× Δt sweep, the gamma cycle period in *milliseconds* is invariant — bursts at the same physical cadence in every panel. What scales is the *count* of E spikes per burst: sparser at Δt = 0.05, denser at Δt = 1.0. The cycle physics is Δt-invariant; per-cycle participation is not.

## Why the rate scales with Δt

In the discretised LIF integrator with forward Euler, $V_{t+1} = V_t + \Delta t \cdot \dot V$, a single timestep advances the membrane farther in mV when Δt is larger. Two consequences relevant here:

- **Threshold crossing per step is more permissive at coarse Δt.** A subthreshold $V_t$ with a positive drift $\dot V$ may not cross $V_\text{th}$ in 0.05 ms but does in 1 ms. Per-cycle, more E cells reach threshold within the depolarising window before the next I burst, so per-burst participation rises with Δt.
- **The refractory window is held constant in physical ms** ($\text{ref\_ms\_E} = 3$ ms → $\text{ref\_steps\_E} = 3/\Delta t$ steps), so refractory dynamics don't explain the rate change.

Together, the rate ceiling **per cycle** scales with Δt, but the **cycle frequency** (cycle period set by $\tau_\text{AMPA} + \tau_\text{GABA}$) does not. The affine law from [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) was $r_E = a + p \cdot f_\gamma$; $f_\gamma$ is Δt-invariant, $p$ is not. nb041 was run at $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms; the slope $p = 0.166$ Hz/Hz is specific to that integration regime.

  Top: test accuracy converges by epoch ~ 10–15 across all Δt. Bottom: test E rate has *not* converged in 30 epochs — every curve is still rising. The Δt-dependent rate scaling reported above is a snapshot of medium-tier training at epoch 30, not a fixed-point ceiling.
</Figure>

Two convergence observations:

1. **Accuracy converges within ~ 15 epochs in every cell.** All Δt values reach 86–91% by epoch 15 and plateau. The Δt = 0.05 cells take slightly longer to climb because the per-step gradient is smaller, but they converge.
2. **E rate does not converge in 30 epochs at any Δt.** The bottom panel shows monotonic rate increase across the entire training run, in every cell. The reported rate-vs-Δt scaling is the snapshot at epoch 30. With longer training every cell would have a higher rate; the *ordering* (coarser Δt → higher rate) should persist, but the absolute span (4.79 Hz at epoch 30) is not a converged value.

The gamma cycle observation from Figure 2 is independent of training convergence — it's a property of the underlying dynamics and would hold at any training duration. The rate-vs-Δt scaling is the part that's snapshot-bound.

## Implication for [art008](/articles/art008/)

This audit succeeds at its job — it found a real Δt dependence that needs to be acknowledged in the paper, but it also confirms the *structural* claim is preserved across Δt:

1. **The gamma cycle period is Δt-invariant** — the cycle is genuine network dynamics, not a step-counting artefact.
2. **The qualitative architectural separation survives.** At every Δt the PING network fires at single-digit-Hz to ~ 11 Hz; the COBA equivalent from [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) sits at ~ 88 Hz. The 10× rate gap that motivates the forbidding claim does not collapse at coarse or fine Δt.
3. **The numerical headline rate is Δt-specific.** Reporting "~ 7 Hz" without qualifying Δt would be sloppy; the right statement is "≈ 7.5 Hz at $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms, scaling to ≈ 11 Hz at $\Delta t = 1$ ms via a per-cycle integration effect, with the gamma cycle invariant in both regimes."
4. **The affine law from [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) is Δt-conditional in its slope.** $f_\gamma$ on the x-axis carries across Δt; $p$ on the y-axis scales with Δt. The *form* of the law is preserved, the numerical $p$ is not.

## Next steps

The art008 framing should anchor the rate claim at $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms and explicitly cite this audit's Figure 2 (the Δt-invariant gamma cycle) as the structural-bound argument's load-bearing observation. The Figure 1 rate-vs-Δt scaling is itself worth one paragraph of methods, noting that the per-cycle integration of the LIF dynamics has a residual Δt effect on participation probability.

A natural follow-up is a *retrained* version of [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) at multiple Δt values — six $\tau_\text{GABA}$ × three Δt would test whether the affine law's slope $p$ scales predictably with Δt or wobbles. That experiment is not on the [art008](/articles/art008/) critical path but is the cleanest way to establish the Δt → $p$ functional form.
