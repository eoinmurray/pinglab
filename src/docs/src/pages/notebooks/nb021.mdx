## Introduction

Train *COBA* ([nb011](/notebooks/nb011/)) and *PING* ([nb012](/notebooks/nb012/)) on MNIST under two loss modes:

- *ce* — cross-entropy on the cumulative-membrane logits (the default across nb010–12).
- *mse* — L2 between logits and one-hot targets (Bohte 2002, Lee 2016).

The motivation is readout scale. *COBA* and *ping* use *--readout-w-out-scale* to amplify the cumulative-membrane logits so that the cross-entropy softmax doesn't saturate. L2 loss has no softmax and is sensitive to the target's absolute scale rather than ratios, so the same scaling factor that helped CE is likely to over- or under-shoot for MSE. This notebook records whether the existing recipes train with *--loss mse* at all, and what the accuracy gap to *ce* is at the calibrated operating point.

## Method

Six cells: three models × two loss modes. Each cell runs the calibrated recipe from its single-model trainer (nb010/11/12) with *--loss ce* or *--loss mse* added; every other knob is held fixed. The runner shells out to [oscilloscope *train*](/docs/the-oscilloscope/#subcommand-train) per cell and renders one accuracy bar chart plus one training video per cell.

- **Recipes are unchanged from nb011/12.** *--readout-w-out-scale* in particular is left at its CE-calibrated value (100 for COBA, 500 for PING); if MSE collapses, the readout scale is the prime suspect.
- **Six cells, one shared seed.** Stochastic optimisation differences between *ce* and *mse* show up as run-to-run noise; tighter follow-ups can re-seed and re-run.
