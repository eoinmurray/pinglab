[nb025](/notebooks/nb025/)'s rate gap admits a cheap reading: PING fires less because the I-loop delivers more inhibition. This entry forecloses that reading and identifies *what* about the I-stream is doing the suppressing — qualitatively (rhythm vs mean), and quantitatively (which temporal precision is required). Scaffolded by [art009](/articles/art009/) §Leg 1 item 2.

## Method

Pure inference on the trained [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) PING baseline (three seeds: 42, 43, 44; $\theta_u = $ off). For each batch the I-population spike tensor $\mathbf{s}^I_\text{base} \in \{0,1\}^{T \times B \times N_I}$ is recorded from a baseline forward pass, then an override tensor replaces it in a second pass via the [nb037](/notebooks/nb037/) hidden-perturbation hook. The E-population experiences only the override I-stream through $W^{IE}$; the readout consumes the perturbed E spikes. Mean per-cell I rate is matched to the baseline to four decimals across every perturbation.

Four perturbation families:

1. **Baseline** — no override; trained PING dynamics.
2. **Cycle-coherent jitter** — partition the trial into blocks of length $1/f_\gamma$ ($\approx 28$ ms at the trained operating point from [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/)). For each (trial, block), draw a single Gaussian offset $\Delta \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ and shift every I-spike in that block by $\Delta$. Within-burst cross-cell synchrony preserved exactly; only the *placement* of each burst is perturbed. Sweep $\sigma \in \{0, 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28, 42, 60, 100\}$ ms.
3. **Phase-shuffle** — per-trial permutation $\pi_b$ of the time axis applied to all I-cells together: $\mathbf{s}^I_\text{shuf}[t, b, n] = \mathbf{s}^I_\text{base}[\pi_b(t), b, n]$. Preserves cross-cell co-firing within a timestep; destroys all phase structure.
4. **Rate-matched Poisson** — per-(trial, cell) Bernoulli with $p = \text{count}_{b,n} / T$. Destroys both temporal and cross-cell structure; tests the $g_i$ variance limit.

Test split from [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) at medium tier (400 samples, $T = 200$ ms, $\Delta t = 0.1$ ms, batch 64).

## Results

### The rate release scales with jitter, with a transition at $1/f_\gamma$

The jitter sweep is the load-bearing experiment. The prediction is sharp: if the operative variable is *temporal phase relative to the gamma cycle*, then the relevant timescale is the cycle period $1/f_\gamma$. At $\sigma \ll 1/f_\gamma$ bursts barely move from their phase-locked slots and the rate should be unchanged. At $\sigma \gtrsim 1/f_\gamma$ bursts can land anywhere within a cycle, losing phase relation to E, and the rate should rise toward an asymptote.

At the trained operating point ($\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms), [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) gives $f_\gamma \approx 36$ Hz, predicting the inflection at $\sigma \approx 28$ ms.

The curve matches the prediction. The rate is flat within seed variance for $\sigma \le 1$ ms ($\approx 7$ Hz, baseline). The transition is centred between $\sigma = 7$ ms and $\sigma = 28$ ms, crossing the phase-shuffle ceiling between $\sigma = 14$ and $\sigma = 21$ ms — just below the predicted inflection. At $\sigma = 100$ ms the rate has saturated at $\approx 42$ Hz, *above* the phase-shuffle level because cycle-coherent jitter at very large $\sigma$ opens gaps in the I-stream wide enough for E to fire densely with no inhibition at all. Accuracy holds at 79–86% throughout — the rate release is not paid in classification.

What this gets us, beyond a binary "the rhythm matters":

1. **Identifies the operative timescale.** If a different process — refractory dynamics ($\sim 3$ ms), membrane time constant ($\sim 20$ ms), AMPA decay ($\sim 2$ ms) — were the operative clock, the inflection would be elsewhere. It is at $1/f_\gamma$.
2. **The dependence is graded, not knife-edge.** The I-loop tolerates sub-cycle jitter and degrades smoothly past it.
3. **The transition timescale is a quantitative prediction.** $1/f_\gamma$ is set by [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/)'s law. The same architecture trained at a different $\tau_\text{GABA}$ would give a different $f_\gamma$ and the jitter inflection should track it.

The rasters at five jitter levels show the gap-opening mechanism without statistics.

At $\sigma = 0$ the gamma bands are crisp — E bursts immediately followed by I bursts, every $\approx 28$ ms. At $\sigma = 7$ ms bursts visibly drift but remain phase-locked enough to gate E. At $\sigma \ge 14$ ms gaps of tens of milliseconds open up in the I-stream, and E fires densely through them. The single-trial rate annotations track the population statistics: 9.6 → 11.5 → 32.8 → 52.5 → 43.6 Hz across the five σ values.

**Phase-shuffle** is consistent with the jitter sweep's $\sigma \to \infty$ asymptote (modulo the fact that phase-shuffle also destroys within-burst structure, which is why the $\sigma = 100$ ms jitter rate exceeds the phase-shuffle ceiling). The smooth sigmoid through 7–28 ms in Figure 1 shows that phase-shuffle is not a special case; it is one endpoint of a continuous family.

**Poisson** silences E entirely, and *not* by the same mechanism the jitter sweep tests. Replacing synchronous bursts with an i.i.d. Bernoulli stream keeps mean $g_i$ identical (the filter is linear) but collapses its *variance*. Instead of large peaks separated by $e^{-\Delta t_\text{cycle}/\tau_\text{GABA}} \approx 0.07$-of-peak troughs in which $V_E$ can recover, $g_i$ becomes a tight low-variance fluctuation around the mean with no troughs at all. Continuous shunting holds $V_\infty$ below threshold and E never fires. This is a separate architectural variable from the phase-locking the jitter sweep tests — bursty inhibition lets E recover between bursts; continuous inhibition at matched mean does not.

## Discussion

Three findings of decreasing cleanliness:

- **The operative variable is phase precision relative to the gamma cycle.** (Jitter sweep, clean.) The rate release is graded, the transition timescale is $1/f_\gamma$ to within seed variance, and the prediction is independently anchored in [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/).
- **At full phase scrambling, rate climbs $\sim 3$× toward COBA territory.** (Phase-shuffle, clean endpoint.) The phase-shuffle E rate (~ 23 Hz across 3 seeds) is the $\sigma \to \infty$ asymptote of the jitter sweep; the bar chart's categorical version is now subsumed by Figure 1's dose-response.
- **Continuous low-variance inhibition silences E.** (Poisson, distinct mechanism.) This is a claim about $g_i$ variance, not about phase. Useful as a complementary observation about the *form* of inhibition required (bursty, with troughs), but not a test of phase-locking.

The combined argument: PING's rate floor is held by gamma phase-locking with sub-cycle precision. The minimum precision required is roughly $1/f_\gamma$; degrade it and the rate rises monotonically toward the COBA regime. Mean inhibition is not the operative variable, and even at matched mean rate the *form* of the inhibitory stream (bursty vs continuous) matters separately.

This integrates with [nb041](/notebooks/nb041/) in a clean way. nb041 retrains PING at different $\tau_\text{GABA}$ and finds $r_E = a + p \cdot f_\gamma$. nb042 (jitter sweep) is the *inference-time analogue*: keep the trained network, degrade the temporal precision of its I-stream, and watch the rate rise. The transition timescale $1/f_\gamma$ is set by the same architectural variable that sets the slope of the affine law. Two perturbations through different doors give the same answer: the gamma cycle is the operative clock.

## Open question

The jitter sweep's $\sigma = 100$ ms asymptote ($\approx 42$ Hz) exceeds the phase-shuffle ceiling ($\approx 23$ Hz) and approaches COBA-territory firing. The mechanism (gaps in the I-stream let E fire densely) is mechanistically clear, but the *quantitative* match between $\sigma = \infty$ jitter and trained-COBA rates would require comparing matched-rate operating points. Worth probing if the architecture-equivalence claim from [nb038](/notebooks/nb038/) is to be tightened.

## Success criteria

- [x] Mean I rate held within 5% across all perturbations (matched to four decimals; control held).
- [x] E rate flat within seed variance at $\sigma \le 1$ ms (within 5% of baseline).
- [x] E rate crosses the phase-shuffle reference between $\sigma = 14$ and $\sigma = 21$ ms — consistent with the $1/f_\gamma \approx 28$ ms prediction.
- [x] E rate under phase-shuffle ≥ 2× baseline (3.3×: 6.91 → 23.11 Hz).
- [x] E rate under Poisson ≤ 0.1 × baseline (silenced to 0 Hz).
