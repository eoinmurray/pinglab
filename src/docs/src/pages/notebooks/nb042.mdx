## Setup

[nb040](/notebooks/nb040/) showed that CUBA-PING's I-loop architecturally clamps the hidden-E rate near 1 Hz, while the CUBA-no-PING control sits at tens of Hz. [nb035](/notebooks/nb035/)–[nb036](/notebooks/nb036/) showed that the COBA-PING baselines walk the same rate–accuracy frontier via the explicit firing-rate regulariser $L_\text{rate}$. This entry transplants the nb035 sweep to the instant-synapse CUBA family — adding the spike-budget penalty $s_u \cdot \text{relu}(\bar{z} - \theta_u)^2$ to CE loss and sweeping $\theta_u$ on both arms — to ask the same two questions:

1. **Does the penalty walk the no-PING control onto a sparse-spike frontier comparable to PING's?** (the *penalty alone* path to low firing rates)
2. **Does the I-loop architecture stay quiet under the penalty, sitting at its natural floor?** (the *architectural floor* should make the penalty redundant on PING)

Same recipe as nb040 — `cuba-ping` and `cuba-noping` via the oscilloscope, $\Delta t = 1$ ms, $T = 200$ ms, hard reset, no refractory period, no synaptic state, mem-mean output readout, TBPTT $K = 10$, Adam at lr $2 \times 10^{-3}$, batch 64, 5000 training samples, 40 epochs. The penalty is applied to the population-mean spike count $\bar{z} = \mathbb{E}_{i, \text{batch}}[z_i]$ — a single scalar across all hidden neurons and the batch — with $s_u = 10^{-3}$ matching nb036. The grid $\theta_u \in \{\text{off}, 5, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.2\}$ spikes-per-trial is identical to nb035/036 so the frontier is directly cross-comparable. At $T = 200$ ms, $\theta_u$ in spk/trial × 5 = Hz, i.e. $\theta_u = 0.2$ corresponds to a 1 Hz target.

## Interpretation

Both architectural and explicit knobs work, but they do different things.

**PING + penalty is flat across the sweep.** For $\theta_u \geq 0.5$ every cell lands at exactly 84.7% / 1.37 Hz / 39.7 Hz I — bit-identical to PING-off. The reason is mechanical: PING's population mean E-rate (≈0.27 spk/trial) sits below every threshold from 0.5 up, so $\text{relu}(\bar{z} - \theta_u) = 0$ exactly, the penalty term vanishes, and the optimisation reduces to the nb040 PING-off problem. The I-loop is already doing all the rate-control work.

The exception is $\theta_u = 0.2$, where the threshold is *below* the population mean. The penalty engages but the optimisation doesn't converge to a low-rate solution — it lands at 6.25 Hz / 82.6%, *higher* than PING-off's rate and slightly lower accuracy. Figure 4's bottom-right panel makes the mechanism visible: the network jumps to a qualitatively different operating regime — dense E firing across the full population, no gamma cycles, I firing every step rather than in bursts. See [bistability](#bistability) below for the trajectory. So the I-loop's natural floor (≈1.4 Hz) is a hard wall — pushing below it with the penalty doesn't help.

**no-PING + penalty walks the frontier cleanly.** From the rate-coded baseline (39 Hz / 90%) down to PING territory (1.28 Hz / 88%) is **30× rate compression at 2.6 pp accuracy cost**. The frontier is monotone, the trajectory is stable on every cell, and at every matched rate against PING the accuracy is comparable or higher. The explicit penalty is doing for the E-only network what the I-loop architecture does for PING — driving the rate down without losing the digit signal.

This reproduces nb035 Figure 5 for the instant-synapse CUBA family. The I-loop and the explicit penalty are *substitutes*: either alone is enough to land the network in the sparse-spike regime at high accuracy. Combining them (PING + tight penalty) doesn't help beyond what the architecture already does, and pushing below the architectural floor with the penalty pays a real cost.

## Bistability

The $\theta_u = 0.2$ PING outlier (Figure 1; Figure 4 bottom-right) traces a clean bifurcation through training. The trajectory has two distinct phases:

- **Phase 1 (epochs 1–28)**: trajectory is *identical* to PING-off. Loss 2.30 → 0.65, accuracy 11% → 82%, E rate climbs to 1.88 Hz, I rate to 66 Hz, gradient norm ≈0.09. The population mean of ≈0.38 spk/trial is only fractionally above $\theta_u = 0.2$, and the gradient on each of 1024 neurons is ≈$3 \times 10^{-7}$ — negligible.
- **Bifurcation at epoch 29**: a one-epoch discontinuity. E rate jumps 1.88 → 4.31 Hz, I rate 66 → 172 Hz, gradient norm 0.090 → 6.45 (a 70× jump in a single epoch).
- **Phase 2 (epochs 29–40)**: settles at a *different attractor*. E rate ≈9 Hz, I rate ≈250 Hz, gradient norm ≈30, accuracy holds at 82–83%, but loss climbs slowly from 0.70 to 1.81.

The interpretation is that PING's recurrent E↔I dynamics support multiple stable fixed points. The low-rate attractor (E ≈ 1.4 Hz, I ≈ 40 Hz) is the one PING-off occupies — the gamma-burst regime visible in Figure 4's top row. The high-rate attractor (E ≈ 9 Hz, I ≈ 250 Hz) is the dense-firing regime in Figure 4 bottom-right. For 28 epochs the penalty applies tiny but persistent downward pressure on the population mean. At epoch 28→29 the cumulative weight changes cross a separatrix in $W_\text{in}$ space, the dynamics flip to the high-rate attractor, and the network gets stuck there: the penalty is now fully active (population mean ≈1.84 spk/trial, well above $\theta_u = 0.2$) and applies real downward gradient (gn ≈30), but the new attractor is stable enough that the optimiser can't pull it back.

The accuracy preservation (83%) is a side note worth understanding: at the new operating point the readout sees many more spike events per trial, so the digit signal is still encoded — just in a denser representation. The *loss* climbs because the logit distribution becomes more extreme (confidently wrong on a small handful of hard samples) while top-1 accuracy is robust to that.

So the $\theta_u = 0.2$ outlier isn't a noise event but concrete evidence that PING has multiple stable operating regimes, and that a sufficiently weak-but-persistent rate penalty can tip the network from one to the other across a long training run.

## Findings

The architectural rate-control (I-loop) and the explicit rate-control (spike-budget penalty) are interchangeable in the instant-synapse CUBA family, just as they are in the COBA-PING family from [nb035](/notebooks/nb035/). On no-PING the penalty pulls rate by 30× for ≈3 pp accuracy; on PING the architectural floor already does the work and the penalty is a no-op until $\theta_u$ drops below the natural floor.

The natural follow-ups:

1. **Spectral-norm regulariser on $W^{EI}, W^{IE}$.** The I-loop's natural floor is set by the operator norm of the recurrent product; controlling that directly is the architectural analogue of $\theta_u$. Combined with the penalty it would give a continuous knob on the floor itself.
2. **Lower-bound regulariser $\theta_l$ (Cramer et al. 2022).** Constrains $\bar{z}$ from below so the optimiser doesn't drive cells silent on hard-to-classify samples. Useful for the tightest-$\theta_u$ no-PING cell where some accuracy is being lost to silence.
3. **Scale to full MNIST.** The 5000-sample / 40-epoch run lands no-PING at 90% accuracy; with full data, both arms should reach the ≈98% regime PING-off does in the COBA-PING baselines.
