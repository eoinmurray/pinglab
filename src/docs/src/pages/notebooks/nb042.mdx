## Setup

[nb040](/notebooks/nb040/) showed that CUBA-PING's I-loop architecturally clamps the hidden-E rate near 1 Hz, while the CUBA-no-PING control sits at tens of Hz. [nb035](/notebooks/nb035/)–[nb036](/notebooks/nb036/) showed that the COBA-PING baselines walk the same rate–accuracy frontier via the explicit firing-rate regulariser $L_\text{rate}$. This entry transplants the nb035 sweep to the instant-synapse CUBA family — adding the spike-budget penalty $s_u \cdot \text{relu}(\bar{z} - \theta_u)^2$ to CE loss and sweeping $\theta_u$ on both arms — to ask the same two questions:

1. **Does the penalty walk the no-PING control onto a sparse-spike frontier comparable to PING's?** (the *penalty alone* path to low firing rates)
2. **Does the I-loop architecture stay quiet under the penalty, sitting at its natural floor?** (the *architectural floor* should make the penalty redundant on PING)

Same recipe as nb040 — `cuba-ping` and `cuba-noping` via the oscilloscope, $\Delta t = 1$ ms, $T = 200$ ms, hard reset, no refractory period, no synaptic state, mem-mean output readout, TBPTT $K = 10$, Adam at lr $2 \times 10^{-3}$, batch 64, 5000 training samples, 40 epochs. The penalty uses $s_u = 10^{-3}$ matching nb036. The grid $\theta_u \in \{\text{off}, 5, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.2\}$ spikes-per-trial is identical to nb035/036 so the frontier is directly cross-comparable. At $T = 200$ ms, $\theta_u$ in spk/trial × 5 = Hz, i.e. $\theta_u = 0.2$ corresponds to a 1 Hz target.

The penalty is applied to the **population mean** spike count $\bar{z} = \mathbb{E}_{i, \text{batch}}[z_i]$ — a single scalar across all hidden neurons and the batch — rather than the per-neuron mean $\langle z_i\rangle$ used in nb035/036. The reason is documented in [the diagnostic](#fr-reg-formulation) at the end of this entry: the per-neuron formulation interacts pathologically with PING's selective sparse code, destabilising training. The population formulation gives identical behaviour on no-PING (uniform population, no high-firing tail) and lets the I-loop's architectural floor coexist with the penalty on PING.

## Interpretation

Both architectural and explicit knobs work, but they do different things.

**PING + penalty is flat across the sweep.** For $\theta_u \geq 0.5$ every cell lands at exactly 84.7% / 1.37 Hz / 39.7 Hz I — bit-identical to PING-off. The reason is mechanical: PING's population mean E-rate (≈0.27 spk/trial) sits below every threshold from 0.5 up, so $\text{relu}(\bar{z} - \theta_u) = 0$ exactly, the penalty term vanishes, and the optimisation reduces to the nb040 PING-off problem. The I-loop is already doing all the rate-control work.

The exception is $\theta_u = 0.2$, where the threshold is *below* the population mean. The penalty engages but the optimisation doesn't converge to a low-rate solution — it lands at 6.25 Hz / 82.6%, *higher* than PING-off's rate and slightly lower accuracy. The penalty gradient and the I-loop's recurrent Jacobian briefly cooperate to dampen suppression, the network finds a higher-rate equilibrium, and accuracy pays a small price. So the I-loop's natural floor (≈1.4 Hz) is a hard wall — pushing below it with the penalty doesn't help.

**no-PING + penalty walks the frontier cleanly.** From the rate-coded baseline (39 Hz / 90%) down to PING territory (1.28 Hz / 88%) is **30× rate compression at 2.6 pp accuracy cost**. The frontier is monotone, the trajectory is stable on every cell, and at every matched rate against PING the accuracy is comparable or higher. The explicit penalty is doing for the E-only network what the I-loop architecture does for PING — driving the rate down without losing the digit signal.

This reproduces nb035 Figure 5 for the instant-synapse CUBA family. The I-loop and the explicit penalty are *substitutes*: either alone is enough to land the network in the sparse-spike regime at high accuracy. Combining them (PING + tight penalty) doesn't help beyond what the architecture already does, and pushing below the architectural floor with the penalty pays a real cost.

## Findings

The architectural rate-control (I-loop) and the explicit rate-control (spike-budget penalty) are interchangeable in the instant-synapse CUBA family, just as they are in the COBA-PING family from [nb035](/notebooks/nb035/). On no-PING the penalty pulls rate by 30× for ≈3 pp accuracy; on PING the architectural floor already does the work and the penalty is a no-op until $\theta_u$ drops below the natural floor.

The natural follow-ups:

1. **Spectral-norm regulariser on $W^{EI}, W^{IE}$.** The I-loop's natural floor is set by the operator norm of the recurrent product; controlling that directly is the architectural analogue of $\theta_u$. Combined with the penalty it would give a continuous knob on the floor itself.
2. **Lower-bound regulariser $\theta_l$ (Cramer et al. 2022).** Constrains $\bar{z}$ from below so the optimiser doesn't drive cells silent on hard-to-classify samples. Useful for the tightest-$\theta_u$ no-PING cell where some accuracy is being lost to silence.
3. **Scale to full MNIST.** The 5000-sample / 40-epoch run lands no-PING at 90% accuracy; with full data, both arms should reach the ≈98% regime PING-off does in the COBA-PING baselines.

## fr-reg formulation

Two formulations of the spike-budget penalty appeared during this work; the picture above uses the population-mean formulation. The per-neuron formulation — which is what nb035/036 use on COBA-PING — destabilises PING at every $\theta_u$ value in the CUBA setting. The diagnostic is mechanical and worth recording.

The per-neuron penalty is $s_u \sum_i \text{relu}(\langle z_i\rangle - \theta_u)^2$ where the sum is over hidden neurons and $\langle z_i\rangle$ is the mean spike count of neuron $i$ across the batch. Each term in the sum has gradient $2 s_u \cdot \text{relu}(\langle z_i\rangle - \theta_u) \cdot \mathbb{1}[\langle z_i\rangle > \theta_u]$, *scaled per neuron*. Replaying the trained PING-off network on a 64-sample MNIST test batch gives the per-neuron distribution:

| Cell | mean | median | p90 | p99 | max |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PING off | 0.37 | **0.00** | **0.00** | 15.3 | 20.1 |
| no-PING off | 13.5 | 13.6 | 16.3 | 18.7 | 20.1 |

PING produces a winner-take-most code: the *median* neuron never fires on any trial, but the top percentile fires ≈15 spk/trial. The per-neuron penalty term activates *only* on those high-firing cells when $\theta_u = 5$ — exactly the cells carrying the digit-classification signal — and pushes them silent. Even though the *population* mean rate sits well below $\theta_u$, the penalty applies non-trivially because it targets the tail.

no-PING is the opposite: median ≈ mean ≈ 13.6 spk/trial, every cell firing at roughly the same rate. The per-neuron penalty distributes uniformly across the population with no single signal channel singled out, and the optimiser walks the frontier smoothly.

The same per-neuron penalty walks the COBA-PING frontier in [nb035 Figure 5](/notebooks/nb035/) because the conductance synaptic filter spreads activity across more cells — there's no sub-1% sparse selective subpopulation for the per-neuron term to concentrate on. In CUBA-PING with instant synapses, the I-loop drives the population straight to a winner-take-most code, and the per-neuron formulation becomes a selective signal-suppressor.

The population-mean formulation used in the main results above replaces the per-neuron mean with $\bar{z} = \mathbb{E}_{i, \text{batch}}[z_i]$ — a single scalar over the entire hidden population. Gradient distributes uniformly across cells, the high-firing tail is no longer preferentially targeted, and the I-loop's architectural floor coexists with the explicit penalty.
