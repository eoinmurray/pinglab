## Introduction

Single-MNIST training shows the network can recognise one digit. This entry asks what it costs to recognise a stream of them. The trial is always 200 ms; the trained single-digit readout is reused frozen, applied window by window. We sweep three axes — window length, input spikes per window, hidden spikes per window — and a single-digit latency curve. Chance is 10%; the single-digit ceiling is $\sim$88%.

## Method

cuba, coba, ping trained on standard single-MNIST per [nb010](/notebooks/nb010/), [nb011](/notebooks/nb011/), [nb012](/notebooks/nb012/), then frozen. Each trial is 200 ms; each window holds a freshly Poisson-encoded MNIST digit. Per-window predictions come from finite-differencing the trained mem-mean accumulator across window boundaries and argmax-ing through the same *W_out*. 800 trials per operating point. Four sweeps:

1. **Window length.** 200 ms / *n* with *n* ∈ {1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 10, 20, 25, 40}, 25 Hz input.
2. **Input rate.** 4 × 50 ms layout, per-pixel rate ∈ {1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 200} Hz.
3. **Hidden spikes.** Re-projects sweep 1 onto measured hidden spike count per window.
4. **Single-window latency.** One digit for the full 200 ms; running argmax scored at sixteen fractions. Mirrors [nb013's latency protocol](/notebooks/nb013/#latency-to-correct-answer-protocol).

### How many ping cycles?

ping's E→I→E loop produces gamma activity, so the natural unit of accumulation might be the *ping cycle* rather than the millisecond. A trial-averaged PSTH only sees stimulus-locked rhythm; phase-varying rhythm is invisible. We look directly: per-trial rate heatmap (top) and trial-averaged autocorrelation post-onset (bottom).

<Figure id="Figure 8" title="ping single-trial rates and trial-averaged autocorrelation" src="/figures/notebooks/nb015/per_trial_dynamics.png" alt="Top: heatmap of single-trial hidden firing rate for ping, 50 trials × 200 ms. Bottom: trial-averaged autocorrelation of the post-onset hidden rate for cuba, coba, and ping over lags 0–60 ms.">
  Top: ping's per-trial smoothed hidden rate (first 50 trials). Bursts are visible but land at different times across trials. Bottom: trial-averaged autocorrelation after dropping the 20 ms onset transient. cuba and coba decay smoothly; ping shows one small peak at lag $\sim$29 ms (height $\sim$0.06 vs lag-0 = 1.0).
</Figure>

- **One real cycle.** The onset burst at $\sim$9 ms is stimulus-locked and lifts running accuracy to {latencyAt("ping", 10)?.accuracy_pct?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}% by 10 ms.
- **Sustained rhythm, but weak and phase-incoherent.** The $\sim$29 ms ($\sim$35 Hz) autocorrelation peak is barely above noise.
- **Past cycle 1 it's integration, not counting.** The mem-mean readout averages out the dephased oscillation. Plateau is one locked cycle plus $\sim$60 ms of drift integration.

## Success criteria

- ✅ All three recover their single-digit baseline at 200 ms (cuba {meanAt(sweepCuba, 200)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%, coba {meanAt(sweepCoba, 200)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%, ping {meanAt(sweepPing, 200)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%) and degrade smoothly as the window shrinks; lowest tested point is ping at 5 ms ({meanAt(sweepPing, 5)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%, still above chance).
- ❌ ping loses its lead below 20 ms and breaks first at 5 ms — recurrent dynamics that help at long windows hurt at short ones.
- ✅ Per hidden spike, ping is $\sim$8× more efficient than cuba: {meanAt(sweepPing, 50)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}% on $\sim${hidAt(sweepPing, 50)?.toFixed(0) ?? "?"} spikes vs cuba {meanAt(sweepCuba, 50)?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}% on $\sim${hidAt(sweepCuba, 50)?.toFixed(0) ?? "?"} (50 ms window).
- ⚠ Readout needs $\sim$25–50 input spikes per window to lock on; below that everything collapses toward chance, cuba most gracefully thanks to dense intrinsic activity.
- ✅ All three reach $\geq$80% within 80 ms of a single 200 ms digit (cuba {latencyAt("cuba", 80)?.accuracy_pct?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%, coba {latencyAt("coba", 80)?.accuracy_pct?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%, ping {latencyAt("ping", 80)?.accuracy_pct?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}%); the rest just sharpens the answer.
- ⚠ ping has one stimulus-locked cycle (the $\sim$9 ms onset burst → {latencyAt("ping", 10)?.accuracy_pct?.toFixed(1) ?? "?"}% by 10 ms); the sustained $\sim$29 ms rhythm is phase-incoherent. cuba and coba have no rhythm.

## Next steps

- Train on the sequential task with a last-window loss; expect ping's short-window curve to flatten once the recurrent state is shaped by the task.
- Move to a task that forces window interaction (sum or compare two digits) so integration time becomes the bottleneck.
- Vary total trial length (100 ms, 400 ms) to separate "network refresh time" from "readout evidence time".
