## Abstract

Third of three Δt-stability notebooks. Asks: *"How quickly does each model class reach the correct answer? At what fraction of the trial does P(correct) plateau?"*

Reads the checkpoints produced by [nb013](/notebooks/nb013/); no retraining.

## Methods

For each of the 10 trained networks, run a deterministic test batch forward pass with `recording=True` to capture the per-step output logits. At a grid of trial-time fractions $f \in [0, 1]$, evaluate the model's argmax prediction *as of step $\lfloor f \cdot T_\text{steps} \rfloor$* and compare to the ground-truth label.

Rate-readout models accumulate output spikes from $t = 0$ up to the cut step; LI-readout models take the running max-over-time of the output membrane up to the cut step. The two readout types are plotted in separate rows so the qualitatively different latency profiles read clearly.
