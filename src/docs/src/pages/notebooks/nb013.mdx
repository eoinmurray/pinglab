## Introduction

The simulator's *dt* is a numerical knob, not a feature of the network — but in spiking models it leaks into the dynamics. Train at one *dt* and evaluate at another and hidden firing rates can shift by an order of magnitude with frozen weights. This notebook quantifies that leakage across five models and traces it to a single expression: the bias term scales as $1/\Delta t$ in the snnTorch family, while cuba/COBA/ping are *dt*-invariant by construction.

## Method

### Setup

Five models are trained on MNIST at two *dt* regimes ({dt1} ms and {dt2} ms), each with the per-model recipe from its calibration sibling ([nb007](/notebooks/nb007/)–[nb012](/notebooks/nb012/)). Weights are then frozen and accuracy is re-evaluated across a sweep of eval-*dt* values under three input-transport rules ([upsample / downsample / resample](#eval-time-dt-transport)). Identity transport (eval-*dt* = train-*dt*) anchors each sweep, so any change downstream is *integrator-side*.

**Models** (see [the ladder](/articles/art002/)):

- [*standard-snn*](/articles/art002/) — pinglab reimplementation of snnTorch's *snn.Leaky*. The Tutorial-3 simplification ($\Delta t = 1$, $R = 1$, $(1-\beta)$ absorbed into $W$) drops $\Delta t$ from the update, which is what produces the *dt*-leakage analysed below.
- [*snntorch-library*](/articles/art002/#snntorch-library) — snnTorch's *snn.Leaky* directly. Parity probe against *standard-snn*.
- [*cuba*](/articles/art002/) — same LIF, exp-Euler + ZOH integration restoring the $(1-\beta)/dt$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors. *dt* is back in the update.
- [*COBA*](/articles/art003/) — conductance-based LIF with exponential synapses, hard reset, refractory. COBANet with *--ei-strength 0* (feedforward-only).
- [*ping*](/articles/art003/) — COBA plus the frozen E→I→E recurrent loop that produces gamma.

### Readout

All five models use *mem-mean*: spiking output LIF, mean-of-membrane as logits. Architecture matches [snnTorch tutorial 3](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_3.html) (leaky neuron after the final projection), training signal matches [tutorial 5](https://snntorch.readthedocs.io/en/latest/tutorials/tutorial_5.html) (CE on the output membrane summed across timesteps — taken in mean form here for *dt*-invariance).

The output LIF uses the same exp-Euler + ZOH form cuba uses for its hidden membrane:

$$
v_\mathrm o^{t+1} = \beta_\mathrm o\, v_\mathrm o^{t} + \tfrac{1-\beta_\mathrm o}{\Delta t}\, W_{ff}^{[-1]} s_t + (1-\beta_\mathrm o)\, b_{ff}^{[-1]},
$$

with $\beta_\mathrm o = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\mathrm o}$, $\tau_\mathrm o = 2$ ms, threshold 1, subtract reset on spike. Output spikes are recorded but *not* used in the loss — they only bound the membrane. The trial logit is

$$
\text{logits}^{(c)} = \tfrac{1}{T}\, \sum_t v_\mathrm o^{(c)}(t)
$$

and CE applies once. Mean-of-membrane gives a dense per-step gradient signal; spike count's gradient flows only through the surrogate at threshold crossings (sparse, high-variance) and earlier *spike-count* runs of this notebook trained at chance for COBANet.

COBANet caveat: hidden firing rate ($\sim$5–30 Hz) is $\sim$10–100× lower than CUBANet's, so per-step drive into the output is correspondingly weaker. We compensate at init (*--readout-w-out-scale*: 100 for COBA, 500 for ping); see the [differences table](#differences-between-cuba-and-COBA) below.

### Differences between CUBA and COBA

Five flags differ between [*cuba*](/articles/art002/) and [*COBA*](/articles/art003/); the classes themselves differ on six more not exposed as flags.

**Recipe-level (visible in the table)**

| Difference | cuba | COBA | Effect | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *--model* | *cuba* | *ping* (with *--ei-strength 0*) | Dispatches [CUBANet](/articles/art002/) vs [COBANet](/articles/art003/). | COBA is feedforward COBANet; ping with the recurrent loop off. |
| *--lr* | 0.04 | 0.0004 | 100× spread. | $g(V - E_\text{rev})$ is far more LR-sensitive than cuba's $+I$. |
| *--readout-w-out-scale* | 1 | 100 / 500 | Multiplies *W_ff[-1]* at init. | COBANet hidden rate $\sim$10–100× below CUBANet's, so output drive is correspondingly weak. nb011 sweep: 1→59%, 10→70%, 30→74%, 100→82%. ping's inhibitory loop wants 5× more. |
| *--kaiming-init* | True | False | Signed vs half-normal init on $W_\text{in}$. | cuba is Dale-off; COBA is Dale-on (non-negative). |
| *--w-in* (mean, std) | (0.3, 0.06) | (0.3, 0.03) | std halved. | Half-normal already biases positive — narrower std avoids overdriving at *t* = 0. |
| *--w-in-sparsity* | 0.0 | 0.95 | Dense vs sparse $W_\text{in}$. | Biophysical µS scale: a few strong inputs per neuron, not dense Kaiming. |
| *--v-grad-dampen* | n/a | 1000 | Soft-clips the voltage gradient on backward. | Conductance Jacobian $\partial U / \partial g$ explodes near threshold. |

**Class-level (not exposed as flags)**

| Difference | cuba | COBA | Effect | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Membrane equation | $\tau\, dV/dt = -V + I$ | $C_m\, dV/dt = -g_L(V - E_L) - g_e(V - E_e) - g_i(V - E_i)$ | Linear additive ODE vs nonlinear multiplicative; COBA clips $V$ at $E_e$. | Headline biophysical step on [the ladder](/articles/art002/); reversal potentials, $g_e/g_i$, µS units all follow. |
| Spike reset | subtract ($U \mathrel{{-}{=}} \theta$) | hard ($V \leftarrow V_\text{reset}$) | Subtract preserves cross-spike momentum; hard reset destroys it. | Matches biological after-hyperpolarisation (the brief K⁺-driven dip below rest after a spike). |
| Refractory | none | $\tau_\text{ref}^E = 3$ ms, $\tau_\text{ref}^I = 1.5$ ms | Caps single-neuron rate at $\sim 1/\tau_\text{ref}$. | Biophysical realism + prevents runaway firing. |
| Dale's law | off | on ($W \geq 0$) | Restricts optimisation landscape. | Real cortical synapses don't switch sign. |
| Synaptic kernel | direct LIF | exponential ($\tau_\text{AMPA} = 2$, $\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms) | Each spike kicks instantaneously in cuba; in COBA it raises a decaying conductance trace. | Required for any oscillation, including PING's gamma. |
| Bias term | trainable $b_\text{ff}$ | none | cuba has a free DC offset per neuron. | A constant bias doesn't fit the biophysical form (would need a current source). |
| Units / scale | dimensionless | mV / µS / ms | Just a rescaling computationally. | Required for matching electrophysiology. Constants on [Parameters & Units](/articles/art004/). |

**What's *not* different.** Both models share the exp-Euler + ZOH integrator (with the $(1-\beta)$ prefactors that make the per-step update *dt*-invariant — the whole point of this notebook), the fast-sigmoid surrogate-gradient primitive, and the same reset-by-subtraction-and-clamp for membrane numerics outside spikes. The integrator is *not* the cuba/COBA boundary; it's what COBA inherits unchanged from cuba.

### Eval-time *dt* transport

With weights frozen, evaluating at a different *dt* needs a rule for how to rebin the input spike train. Three modes are run:

- **Upsample** (count-preserving, eval-*dt* ≤ train-*dt*) — generate Poisson at train-*dt*, then expand the time axis by $k = \Delta t_\text{ref}/\Delta t_\text{target}$, placing each reference spike at the *first* fine sub-step of its block. Total spike count exact, per-ms rate exact, per-step rate drops by $1/k$.
- **Downsample** (count-preserving, eval-*dt* ≥ train-*dt*) — generate Poisson at train-*dt*, then sum-pool blocks of $k$ steps (§2.3): split the time axis into non-overlapping windows of $k$ consecutive ref-steps, replacing each window with the sum of its values. Per-ms rate exact, total count preserved up to leftover steps trimmed when $T$ is not divisible by $k$. Output is non-binary (counts per coarse bin).
- **Resample** — draw a fresh Poisson stream at the target *dt* with the same per-ms rate. Sampling noise re-enters; works in both directions.

### Latency-to-correct-answer protocol

One forward pass per (model, train-*dt*) on a deterministic test batch with `recording=True`; the per-timestep readout state lands in *net.spike_record["out"]*, shape *(T_steps, B, N_OUT)*. For *mem-mean* the state at step $t$ is the cumulative $v_\mathrm o$ sum so far (scaled by a constant, so argmax is preserved). The anytime prediction at fraction $f$ is

$$
\text{prediction}(f) = \arg\max_c \; \sum_{t=1}^{f \cdot T} v_\mathrm o^{(c)}(t),
$$

monotonic by construction. Sweep $f$ over 20 points $\{0.05, 0.10, \ldots, 1.00\}$, compare to labels, plot $P(\text{correct} \mid f)$ — one curve per (train-*dt*, model).

The snnTorch family (blue) rises as eval-*dt* shrinks; cuba/COBA/ping stay flat. The mechanism is visible in the steady-state membrane.

Update rule, with $\beta = e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{mem}}$:

$$
U_{t+1} = \beta U_t + W s_t + b.
$$

Take the expectation at mean input rate $r$ (so $\mathbb{E}[s_t] = r\Delta t$); each step adds $Wr\Delta t + b$ on average. The fixed point is

$$
U_{ss} = \frac{Wr\Delta t + b}{1 - \beta} \approx Wr\tau + \frac{b\tau}{\Delta t}
$$

using $1-\beta \approx \Delta t/\tau$.

The spike contribution $Wr\tau$ is *dt*-invariant; the bias contribution $b\tau/\Delta t$ scales as $1/\Delta t$. Halving eval-*dt* doubles bias drive; firing rate rises with it. cuba/COBA/ping restore the missing $(1-\beta)/\Delta t$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors at integration time, giving $U_{ss} = Wr + b$ — *dt*-invariant by construction.

## Discussion

The difference between CUBA and COBA amounts to several changes at once — which ones are crucial?

**Crucial — the ones that change network behaviour**

1. **Conductance form $g \cdot (V - E_\text{rev})$ instead of additive $+I$.** Excitation saturates as $V \to E_e$ (can't drive past 0 mV); inhibition becomes shunting (raises $g_\text{tot}$, shortens effective $\tau$). CUBA has neither — additive currents drive $V$ arbitrarily, no shunting, no biophysical clipping. Everything downstream (reversal potentials, separate $g_e / g_i$ channels, units in µS) follows from this one switch.
2. **Hard reset + refractory.** Hard reset to $V_\text{reset}$ destroys cross-spike membrane momentum; refractory ($\tau_\text{ref}^E = 3$ ms, $\tau_\text{ref}^I = 1.5$ ms) puts a hard ceiling on firing rate at $\approx 1/\tau_\text{ref}$. Reset-by-subtraction (cuba's default) lets the membrane carry residual depolarisation forward, so high rates can run away. Under the uniform *mem-mean* readout this is exactly what shows up as the COBANet family's lower trial-level drive into the output LIF — it's why we have to scale *W_ff[-1]* by 100× (COBA) and 500× (ping) to recover competitive accuracy.
3. **Exponential synapses.** $\tau_\text{AMPA} = 2$ ms, $\tau_\text{GABA} = 9$ ms. Low-passes the presynaptic spike train into the membrane drive and separates fast-E from slow-I — required for any oscillation, including PING. Standard in COBA.

**Secondary — change the *constraints*, not the dynamics**

4. **Dale's law** ($W \geq 0$). A regulariser on the optimiser, not a dynamics change. Off in cuba, on in COBA.
5. **Biophysical units** (mV, µS, ms with realistic constants). Required for matching electrophysiology; computationally just a rescaling.

**Not actually different**

6. **Integrator.** Both cuba and COBA use exp-Euler + ZOH with $(1 - \beta)$ prefactors built into the update — that's what makes both *dt*-invariant in this notebook. The integrator is *not* the cuba/COBA boundary; it's what COBA inherits unchanged from cuba.
7. **Surrogate-gradient spike primitive.** Same fast-sigmoid in both.

If you had to keep one: conductance form (1). Without it there's no shunting and no biophysics — *cuba-with-hard-reset-and-refractory* is just a stiffer LIF.
