Three of those customisations are what let *standard-snn* train at this *dt*/*T* combination; the others (model, dataset, hidden size, tier-driven schedule) are routine.

- [**readout = li**](/the-oscilloscope/#readouts) — the default *rate* readout accumulates hidden spikes across *T* = 2000 steps and produces logits large enough that CE saturates to zero on the forward pass, masking the real gradient signal.
- [**surrogate-slope = 1**](/the-oscilloscope/#network-flags-shared) — the global default slope = 5 × 2000 timesteps of BPTT overflows fp32 in the *W_ff* input-layer gradient (*finite_max* ≈ 9 × 10³⁷ then NaN). Slope = 1 bounds the fast-sigmoid pseudo-derivative so grads stay finite end-to-end; the *skipped_steps* counter goes from 7/7 to 0/7.
- [**kaiming-init (signed)**](/the-oscilloscope/#network-flags-shared) — signed Kaiming on *W_in* is what gives the hidden layer enough starting drive to spike at all; without it the default non-negative init leaves *rate_e* ≈ 0.

"?"}% with no late-epoch collapse and hidden rate back inside the 1–200 Hz band — all six gate criteria pass. The intermediate **medium** tier (2000 × 10) had scraped to ~80% but overshot the rate ceiling at 205 Hz; the extra 30 epochs at the same $\eta$ let the readout pull the hidden population back down to ~125 Hz without losing accuracy, so the rate drift was a short-run transient rather than the recipe being genuinely unstable.
