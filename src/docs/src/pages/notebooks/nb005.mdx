## Abstract

Gamma has a period of $\sim$15–30 ms, so a coarse integration step *dt* can't resolve the E→I→E round-trip. With the Poisson input overdrive pinned well past the PING threshold from [nb003](/notebooks/nb003/) (so the rhythm would be robust at fine *dt*), this notebook sweeps *dt* — isolating the timestep as the only knob that can break the oscillation. Companion sweeps: [nb003](/notebooks/nb003/) (drive) and [nb006](/notebooks/nb006/) (E→I coupling). See [The Notebooks](/docs/the-notebooks/) for how this entry's runner/artifact/figure triple is wired up.

## Methods

A scan over the [oscilloscope *video*](/docs/the-oscilloscope/#subcommand-video) subcommand with stim-overdrive held at {scan.fixed_overdrive}× (firmly inside the stable-PING regime mapped by [nb003](/notebooks/nb003/)) while *dt* sweeps {scan.min} → {scan.max} ms over {scan.frames} frames. All knobs are hardcoded literals in *src/pinglab/notebooks/nb005.py* per the [runner contract](/docs/the-notebooks/#runner-contract).

## Next steps

The breakdown around *dt* ≈ 1.65 ms sets a hard ceiling on the timestep any downstream training entry can use without losing the rhythm. Whether this same ceiling holds end-to-end through training — whether a model trained at *dt* near the limit still produces PING at inference — is a separate, open question. The breakdown mode here (saturation, not collapse) suggests the failure is integrator instability rather than loss of the rhythm itself; a side-by-side with explicit-Euler vs the current scheme would isolate that.
