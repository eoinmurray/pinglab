Gamma has a period of $\sim$15–30 ms, so a coarse integration step *dt* can't resolve the E→I→E round-trip. With the Poisson input overdrive pinned well past the PING threshold (so the rhythm would be robust at fine *dt*), this notebook sweeps *dt* from {scan.min} to {scan.max} ms — isolating the timestep as the only knob that can break the oscillation. Split out from [nb002](/notebooks/nb002/); companion dt-stability training experiment is [nb010](/notebooks/nb010/).

<Figure type="video" id="Figure 1" title="Integration-step sweep (scan_dt.mp4)" src="/figures/notebooks/nb003/scan_dt.mp4">
  Each frame is a fresh {cfg.sim_ms} ms sim on MNIST digit {input.digit}, sample {input.sample} with stim-overdrive held at {scan.fixed_overdrive}×. *dt* sweeps {scan.min} → {scan.max} ms over {scan.frames} frames.

  Stable until about *dt* = 1.65 ms; beyond that the whole population saturates after PING onset.
</Figure>
