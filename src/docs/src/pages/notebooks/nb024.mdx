## Introduction

Three rungs of the biophysical ladder — [cuba](/docs/models/#cubanet), [coba](/docs/models/#cobanet) and [ping](/docs/models/#ping) — are trained on MNIST with their calibrated nb010 / nb011 / nb012 recipes. The headline deliverable is a single bar chart with two y-axes: final-epoch test accuracy on the left, mean hidden-E firing rate on the right (Figure 1).

Each model is also re-trained at five values of the upper-bound spike-budget regulariser $\theta_u$ (off + 5 finite values). The frontier those $(\text{acc}, \text{rate})$ pairs trace out (Figure 3) characterises where each model can be pushed in the accuracy/rate plane. This subsumes the now-retired nb020.

## Method

Three cells per model, six $\theta_u$ values each (18 cells total):

- *cuba* — [cuba](/docs/models/#cubanet) with *--kaiming-init*, *--lr=0.04*, *--readout=mem-mean*.
- *coba* — [coba](/docs/models/#cobanet) recipe (*--ei-strength=0*, *--w-in=0.3*, *--lr=0.0004*, *--readout-w-out-scale=100*).
- *ping* — [ping](/docs/models/#ping) recipe (*--ei-strength=1*, *--w-in=1.2*, *--lr=0.0004*, *--readout-w-out-scale=500*).

$\theta_u$ grid (spikes/trial): *off, 5, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.2*. At $T = 200$ ms these correspond to *off, 25, 10, 5, 2.5, 1* Hz, with regulariser strength $\lambda_u = 10^{-3}$ held fixed.

Every other knob (surrogate slope, batch size, dt, t-ms, seed=42) is held fixed at the per-model calibration. Training uses the [oscilloscope](/docs/the-oscilloscope/) directly via [BatchDispatcher](/docs/the-oscilloscope/#modal-dispatch).

### COBA → PING weight transfer

The *coba__off__seed42* baseline is re-evaluated with a fresh ping-arch I-loop at 11 values of *--ei-strength* from 0.0 to 1.0. *W_ff* and *W_ee* load from the trained COBA checkpoint; *W_ei* and *W_ie* are *re-initialised* at the inference-time ei_strength so each sweep point has a genuinely active I-loop (the trained-coba checkpoint has W_ei = W_ie = 0, which would otherwise nullify the loop at any strength).

<Figure id="Figure 7" title="Transfer accuracy across the I-loop sweep" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ei_acc_sweep.png" alt="Test accuracy versus inference-time ei_strength on COBA-trained weights. Dotted lines mark COBA baseline and 10% chance.">
  Test accuracy vs inference-time *ei_strength*. Dotted lines: COBA baseline and 10% chance. Accuracy stays within $\sim 10$ pp of baseline across the full sweep — the trained feedforward weights transfer cleanly to PING dynamics at inference.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 8" title="E and I rates across the I-loop sweep" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ei_rates_sweep.png" alt="Mean E (hidden) and I (inhibitory) population firing rates per test pass versus ei_strength.">
  Mean E and I population rates per test pass vs *ei_strength*. E rate falls as the loop is dialled up; I rate rises from zero.
</Figure>

## Discussion

- **Accuracy gap.** All three models converge to within $\sim 2$ pp at θ_u = off; the headline claim is that the ladder is *accuracy-equivalent* but not *rate-equivalent*.
- **Rate at headline accuracy.** CUBA and COBA settle at $\sim 85$ Hz; PING settles at $\sim 5$ Hz, an order of magnitude lower with the same accuracy.
- **Frontier shape.** Adding the $\theta_u$ regulariser to CUBA drags its rate down to PING-like values but at a $\sim 10$ pp accuracy cost; PING's frontier is flat because its baseline already sits in the budget regime.
- **PING is the architecture that's already biological.** No regulariser needed to reach gamma-band firing rates.

## Next steps

- Extend the $\theta_u$ grid downward (e.g. 0.1, 0.05) to find where every model breaks.
- Add a *coba-with-loop* cell (active recurrent inhibition but trained as coba) to disentangle "the loop on" from "the loop being trained against".
- Repeat at *large* tier on Modal to tighten the per-point variance.
