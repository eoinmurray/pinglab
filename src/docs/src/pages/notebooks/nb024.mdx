## Summary

Same MNIST accuracy, ~16× fewer spikes. Coba (no I-loop) settles at ~80 Hz hidden firing; ping (E↔I loop wired up, frozen) settles at ~5 Hz with comparable accuracy. A spike-budget regulariser drags coba toward ping-like rates at an accuracy cost; ping's frontier is flat — it's already at its floor. **The floor is dynamical, not informational**: hidden-spike drop shows the readout works at ~1 Hz, but the gamma loop's preferred cycle period (τ_AMPA + τ_GABA ≈ 11 ms) keeps the network from going there spontaneously.

## Method

Two COBANet recipes, identical except for `ei_strength`:

- **coba** — `--ei-strength 0`, `--w-in 0.3`, `--readout-w-out-scale 100`.
- **ping** — `--ei-strength 1`, `--w-in 1.2`, `--readout-w-out-scale 500`.

E↔I matrices in ping are wired at init and held `requires_grad=False`. Same surrogate, lr, dt, seed everywhere. Spike-budget regulariser θ_u ∈ {off, 5, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.2} spikes/trial (12 cells total).

**Hidden-spike perturbation.** A per-step callback rewrites the emitted E and I spike vectors *inside* the forward loop, so the I-feedback and readout both react within the same trial. Two modes:

- *drop* — each emitted spike kept w.p. $1 - p_\text{drop}$.
- *add* — each silent slot turns to a spike w.p. $r_\text{add} \cdot \Delta t / 1000$.

Both populations get the same statistics. Full MNIST test set per (model, mode, level).

## Discussion

The drop/add asymmetry separates two candidate explanations of ping's rate floor:

1. **Information-bound** — the readout needs ~3–5 Hz of spikes to classify. Drop refutes this: ping still classifies at ~73% with 90% of its emitted spikes thrown away.
2. **Dynamics-bound** — the gamma loop has a preferred cycle period of $\tau_\text{AMPA} + \tau_\text{GABA} \approx 11$ ms; the readout is phase-locked to it. Add confirms this: extra spikes at the wrong phase corrupt the readout's signal, even though they *raise* the E rate. Coba has no phase to be off of, so add degrades smoothly.

The rate floor is the loop's natural cadence, not what the task requires. The spike-budget penalty has no leverage because pushing the rate lower would require the loop to oscillate slower than its time constants permit. "Inhibition does sparsity" here is a physical consequence of the cycle period, not an optimisation preference.

**Predictions for follow-up.** Lengthening $\tau_\text{GABA}$ at inference should lower the floor; shortening it should raise it. Decoupling the readout from gamma phase (e.g. mem-mean over many cycles) should trade sparsity for additive-noise robustness.

## Limitations

Small tier only (max_samples = 500, epochs = 5); absolute accuracies are below state-of-the-art. MNIST only. Sweep cells single-seed; baselines 3-seed. Drop/add applies the same statistic to E and I — a population-resolved version would isolate which population's timing the readout uses.
