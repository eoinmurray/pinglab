<Figure id="Figure 5" title="Trained coba raster (no I-loop)" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/raster__coba.png" alt="Spike raster of trained coba showing dense asynchronous E firing and an empty I row.">
  **How.** Same protocol as Figure 4 on the trained coba baseline (*--ei-strength 0*).
  **What.** Asynchronous-dense E activity at ≈52 Hz; I population silent because the loop is disabled. No gamma cycle, no phase structure — the readout integrates over uniform firing.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 6" title="Hidden-spike perturbation" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/perturbation_curves.png" alt="Test accuracy vs p(drop) (left) and vs Poisson-add rate (right) per model.">
  **How.** Attach a per-step callback to the trained network's forward loop that rewrites the emitted E and I spike vectors. *Drop* keeps each emitted spike with probability $1 - p_\text{drop}$; *add* injects a fresh Bernoulli spike per silent slot at Poisson rate $r_\text{add} \cdot \Delta t / 1000$. Both populations get the same statistics; I-feedback and readout react within the same trial. 44 forward passes total (2 models × 2 modes × 11 levels).
  **What.** Asymmetric. Under drop, ping holds 73% at $p_\text{drop} = 0.9$ — the readout is happy at a tenth of ping's natural firing rate. Under add, ping collapses to chance at 10 Hz of injected noise; coba degrades smoothly (84% → 67% across 0–100 Hz). The readout cares about *which spikes* survive (drop preserves the gamma cycle) and is destroyed by spikes that arrive at the wrong phase (add injects them anywhere).
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 7" title="Trained ping — accuracy and E rate vs τ_GABA" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/tau_gaba_sweep.png" alt="Red curve: E firing rate falls from ≈7 Hz at 4.5 ms to ≈2.4 Hz at 27 ms. Black curve: accuracy holds 80–86%.">
  **How.** Load the trained ping checkpoint. For each $\tau_\text{GABA} \in \{4.5, 6, 9, 12, 18, 27\}$ ms, mutate the module-level decay constant before the forward pass; evaluate on the full MNIST test set. 6 forward passes; no retraining.
  **What.** The rate floor moves monotonically with cycle period: ≈7 Hz at 4.5 ms → ≈5 Hz at the trained 9 ms → ≈2.4 Hz at 27 ms. Accuracy holds 80–86% across the whole sweep. **Direct confirmation that the floor is set by $\tau_\text{AMPA} + \tau_\text{GABA}$** — shortening GABA tightens the loop and produces more cycles per second; lengthening stretches the cycle and produces fewer.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 8" title="Trained ping — accuracy and E rate vs uniform threshold offset" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/threshold_shift_sweep.png" alt="Red curve: E rate rises from 4.7 Hz to ≈7 Hz at +10 mV then falls. Black curve: accuracy holds 80–86%.">
  **How.** Structure-preserving alternative to add. Monkey-patch the module's *spike_biophysical* to add a constant offset to the LIF firing threshold across both E and I; set PINGLAB_NO_COMPILE = 1 so the patch reaches the forward path. Sweep the offset across $\{0, 2.5, 5, 7.5, 10, 15\}$ mV. 6 forward passes.
  **What.** E rate doesn't fall — it *rises* from 4.7 to 7.0 Hz before eventually collapsing. The I population fires ≈5× higher baseline (23 vs 4.7 Hz), so the offset suppresses I first; with the inhibitory grip loosened, E fires more freely. Beyond +15 mV both populations are saturated-suppressed. **Accuracy holds 80–86% across the whole sweep** — in sharp contrast to Figure 6's add curve which collapsed at 10 Hz. The readout is robust to rate changes that preserve gamma phase, brittle to noise that breaks it.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 9" title="Input-rate sweep on trained ping" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/rate_sweep__ping.mp4" type="video" alt="Oscilloscope scope-frame video sweeping input Poisson rate from 0 to 100 Hz on trained ping with MNIST digit 0.">
  **How.** Replay the trained ping baseline through the oscilloscope's video subcommand with *--scan-var spike_rate* over [0, 100] Hz × 40 frames on MNIST digit 0 sample 0. 400 ms per frame; rendered to mp4.
  **What.** At zero drive the network is silent. As the input rate crosses a recruitment threshold the gamma loop engages and locks; once locked, the loop's cycle period stays roughly constant across the rest of the sweep — the loop's natural cadence is invariant to input strength once it's running.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 10" title="Coba → ping I-loop transfer at inference" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ei_rasters.png" alt="Stacked raster panels showing trained coba replayed with increasing inference-time ei_strength.">
  **How.** Take the trained coba checkpoint (W_ei = W_ie = 0 from init, never updated because *--ei-strength 0*). Build a fresh ping network at six inference-time ei_strength values; load W_ff and W_ee from the coba checkpoint, *skip* W_ei/W_ie so the freshly-initialised PING I-loop survives. Run forward over one MNIST digit; plot rasters.
  **What.** At *ei = 0* the asynchronous-dense pattern of trained coba is preserved. By *ei ≈ 0.4* the same feedforward weights produce gamma-band E-then-I cycles. The PING dynamics (loop + rate suppression) emerge from the inhibitory architecture itself, not from training — the same trained weights that fired at 52 Hz with no loop fire at ≈5 Hz with the loop wired up at inference.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 11" title="ei_strength sweep — accuracy and rates" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ei_acc_sweep.png" alt="Test accuracy vs inference-time ei_strength on coba-trained weights, with horizontal lines at the trained coba baseline and at 10% chance.">
  **How.** Same inference-only setup as Figure 10, but on the full test set: 11 ei_strength points from 0.0 to 1.0; record accuracy at each.
  **What.** Accuracy holds within ≈10 pp of the coba baseline across the whole range. The trained feedforward weights transfer cleanly to PING dynamics at inference — neither the loop's gamma cadence nor the rate suppression breaks classification.
</Figure>

<Figure id="Figure 12" title="ei_strength sweep — E and I rates" src="/figures/notebooks/nb024/ei_rates_sweep.png" alt="Mean E (hidden) and I (inhibitory) population firing rates per test pass versus ei_strength.">
  **How.** Same sweep as Figure 11, additionally recording the mean hidden-E and inhibitory-I rates from the spike record.
  **What.** E rate falls monotonically as the loop is dialled up — from ≈52 Hz at ei = 0 (the coba baseline) to ≈5 Hz at ei = 1 (the ping rate). I rate rises from zero to ≈23 Hz. The rate suppression is a continuous function of the I-loop's authority over E.
</Figure>
