A scope frame is the canonical oscilloscope layout — the multi-panel figure that every video in this notebook is a stack of. This entry holds a single still rendering of it at a fixed working point so the layout can be iterated on for aesthetics independently of sweep content.

## Panels

- **Header** — model name, integration step, population size, input rate on the top row; live readouts (E/I firing rates, fundamental frequency f₀, population CV, I/E ratio, active-neuron fraction) on the second row.
- **E Neurons** — raster of excitatory-population spike times over the full simulation window. The dominant 2×2 block of the figure because rhythm is the thing we're measuring.
- **Input** — the MNIST digit being shown to the network, rendered at native pixel resolution.
- **Weights** — histograms of every weight matrix the model owns (*In*, *Out*, *E→E*, *E→I*, *I→E*). Lets you see at a glance whether a matrix has collapsed, saturated, or drifted out of scale.
- **Input Spikes** — raster of the Poisson-encoded spike train hitting the network. A dense band during the 200–300 ms stimulus window corresponds to the pixel intensities of the digit.
- **I Neurons** — raster of inhibitory-population spike times, same axes as the E raster. The I population is 1/4 the size of E and fires on an offset phase from E, producing the gamma rhythm.
- **Output (logits)** — horizontal bar chart of the 10 class logits at the final timestep. The predicted class (argmax) is filled red; the rest are outlined.
- **PSD** — power spectral density of the E-population rate, restricted to 5–200 Hz. The red vertical marker is the gamma peak frequency f₀.
- **E Population Rate** — binned firing-rate trace of the E population over time (2 ms bins). The envelope that the PSD is computed from.
