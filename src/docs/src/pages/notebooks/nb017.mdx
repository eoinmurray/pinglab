### Population rate

Replay the same Poisson input through a fresh ping network at each frame's *ei* value, record the E spike train $s_{i,t}$, and bin at $\Delta_\text{bin} = 2$ ms,

$$
r_k \;=\; \frac{1000}{N_E \, \Delta_\text{bin}} \sum_{i=1}^{N_E} \sum_{t \in \mathrm{bin}_k} s_{i, t}
\quad \text{(Hz)}.
$$

Discard the first {numbers.autocorr_metric?.onset_skip_ms ?? "20"} ms onset transient before the downstream metrics; the full trace is shown in the stacked plot.

### Autocorrelation

The candidate single-frame metric is the **autocorrelation peak** at non-zero lag ÔÇö async noise has none, a periodic burst train has a tall, sharp one at the gamma period. Let $r_k$ be the post-onset rate ($k = 0, \dots, N-1$), with mean $\bar r$ and standard deviation $\sigma_r$. Z-score and autocorrelate via FFT:

$$
\tilde r_k \;=\; \frac{r_k - \bar r}{\sigma_r}, \qquad
C(\ell) \;=\; \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k} \tilde r_k \, \tilde r_{k+\ell}.
$$

Z-scoring puts every frame's trace on the same dimensionless scale so the autocorrelation reads out *shape*, not amplitude, and pins $C(0) = 1$. The metric is the *prominence* of the strongest peak in the lag window {numbers.autocorr_metric?.lag_min_ms ?? "5"}ÔÇô{numbers.autocorr_metric?.lag_max_ms ?? "60"} ms,

$$
\mathrm{prom}(\ell^\star) \;=\; C(\ell^\star) \;-\; \min_{\ell \in [\ell_l, \ell_r]} C(\ell),
$$

with $\ell^\star$ the highest-prominence peak and $[\ell_l, \ell_r]$ the interval bounded by its nearest valleys (scipy's *find_peaks* convention).

### Bayes factor

The two metrics above are descriptive ÔÇö they summarise the trace but don't say "this is ping with probability $P$". The Bayesian alternative is to fit two generative recipes to each trace and report the log-odds. We work on the raster directly (per-neuron spike times $\{t_{j,1} < \dots < t_{j,n_j}\}$ for $j = 1, \dots, N_E$ over the post-onset window $[0, T]$). Both recipes match the validated step-1a sims rather than the simpler Poisson placeholders.

**Async** is a per-neuron gamma-renewal process. The generative recipe is:

1. Pick parameters: shape $k$, scale $\theta$, so each neuron's mean firing rate is $1/(k\theta)$ and the ISI coefficient of variation is $1/\sqrt{k}$.
2. For each neuron $j = 1, \dots, N_E$, independently:
   - Draw a starting offset $u_j \sim \mathrm{Uniform}(0, k\theta)$ (random initial phase).
   - Set $t_{j,1} = u_j$.
   - For $i = 2, 3, \dots$, draw $\tau_{j,i} \sim \Gamma(k, \theta)$ and set $t_{j,i} = t_{j,i-1} + \tau_{j,i}$, until $t_{j,i} > T$.

$k = 1$ recovers Poisson; $k = 10$ matches the visible *ei* = 0 stripe pattern (CV $\approx$ 0.32). Conditional on the inner ISIs $\tau_{j,i} = t_{j,i} - t_{j,i-1}$ for $i \ge 2$, the log-likelihood is

$$
\log P(D \mid \text{async}, k, \theta) \;=\; \sum_{j,\, i \ge 2} \big[(k - 1)\log\tau_{j,i} \;-\; \tau_{j,i}/\theta \;-\; k\log\theta \;-\; \log\Gamma(k)\big].
$$

To see where this comes from, start with the gamma probability density of one inter-spike interval,

$$
f_\Gamma(\tau; k, \theta) \;=\; \frac{\tau^{k-1} \, e^{-\tau/\theta}}{\theta^{k} \, \Gamma(k)}, \qquad \tau > 0,
$$

whose logarithm is the bracketed expression in the sum: the first three terms come from the $\tau^{k-1}$, $e^{-\tau/\theta}$, and $\theta^{-k}$ factors of the density; the last is the gamma-function normaliser. The renewal property says that ISIs are independent draws from this density, so across one neuron $j$ the joint log-likelihood of the inner ISIs $\tau_{j,2}, \tau_{j,3}, \dots$ is just the sum $\sum_{i \ge 2} \log f_\Gamma(\tau_{j,i})$. Different neurons are independent renewal processes, so the joint log-likelihood across all $N_E$ neurons sums over $j$ as well, giving the double sum above.

The $i \ge 2$ constraint is deliberate: the *first* spike $t_{j,1}$ is not the end of an ISI but a forward-recurrence time relative to a fictional spike at $t = 0$, so it belongs to a different distribution. Likewise, the post-last-spike interval $T - t_{j, n_j}$ is right-censored ÔÇö we know the next spike came after $T$ but not when. Boundary terms ÔÇö first-spike forward-recurrence density and final censored survival ÔÇö drop out as a per-neuron $O(1)$ offset, small against the $O(\sum_j n_j)$ inner-ISI bulk. At fixed $k$ the MLE for $\theta$ is closed form, $\hat\theta = \bar\tau / k$ with $\bar\tau$ the global mean inner ISI; we then marginalise over $k$ on a grid spanning Poisson to highly regular.

**Ping** is an inhomogeneous Poisson process with a **von Mises rhythm-modulated** rate, the natural circular analog of a sinusoidal modulation. The generative recipe (used for the *likelihood*) is:

1. Pick parameters: baseline per-neuron rate $\lambda_0 > 0$, concentration $\kappa \ge 0$, frequency $f$, phase $\phi \in [0, 2\pi)$. Define the time-varying rate
$$
\lambda(t) \;=\; \lambda_0 \cdot \frac{e^{\kappa \cos(2\pi f t - \phi)}}{I_0(\kappa)},
$$
where $I_0$ is the modified Bessel function of the first kind. By construction the cycle-average of $\lambda(t)$ equals $\lambda_0$.
2. For each neuron $j$, sample spike times as an inhomogeneous Poisson process with intensity $\lambda(t)$.

$\kappa = 0$ gives $\lambda(t) = \lambda_0$ (since $I_0(0) = 1$), collapsing ping to homogeneous Poisson ÔÇö so async (at $k = 1$) is exactly nested inside ping. Large $\kappa$ concentrates probability mass sharply at the burst phase. Pooling across neurons, the population intensity is $N_E \lambda(t)$, and the joint log-likelihood of the pooled spike times $\{t_i\}$ is the standard inhomogeneous-Poisson form

$$
\log P(D \mid \text{ping}, \lambda_0, \kappa, f, \phi)
\;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{n_\text{tot}} \log\!\big[N_E \lambda(t_i)\big] \;-\; N_E \int_0^T \lambda(t)\, dt.
$$

Profiling the baseline gives $\hat\lambda_0 = n_\text{tot}/(N_E T)$, and the integral becomes $n_\text{tot}$ up to end-effect corrections of order $1/(fT)$. Defining the spike-time resultant length at frequency $f$ as

$$
R(f) \;=\; \Big|\sum_i e^{i \, 2\pi f t_i}\Big|
\;=\; \sqrt{\big(\textstyle\sum_i \cos 2\pi f t_i\big)^2 + \big(\sum_i \sin 2\pi f t_i\big)^2},
$$

the phase $\phi$ marginalisation under a uniform $[0, 2\pi)$ prior is *closed form* via Bessel:

$$
\int_0^{2\pi} e^{\kappa R \cos(\phi - \phi^\star)} \tfrac{d\phi}{2\pi}
\;=\; I_0(\kappa R),
$$

leaving the $\phi$-marginalised log-likelihood

$$
\ell(\kappa, f) \;=\; n_\text{tot}\log\hat\lambda_0 \;-\; n_\text{tot}
\;+\; \log I_0\!\big(\kappa R(f)\big) \;-\; n_\text{tot}\log I_0(\kappa).
$$

The remaining nuisance parameters get gridded: $\kappa$ on a logarithmic grid spanning ~0.01 to ~1000, $f$ on a linear grid in the gamma band. The von Mises form has built-in resistance to over-fitting on null data: at random data $R \sim \sqrt{n_\text{tot}}$, the maximum-likelihood $\hat\kappa$ is small and the rhythm gain $\log I_0(\kappa R) - n_\text{tot}\log I_0(\kappa)$ stays $O(1)$.

A note on simulator vs likelihood: the visualisation in Figure 5 below uses a different generative form for ping ÔÇö a marked point process with population synchrony (shared burst schedule, Bernoulli participation, Gaussian jitter) ÔÇö because that form *visually* matches real PING rasters. The BF math uses the von Mises rhythm-Poisson because it has a clean Poisson nested limit ($\kappa \to 0$), closed-form phase marginalisation, and well-behaved null evidence. The mismatch (sharp coupled real bursts vs smoothed-rate ping model) means the BF *under*-credits real ping rather than over-credits noise ÔÇö a conservative bias.

The Bayes factor is the ratio of marginalised likelihoods,

$$
\log\mathrm{BF} \;=\; \log P(D \mid \text{ping}) \;-\; \log P(D \mid \text{async}),
$$

each side integrated over its model parameters (Laplace around the MLEs for the continuous scale parameters, grid for the discrete priors). Ping has more degrees of freedom than async, so marginalisation imposes an automatic Occam penalty ÔÇö ping only beats async when the data demand the burst structure. $\log\mathrm{BF}$ scales linearly with total spike count $\sum_j n_j$, so a 250 ms trace from $N_E$ = 512 neurons at $\sim$10 Hz already carries $\sim$1280 spikes of evidence.

The per-frame output is $\log\mathrm{BF}$ in nats (positive favours ping, zero undecided), converted to $P(\text{ping} \mid D) = \sigma(\log\mathrm{BF})$ under equal prior odds.

The async-regime classification is unambiguous (probability pinned at 0 with thousands of nats of evidence below threshold). The PING-regime saturation is noisy: real PING data carries surprisingly modest BF evidence (a few hundred nats per trace) compared to clean synthetic ping at matched parameters (~700 nats). The gap is real network structure that neither sinusoidal nor von Mises rhythm models capture ÔÇö phase wander between bursts, occasional missed bursts, refractoriness. The conservative bias means the BF is honest about *its* uncertainty rather than reading high confidence from a misspecified model fit. To get smooth saturation across PING you'd need either a more sophisticated rhythm model (e.g. coupled-oscillator marked-point process ÔÇö losing the closed-form Bessel marginalisation), longer trace lengths to accumulate more spikes per frame, or accept that log-BF itself (top panel) is the cleaner-reading metric since the sigmoid step at zero is the only thing producing the bimodality.
