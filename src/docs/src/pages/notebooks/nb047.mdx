## Abstract

How many inhibitory cells does a PING network need, and how should the $I \to E$ synapses scale with that number? We sweep $N_I$ from 0% to 25% of the excitatory pool ($N_E = 1024$ fixed) on an untrained network and find the per-cell rates obey a conservation law. A quasi-static reduction of one E cell names the conserved quantity: $g_i^\star$, the inhibitory conductance at which the cell is released from the shunt — built from intrinsic constants and the drive alone, independent of $N_I$ and of the per-edge weight. The gamma rate then turns on the **per-event shunt** $W^{IE}$ relative to $g_i^\star$, not on the time-averaged total inhibition. This predicts — and the three sweeps confirm — that **constant per-edge** weights hold the rate flat across the sweep even as the summed drive varies ≈ 250×, while synaptic ($1/N$) and critical-balance ($1/\sqrt{N}$) scaling inflate the per-event shunt at small $N_I$ and drop the rate. $N_I$ is a gauge knob: physical only when a scaling ties it to $W^{IE}$.

## Methods

Reduce to one E cell — the COBANet membrane ([ar003](/articles/ar003/), eq 4) with the feedforward excitation written as a tonic current $I_\text{ext}$, as in the DMFT reduction of [nb033](/notebooks/nb033/):

$$
C_m \dot V = -g_L(V - E_L) - g_i(t)\,(V - E_i) + I_\text{ext},
\tag{1}
$$

with $g_i(t)$ the total inhibitory conductance onto the cell and $I_\text{ext}$ the quasi-steady excitatory drive. When $g_i$ varies slowly against the membrane time constant the voltage tracks its instantaneous fixed point — the $V_\infty$ and total conductance $g_\text{tot}$ of [ar003](/articles/ar003/) (eqs 11–13):

$$
V_\infty(t) = \frac{g_L E_L + g_i(t)\,E_i + I_\text{ext}}{g_\text{tot}}, \qquad g_\text{tot} = g_L + g_i(t),
\tag{2}
$$

so the cell fires while $V_\infty \ge V_\text{th}$ and is silenced below it. Setting $V_\infty = V_\text{th}$, and writing $I_\text{th} \equiv g_L(V_\text{th} - E_L)$ for the threshold-holding current of [nb050](/notebooks/nb050/), the inhibitory conductance at which firing is *released* is

$$
g_i^\star = \frac{I_\text{ext} - I_\text{th}}{V_\text{th} - E_i}.
\tag{3}
$$

$g_i^\star$ is built from intrinsic constants and the drive alone — **it contains neither $N_I$ nor $W^{IE}$.** It is the level the cycle is pinned to.

The inhibitory conductance onto the cell is ar003's exponential GABA synapse ([ar003](/articles/ar003/), eq 8) — one $\delta$-kick per inhibitory spike, decay-then-add (a spike adds its full weight $W^{IE}$):

$$
\dot g_i = -\frac{g_i}{\tau_\text{GABA}} + W^{IE}\!\sum_{k=1}^{N_I}\sum_{m}\delta(t - t_k^m).
$$

This linear first-order ODE integrates termwise: each kick at $t_k^m$ adds $W^{IE}$ and decays as $e^{-(t - t_k^m)/\tau_\text{GABA}}$ thereafter, so the solution is the Green's-function form

$$
g_i(t) = \sum_{k=1}^{N_I} W^{IE} \sum_{m} e^{-(t - t_k^m)/\tau_\text{GABA}}\,\Theta(t - t_k^m).
\tag{4}
$$

To get the cycle clock, follow (4) after one inhibitory volley. Write $g_i^\text{pk} = \max_t g_i(t)$ for the peak conductance at the crest of the volley (time $t_\text{pk}$) — the sum of the fresh $W^{IE}$ kicks from the I cells that just fired. With no further spikes (4) is then a single decaying exponential from that peak,

$$
g_i(t) = g_i^\text{pk}\,e^{-(t - t_\text{pk})/\tau_\text{GABA}},
$$

and the cell is released the moment this falls back through $g_i^\star$ (eq 3). Setting $g_i^\text{pk}\,e^{-T_\text{sup}/\tau_\text{GABA}} = g_i^\star$ and solving for the elapsed time $T_\text{sup}$ gives the suppression interval

$$
T_\text{sup} = \tau_\text{GABA} \,\ln\!\frac{g_i^\text{pk}}{g_i^\star},
\tag{5}
$$

which with the period $T \approx T_\text{build} + T_\text{sup}$ fixes $r_E \propto 1/T$. The mean-field convention instead conserves the time-averaged total drive,

$$
\langle g_i\rangle = N_I\,W^{IE}\,r_I\,\tau_\text{GABA},
\tag{6}
$$

but (5) shows the rate is set by the ratio $g_i^\text{pk}/g_i^\star$, not by $\langle g_i\rangle$.

**Predictions.** A self-organized PING recruits I cells only until $g_i$ crosses $g_i^\star$ and E shuts off, so the volley overshoots by about one quantum, $g_i^\text{pk} \approx g_i^\star + W^{IE}$, and (5) collapses to

$$
T_\text{sup} \approx \tau_\text{GABA} \,\ln\!\left(1 + \frac{W^{IE}}{g_i^\star}\right).
\tag{7}
$$

The suppression — hence the rate — depends only on the per-event jump $W^{IE}$ relative to the conserved $g_i^\star$, never on $N_I$ or the summed $\langle g_i\rangle$. Each scaling therefore predicts a distinct $r_E(N_I)$:

| scaling | per-event $W^{IE}$ | summed $\langle g_i\rangle$ | predicted $r_E(N_I)$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| constant per-edge | $W_\text{ref}$ (fixed) | $\propto N_I$ (≈ 250×) | **flat** |
| $1/N$ | $W_\text{ref}\,256/N_I$ | fixed | drops at small $N_I$ — steepest |
| $1/\sqrt N$ | $W_\text{ref}\sqrt{256/N_I}$ | $\propto \sqrt{N_I}$ | drops at small $N_I$ — milder |

Constant per-edge fixes the per-event jump, so (7) predicts the rate is **invariant** across the sweep even though $\langle g_i\rangle$ varies ≈ 250×. The $1/N$ and $1/\sqrt N$ scalings inflate $W^{IE}$ as $N_I$ shrinks — by $256\times$ and $16\times$ at $N_I = 1$ — so a single event overshoots $g_i^\star$ and adds $\tau_\text{GABA}\ln(W^{IE}/g_i^\star)$ to the suppression, lowering $r_E$ logarithmically (a gentle, saturating falloff). The results test these predictions in turn.

## Results

### Constant per-edge

As predicted, the rates are **flat** across the sweep — eq (7) with $W^{IE}$ fixed — holding to within 1% even as the summed drive $\langle g_i\rangle$ varies ≈ 250× (eq 6).

### Synaptic normalization ($1/N$)

As predicted, the rates **fall at small $N_I$**, most steeply of the three: the per-event shunt inflates as $1/N_I$, so a lone I cell over-pins each E cell.

The curves saturate near the canonical 25% endpoint where W^IE matches the constant-per-edge value.">
  Rates *drop* at small $N_I$ — opposite of the naïve mean-field prediction. At $N_I = 1$, $r_E$ falls to 7.7 Hz (vs 13.5 Hz under constant per-edge). All three regimes converge at $N_I = 256$.
</Figure>

### Critical balance ($1/\sqrt{N}$)

As predicted, an **intermediate falloff**: the $1/\sqrt N$ inflation of the per-event shunt perturbs the cycle less than $1/N$ but is still enough to lower $r_E$.
