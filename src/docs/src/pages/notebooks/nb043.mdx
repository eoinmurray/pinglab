Closes the forbidding-claim chain for [art010](/articles/art010/) by inverting the regulariser. [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) penalised firing *above* a threshold; this entry penalises firing *below* one. The Lagrangian goes through zero into reward-for-spikes territory. If PING is genuinely forbidden from the high-rate manifold, the optimiser should be unable to push its post-training mean rate up — even when the loss is actively pulling that way. COBA without the loop should track the target in both directions. Scaffolded by [art009](/articles/art009/) §Leg 1 item 3.

## Method

Symmetric extension of the [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) recipe via a new pair of CLI flags on the trainer:

$$
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{CE} + s_u \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\langle z_i\rangle - \theta_u)^2 + s_l \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\theta_l - \langle z_i\rangle)^2
$$

where $\langle z_i\rangle$ is the per-neuron mean spike count per trial. The upper-bound term (existing, $s_u = 10^{-3}$) penalises firing above $\theta_u$ — used in [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) to squeeze COBA down. The lower-bound term (new this entry, $s_l = 10^{-3}$) penalises firing *below* $\theta_l$ — pushes rate *up*.

Train one PING and one COBA-no-loop network per signed target $T \in \{+1, +2, +5, 0, -4, -10, -20\}$ spikes/trial ($\times 5$ Hz at $T = 200$ ms). Positive $T$ uses $\theta_u = T$ (upper-bound active); negative $T$ uses $\theta_l = |T|$ (lower-bound active); zero is no regulariser. All other hyperparameters are the [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) baseline recipe at medium tier (2000 train samples, 100 epochs, seed 42), dispatched to Modal A100.

The forbidding signature would be PING flatlining across the full sweep — its post-training rate staying inside a narrow band regardless of which Lagrangian direction is pulled. COBA should follow the target.

COBA spans 1.4–144.6 Hz across the axis (102× range). PING spans 4.7–96.4 Hz (20× range). The signature is *not* "PING is flat everywhere" — under heavy reward PING *does* climb to ≈ 96 Hz, the same rate COBA sits at without any regulariser. But PING pays for it: accuracy collapses to 66% at the same point COBA holds at 89%. The strict-forbidding reading from the prior small-tier writeup was an undertrained-network artefact; the *coupled* forbidding signature is what survives.

The signature lives in the **coupled rate-accuracy trade-off**, which is asymmetric between architectures:

- **PING under reward.** Rate climbs (9.9 → 96.4 Hz, a 10× walk) but accuracy collapses 88% → 66%. The optimiser is paying classification accuracy for every Hz of rate gain.
- **COBA under reward.** Rate climbs (96 → 145 Hz, a 1.5× walk) with accuracy *preserved* at 89%. COBA is comfortable as a high-rate classifier and the reward objective is non-disruptive.
- **COBA under penalty.** Rate drops (96 → 1.4 Hz) and accuracy collapses 90% → 67%. COBA cannot do MNIST at single-digit Hz — it needs the high rate to drive its readout.
- **PING under penalty.** Rate moves modestly (10 → 4.7 Hz) — PING is already firing near the ceiling the penalty asks for, so the penalty engages only at the tightest targets. Accuracy is preserved at 87-88%.

The signature is bidirectional: each architecture has a **natural operating point** where it classifies well, set by the recurrent dynamics, and the optimiser can push it away from that point only at the cost of accuracy. COBA's natural point is ≈ 96 Hz; PING's is ≈ 10 Hz. The cleanest matched-rate comparison is at the rightmost target: at 96 Hz, COBA classifies at 90% while reward-driven PING manages 66% — a 24 pp accuracy gap at the same firing rate.

## Discussion

This is the directional complement of [nb042](/notebooks/nb042/). nb042 showed that destroying the *gamma rhythm* releases the E rate even when mean I is held; this entry shows that *applying gradient pressure in the direction of more spikes* moves PING's rate but with a steep accuracy cost. Two different levers, two complementary observations about how the rhythm constrains the classifier.

The earlier strict forbidding claim — that the rate "cannot be moved" — was too strong. The right claim is that **each architecture has a natural operating point at which classification is preserved, and the inhibitory-loop architecture moves PING's natural point down by an order of magnitude**. The optimiser can deviate from that point if pushed, but pays accuracy proportional to the deviation. This is a softer claim than "rate is locked" but more accurate, and arguably more interesting: it's a statement about the geometry of the trained-network manifold, not just about the rate scalar.

## Implementation note

This entry required the lower-bound regulariser, which was *referenced* in the CLI docstring but never implemented. Added `--fr-reg-lower-theta` and `--fr-reg-lower-strength` to `oscilloscope train` as the symmetric counterpart of the existing `--fr-reg-upper-*` pair (same pooling rules, same per-neuron / population modes). The penalty is $s_l \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\theta_l - \langle z_i\rangle)^2$ — zero when every neuron fires at or above $\theta_l$, quadratic in the deficit otherwise.

Two convergence notes that bear on the headline:

1. **PING under heaviest reward (−100 Hz target) reaches ≈ 96 Hz at epoch 100.** Rate still drifts at the end of training; whether 96 Hz is the asymptote or a snapshot is open. The accuracy floor of 66% at this point — vs 89% for COBA at the same rate — is the load-bearing observation either way.
2. **COBA under heavy reward.** Heavy-reward COBA reaches ≈ 145 Hz at the −100 Hz target by epoch 100 with accuracy preserved at 89%. No overshoot-and-decline behaviour at 100 epochs.

The architectural-asymmetry claim is robust to these caveats — both directions of the trade-off persist, and the matched-rate accuracy gap at 96 Hz (90% COBA vs 66% reward-driven PING) is the cleanest single number.
