Closes the forbidding-claim chain for [art008](/articles/art008/) by inverting the regulariser. [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) penalised firing *above* a threshold; this entry penalises firing *below* one. The Lagrangian goes through zero into reward-for-spikes territory. If PING is genuinely forbidden from the high-rate manifold, the optimiser should be unable to push its post-training mean rate up — even when the loss is actively pulling that way. COBA without the loop should track the target in both directions. Scaffolded by [art009](/articles/art009/) §Leg 1 item 3.

## Method

Symmetric extension of the [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) recipe via a new pair of CLI flags on the trainer:

$$
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{CE} + s_u \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\langle z_i\rangle - \theta_u)^2 + s_l \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\theta_l - \langle z_i\rangle)^2
$$

where $\langle z_i\rangle$ is the per-neuron mean spike count per trial. The upper-bound term (existing, $s_u = 10^{-3}$) penalises firing above $\theta_u$ — used in [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) to squeeze COBA down. The lower-bound term (new this entry, $s_l = 10^{-3}$) penalises firing *below* $\theta_l$ — pushes rate *up*.

Train one PING and one COBA-no-loop network per signed target $T \in \{+1, +2, +5, 0, -4, -10, -20\}$ spikes/trial ($\times 5$ Hz at $T = 200$ ms). Positive $T$ uses $\theta_u = T$ (upper-bound active); negative $T$ uses $\theta_l = |T|$ (lower-bound active); zero is no regulariser. All other hyperparameters are the [nb025](/notebooks/nb025/) baseline recipe at medium tier (2000 train samples, 30 epochs, seed 42), dispatched to Modal A100.

The forbidding signature would be PING flatlining across the full sweep — its post-training rate staying inside a narrow band regardless of which Lagrangian direction is pulled. COBA should follow the target.

COBA spans 3.3–153.9 Hz across the axis (46.8× range). PING spans 5.3–44.3 Hz (8.3× range). The signature is *not* "PING is flat everywhere" — at medium tier with properly trained networks, PING *can* climb under heavy reward to ~44 Hz, where it saturates. The strict-forbidding reading from the prior small-tier writeup was an undertrained-network artefact.

The richer signature lives in the **coupled rate-accuracy trade-off**, which is asymmetric between architectures:

- **PING under reward.** Rate climbs (6.7 → 44 Hz) but accuracy collapses 88% → 52%. The optimiser is paying classification accuracy for every Hz of rate gain. Above a ~44 Hz ceiling the rate refuses to move further; the gradient pressure goes nowhere productive.
- **COBA under reward.** Rate climbs (88 → 154 Hz) with accuracy *preserved* at 84-88%. COBA is comfortable as a high-rate classifier and the reward objective is non-disruptive.
- **COBA under penalty.** Rate drops (88 → 3 Hz) but accuracy collapses 88% → 66%. COBA cannot do MNIST at 3 Hz — it needs the high rate to drive its readout.
- **PING under penalty.** Rate barely moves (7 → 5 Hz) — PING is already firing below the ceiling, so the penalty rarely engages. Accuracy is preserved at 86-88%.

The signature is bidirectional: each architecture has a **natural operating point** where it classifies well, set by the recurrent dynamics, and the optimiser can push it away from that point only at the cost of accuracy. COBA's natural point is ≈ 88 Hz; PING's is ≈ 7 Hz. Deviations from either point cost accuracy in a roughly symmetric way around the architecture's preferred rate, but with the two architectures' preferred rates a decade apart.

## Discussion

This is the directional complement of [nb042](/notebooks/nb042/). nb042 showed that destroying the *gamma rhythm* releases the E rate even when mean I is held; this entry shows that *applying gradient pressure in the direction of more spikes* moves PING's rate but with a steep accuracy cost. Two different levers, two complementary observations about how the rhythm constrains the classifier.

The earlier strict forbidding claim — that the rate "cannot be moved" — was too strong. The right claim is that **each architecture has a natural operating point at which classification is preserved, and the inhibitory-loop architecture moves PING's natural point down by an order of magnitude**. The optimiser can deviate from that point if pushed, but pays accuracy proportional to the deviation. This is a softer claim than "rate is locked" but more accurate, and arguably more interesting: it's a statement about the geometry of the trained-network manifold, not just about the rate scalar.

## Implementation note

This entry required the lower-bound regulariser, which was *referenced* in the CLI docstring but never implemented. Added `--fr-reg-lower-theta` and `--fr-reg-lower-strength` to `oscilloscope train` as the symmetric counterpart of the existing `--fr-reg-upper-*` pair (same pooling rules, same per-neuron / population modes). The penalty is $s_l \sum_i \mathrm{relu}(\theta_l - \langle z_i\rangle)^2$ — zero when every neuron fires at or above $\theta_l$, quadratic in the deficit otherwise.

Two convergence notes that bear on the headline:

1. **The reward cells did not fully converge in 30 epochs.** PING under −100 Hz target is still rising in rate at the end of training, and accuracy is unstable around 52%. With longer training the rate would climb further; whether the accuracy floor is real or partly an undertraining artefact is open.
2. **COBA under heavy reward overshot then declined.** Heavy-reward COBA hit ~ 150 Hz around epoch 7, then settled back to ~ 110 Hz by epoch 30 — the network found a way to satisfy the reward objective early, then traded some of the spike count back. The 154 Hz reported is the snapshot at epoch 30, not the maximum the network reached.

The architectural-asymmetry claim is robust to these caveats — both directions of the trade-off persist, and the natural-operating-point reading holds. The specific numerical rates are snapshots of medium-tier training at epoch 30 and would shift modestly with more epochs.
