## PING training

PING has been modelled but not trained on a task. We train it here on MNIST classification.

The network is a conductance-based E/I model with a fixed E→I→E loop. Feedforward and E→E weights are learned; the inhibitory loop is held at its initialised strength. Training uses surrogate-gradient backprop with a cumulative-membrane readout (Figure 1).

  Per-epoch train loss and test accuracy for PING on MNIST. From [012 — PING](/notebooks/nb012/), Figure 1.
</Figure>

Figures 2 and 3 record whether the gamma rhythm survives training. Mean excitatory rate settles in the $\sim$20–40 Hz band, and the hidden layer organises into E-then-I cycles by the final epoch.

## dt stability vs other models

Six models are trained on MNIST at two *dt* regimes (0.1 ms and 1 ms), each with the per-model recipe from its calibration sibling ([nb007](/notebooks/nb007/)–[nb012](/notebooks/nb012/)). Weights are then frozen and accuracy is re-evaluated across a sweep of eval-*dt* values under three input-transport rules ([upsample / downsample / resample](#eval-time-dt-transport)). Identity transport (eval-*dt* = train-*dt*) anchors each sweep, so any change downstream is *integrator-side*.

**Models** (see [the ladder](/docs/models/)):

- [*standard-snn*](/docs/models/#standard-snn) — pinglab reimplementation of snnTorch's *snn.Leaky*. The Tutorial-3 simplification ($\Delta t = 1$, $R = 1$, $(1-\beta)$ absorbed into $W$) drops $\Delta t$ from the update, which is what produces the *dt*-leakage analysed below.
- [*standard-snn-exp*](/docs/models/#standard-snn) — *standard-snn* with an exponential synapse ($\tau_\text{syn} \approx 2$ ms) on the feedforward inputs.
- [*snntorch-library*](/docs/models/#snntorch-library) — snnTorch's *snn.Leaky* directly. Parity probe against *standard-snn*.
- [*cuba*](/docs/models/#cuba) — same LIF, exp-Euler + ZOH integration restoring the $(1-\beta)/dt$ and $(1-\beta)$ prefactors. *dt* is back in the update.
- [*coba*](/docs/models/#coba) — conductance-based LIF with exponential synapses, hard reset, refractory. COBANet with *--ei-strength 0* (feedforward-only).
- [*ping*](/docs/models/#ping) — COBA plus the frozen E→I→E recurrent loop that produces gamma.


All six models reach comparable accuracy at their training dt (Figure 4); differences appear once dt is perturbed at inference.

## COBA to PING weight transfer

PING and COBA share the same units and feedforward path; they differ only in whether the E→I→E loop is active. Does the loop need to be active during training, or can it be switched on at inference? We sweep *ei_strength* at test time on frozen COBA weights.

<Figure id="Figure 8" title="Transfer accuracy vs I-loop strength" src="/figures/notebooks/nb019/acc_sweep.png" alt="Line plot: test accuracy versus inference ei_strength, on coba-trained weights.">
  Test accuracy vs inference-time *ei_strength* on COBA-trained weights. Dotted lines: COBA baseline and 10% chance. From [019 — COBA-to-PING Weight Transfer](/notebooks/nb019/), Figure 1.
</Figure>

Figure 9 reports population firing rates across the sweep: E rate falls as the loop is dialled up, I rate rises from zero.

<Figure id="Figure 9" title="E and I firing rates vs I-loop strength" src="/figures/notebooks/nb019/rates_sweep.png" alt="Line plot: mean excitatory and inhibitory population firing rates versus inference ei_strength.">
  Mean E (hidden) and I population firing rates per test pass vs *ei_strength*. From [019 — COBA-to-PING Weight Transfer](/notebooks/nb019/), Figure 2.
</Figure>

Figure 10 shows single-trial rasters at six *ei* values. At ei = 0 the network is asynchronous; by ei ≈ 0.4 the same weights produce gamma-band E-then-I cycles.

## Spike Penalty

Unpenalised, the three models settle at different rates (Figure 12). Adding an upper-bound regulariser $\lambda_u \cdot \sum \text{ReLU}(\bar s - \theta_u)^2$ — with strength $\lambda_u = 10^{-3}$ held fixed — and sweeping the budget $\theta_u$ (mean spikes per trial per unit) traces each model's accuracy–rate frontier.

<Figure id="Figure 13" title="Accuracy / rate frontier" src="/figures/notebooks/nb020/frontier.png" alt="Pareto frontier: best test accuracy versus achieved hidden E rate, one line per model. Each point labelled with its θ_u value.">
  Test accuracy vs achieved hidden-E firing rate, one curve per model; each point is one *(model, $\theta_u$)* configuration. From [020 — Spike-Penalty Sweep Across the Ladder](/notebooks/nb020/), Figure 1.
</Figure>

PING's frontier is flat: its unpenalised rate is already biological, and accuracy is insensitive to $\theta_u$. CUBA loses accuracy when forced down toward PING's rate.
