<VeslxGallery
  single
  path="_artifacts/sweeps"
  globs={["experiment_5_crosscorr_*.png"]}
  title="E–I cross-correlogram in oscillatory regime"
  subtitle="$I_E = 1.5$ nA, $g_{ei} = 1.4$ nS"
  caption="Characteristic E→I→E timing loop."
  captionLabel="Figure 5"
>
We compute cross-correlograms between excitatory and inhibitory populations in
the oscillatory regime.

**Readout:** E–I cross-correlation  
**Interpretation:** Inhibitory spikes reliably lag excitatory spikes by a few
milliseconds, consistent with the PING mechanism.

**How the plot is produced (math):** Let $\{t^E_m\}$ and $\{t^I_n\}$ be excitatory
and inhibitory spike times. For a lag $\tau$, we count E–I pairs whose time
difference falls in a bin around $\tau$:

$$
H(\tau) = \sum_{m,n} \mathbf{1}\!\left[t^I_n - t^E_m \in \left(\tau-\tfrac{\Delta}{2},\,\tau+\tfrac{\Delta}{2}\right)\right],
$$

where $\Delta$ is the bin width. The plotted curve is the normalized
cross-correlogram $H(\tau)/\max_\tau H(\tau)$, with $\tau>0$ indicating I spikes
lagging E spikes.
</VeslxGallery>

<VeslxGallery
  title="Phase-dependent precision (GPP-1)"
  single
  path="_artifacts/phase-dependence"
  globs={['phase_precision_gpp1*']}
  caption="Phase-resolved gain, Fano factor, and latency jitter. Oscillatory phase modulates both response strength and precision."
>

We ask whether oscillatory phase also affects **response reliability and
temporal precision** under noise.

For each of the 100 pulse phases, we run 30 trials with independent noise
realizations. For each phase we compute:

- **Gain:** Δ-spikes rate (spikes/ms)
- **Reliability:** Fano factor of spike counts across trials
- **Temporal precision:** standard deviation of first-spike latencies

- Gain reproduces the phase dependence observed in the single-trial sweep.
- Reliability and latency precision vary with phase, indicating that oscillatory
  timing modulates not only response magnitude but also trial-to-trial
  variability.
- Periods of low inhibition tend to support more temporally precise responses,
  though the relationship is not strictly monotonic.
</VeslxGallery>
