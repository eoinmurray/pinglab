<FrontMatter />

The goal is to show, quantitatively, **when PING/gamma helps** communication and
routing **under matched mean rate and matched energy proxy**, and **when it
hurts** (over-coherence / interference). Treat PING as a **control primitive**,
not a representational theory.

The code rule is every comparison that isn't explicitly about rate must be **rate-matched**
(mean_rate_E/I) and ideally **energy-matched** (conductance integral + spikes).
Otherwise you're just measuring "more activity".

### 2) Single-network task: "image in → decode out"

**Purpose:** First real task beyond regime diagnostics. Keep it simple and
debuggable.

**Critical design decision (pick one first)**

* **Rate code**: pixel intensity → Poisson rate for a fixed window (simplest)
* or **Latency code**: brighter pixel → earlier spikes (more spiking-native)

**TODO**

* Choose small images (start **synthetic**: bars/blobs/checkerboards; later
  MNIST)
* Define mapping: image pixels → subset of E neurons (topographic or random
  projection)
* Stimulus protocol: present image for $T_{\text{img}}$ ms with baseline noise;
  include blank trials
* Readout: linear ridge regression on binned rates or filtered spikes to
  reconstruct pixels
* Metrics:

  * fidelity: MSE + (PSNR or SSIM)
  * correlation(image, recon)
  * cost: spikes per fidelity + energy proxy
* Controls:

  * shuffled mapping should collapse reconstruction
  * **rate-matched AI vs PING** comparison

**Exit criteria**

* Reliable above-chance reconstruction with stable metrics across seeds.

### 3) Two-network transfer: image A → B with regime + phase sweeps

**Purpose:** The headline “communication-through-coherence” test: does relative
phase change effective transfer without big rate changes?

**Minimal architecture**

* Network A encodes image → produces spikes
* Projection A→B converts A spikes into synaptic input to B (with controllable
  delay)
* Network B decodes/reconstructs the image (or classifies it)

**TODO**

* Define coupling interface: how A spikes become conductance in B (with delay)
* Phase control knobs (use at least one “clean” knob):

  * **delay sweep** (best)
  * tonic drive offset (phase drift/frequency mismatch)
  * optional weak forcing to phase-lock (later)
* Regime conditions to compare:

  * AI/AI
  * PING/PING in-phase
  * PING/PING out-of-phase
  * PING/AI (both directions)
* Enforce **rate + energy matching** across conditions
* Transfer metrics:

  * B reconstruction fidelity (MSE/SSIM etc.)
  * coherence/phase-locking between A and B (prove in/out-of-phase)
  * optional: transfer entropy / mutual information later

**Exit criteria**

* A clear curve: fidelity in B vs phase offset (or delay), with a peak when
  inputs arrive in excitable windows, holding under rate matching.
