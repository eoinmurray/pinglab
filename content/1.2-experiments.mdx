**Goal:** Show quantitatively when PING/gamma is useful.

**Rule:** Every comparison must be **rate-matched** (`mean_rate_E/I`) and ideally
**energy-matched** (conductance integral + spikes).

## Planned

### 1. Single-network image decode

Image pixels → E neuron subset → linear readout to reconstruct. Start with
synthetic patterns (bars/blobs), then MNIST. Metrics: MSE, correlation,
spikes/fidelity. Controls: shuffled mapping, rate-matched AI vs PING.
Above-chance reconstruction, stable across seeds.

### 2. Two-network transfer

Network A encodes image, projects to B, B decodes. Sweep coupling delay to
control relative phase. Compare AI/AI, PING/PING in-phase, PING/PING
out-of-phase, PING/AI. Enforce rate + energy matching. Fidelity vs phase curve
with peak at excitable window, holding under rate matching.
