## Overview

This experiment asks whether a single PING network can carry a static spatial
pattern through its dynamics in a way that supports a simple readout. The
narrative is: define a controlled image stimulus, map pixels to neuron groups,
compare PING and AI regimes under rate and energy matching, then evaluate
reconstruction quality and cost.

## Experiment Design

### Question

Can coherent E–I dynamics preserve a spatial pattern well enough that a simple
readout reconstructs the image, once mean rates and energy are controlled?

### Stimulus

We start with synthetic images before moving to larger datasets. The initial
library includes blobs, bars, checkerboard-like patterns, and 2–4 blobs at
random locations. Resolution is small (e.g. 16×16, 256 pixels) to keep the
mapping transparent.

The image is presented as a step input: after a warmup, we apply a constant
additional current for a window $T_{img}$ (e.g. 300–800 ms). Readout integrates
spikes during this window.

### Encoding (pixels to neurons)

We assign each pixel to a fixed group of E neurons:

- Partition E neurons into $P = H\times W$ groups (one group per pixel).
- Pixel intensity $x_p \in [0,1]$ scales the additional current to that group.
- Each group has a fixed size $k$ (e.g. 5–20 neurons).

This one-to-one mapping keeps reconstruction rules simple and makes failures
interpretable.

### Regimes and matching

We compare two regimes with the same stimulus:

- **PING regime:** strong gamma, coherent E–I loop.
- **AI regime:** asynchronous irregular, weak gamma.

Matching constraints are non-negotiable:

- Match **mean_rate_E** and **mean_rate_I** during the image window.
- Preferably match an **energy proxy** (conductance integral + spikes) so that
  decoding is not just a proxy for higher activity.

Practically, we tune baseline drive and noise within each regime and reject runs
that miss the matching tolerance.

### Readout and reconstruction

We read out spike counts per pixel group over the image window:

- $r_p = \mathrm{spike\_count}(\mathrm{group}\ p,\ \mathrm{window}) / \mathrm{window\_duration}$

The default reconstruction is an untrained linear mapping:

- $\hat{x} = \mathrm{normalize}(r)$ to rescale into [0,1]

A trained linear readout (ridge regression) is optional later, but only after
establishing the untrained baseline.

### Metrics

Primary fidelity measures:

- **MSE:** $\|x - \hat{x}\|^2 / P$
- **Pearson correlation** between flattened images
- **SSIM** (optional, for structured patterns)

Cost-aware measures:

- Total spikes vs reconstruction quality
- MSE or correlation per spike

### Controls

To validate that reconstruction reflects the mapping, not incidental activity:

1. **Shuffled mapping control:** permute pixel-to-group mapping at readout.
2. **Uniform image control:** all pixels equal intensity.
3. **No-image baseline:** no additional current during the image window.
4. **Rate-matched AI vs PING:** the central comparison under constraints.

### Expected outcomes

If PING helps, we expect higher correlation or lower MSE at matched rates, plus
lower trial-to-trial variance in reconstruction. If PING hurts, over-coherence
should blur spatial selectivity and reduce fidelity even as global oscillation
strength increases.
