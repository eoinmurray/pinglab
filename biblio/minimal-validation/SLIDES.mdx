---

## Overview

Validation results for a minimal PING spiking neural network model

- AI-OSC transitions via two control parameters
- F-I curves characterizing population responses
- Spectral analysis of oscillatory activity
- Temporal relationships between E and I populations
- Firing regularity metrics

---

## AI-OSC Transition via E-I Coupling

Raster plots demonstrating the AI-to-OSC transition as $g_{ei}$ increases.

**Setup:** Fixed $I_E = 1.2$, sweep $g_{ei}$ from 1.3 to 1.8 nS

**PING mechanism:**
- E fires → recruits I → I inhibits E → E recovers → cycle repeats

## AI-OSC Transition via E-I Coupling: Interpretation

**Low $g_{ei}$:** E neurons cannot effectively recruit I neurons → asynchronous firing

**High $g_{ei}$:** Strong E→I coupling engages inhibitory population → synchronized feedback inhibition

- Transition creates characteristic PING rhythm
- Gamma oscillations emerge progressively

## AI-OSC Transition via External Drive: Interpretation

**Low $I_E$:** Asynchronous irregular firing, spikes scattered randomly

**High $I_E$:** Synchronized oscillatory activity at gamma frequencies (~30–80 Hz)

- E population fires first in each cycle
- Recruits I neurons which then inhibit E
- Creates characteristic PING rhythm

## Power Spectral Density

PSD of E population firing rate quantifies frequency content of network activity.

**Setup:** Sweep $I_E$ from 0.5 to 2.0, fixed $g_{ei} = 1.4$

**Method:**
- 1 ms bins for instantaneous population rate
- Gaussian smoothing ($\sigma$ = 2 ms)
- Welch's method, 5–150 Hz range

## Cross-Correlogram

Reveals temporal relationship between E and I populations in PING networks.

**Setup:** $g_{ei} = 1.4$, $I_E = 1.5$ (clear oscillation regime)

**Method:**
- Find all I spikes within ±50 ms of each E spike
- Histogram time differences (I − E)
- Normalize to peak value

## Cross-Correlogram: Interpretation

**Expected:** Peak at positive lag (I lags E by ~2–5 ms)

- Reflects PING mechanism: E fires → synaptic delay → I responds
- Peak width reflects timing jitter
- Secondary structure at oscillation period (~15–25 ms for gamma)

## Population ISI CV

Coefficient of variation of inter-spike intervals measures firing regularity.

| CV Value | Interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| CV = 1 | Poisson process |
| CV < 1 | Regular (periodic) firing |
| CV > 1 | Bursty firing |

**Setup:** Sweep $I_E$ from 0.5 to 2.0 (40 values), fixed $g_{ei} = 1.4$

## Population ISI CV: Interpretation

**Low $I_E$:** Neurons fire irregularly, CV approaches 1

**High $I_E$:** Neurons entrain to rhythm, CV drops below 1

- Network shows some synchrony (CV < 1) even at weak drive
- Indicates oscillatory tendency is intrinsic to network structure

## Summary

| Metric | AI State | OSC State |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Raster | Scattered spikes | Rhythmic volleys |
| PSD | Flat spectrum | Gamma peak |
| ISI CV | ~1 (Poisson) | {"<1"} (regular) |
| Cross-corr | No structure | E leads I by 2–5 ms |

---

## Conclusions

1. PING network transitions from AI to OSC with increasing $g_{ei}$ or $I_E$
2. E→I coupling is essential for recruiting inhibitory feedback
3. Gamma oscillations (~40–60 Hz) emerge in oscillatory regime
4. E population leads I population by characteristic synaptic delay
5. Network shows oscillatory tendency even at weak drive (CV < 1)
